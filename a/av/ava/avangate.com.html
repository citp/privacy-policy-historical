Definitions

  • "personal information" is defined as any information that identifies or can be used to identify, contact, or locate the person to whom such information pertains);
  • „personal information processing” is defined as any operation or set of operations that is performed against personal information, using automated or manual methods, such as: collecting, registering, organizing, storing, changing or alteration, quering, drilling/mining, using, transmitting – directly or through disemination, combining, blocking, deletion or destruction;
  • „storage” is defined as the operation of keeping on any type of support of the personal information;
  • „personal information management system” is defined as any organized structure/system used to manage personal information;
  • „operator” is defined as any private or corporate legal entity , including public authorithies, that defines the purpose and the objectives of the personal information processing;
  • „anonymous data” is defined as the data that cannot be linked and/or associated with an identified or identifiable person;
  • „business contact data” is defined as the data that includes name, position, business address, phones, faxes, e-mail address of an employee of an organization, having this quality. Business contact data do not constitute personal information;
  • „statistical data” is defined as the data resulting from the processing of personal information using statistical methods and technologies, data that cannot be used in identifying a person and that it is used only for statistical, communication or promotion purposes.

Use of cookies

When you visit this Site, you can browse the Site and access important information without revealing your identity. In order to improve our Site, we use “cookies” to track your visit. A cookie is small amount of data that is transferred to your browser by a Web server and can only be read by the server that gave it to you. It functions as your identification card and enables Avangate to record your passwords, purchases, and preferences. It cannot be executed as code or deliver viruses.

Most browsers are initially set to accept cookies. You can set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the chance to decide whether or not to accept it. (For some Web pages that require an authorization, cookies are not optional. Users choosing not to accept cookies will probably not be able to access those pages.)

While Avangate uses cookies to track your visit to the Sites, and our Web servers automatically log the IP/Internet address of your computer, Avangate does not generally use this information to identify you personally. We associate this data to understand how our Site and services are being used so we can understand our customers and offer appropriate products and services.
