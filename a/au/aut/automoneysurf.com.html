What are cookies are and how they are used by AutomoneySurf?



A cookie is a small amount of data, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier, which is sent to your browser from a web site"s computers and stored on your computer"s hard drive. As a standard practice, AutomoneySurf assigns a cookie with an anonymous identifier to each end user.



AutomoneySurf uses its own cookies for a number of purposes, including to:



Access your information when you "sign in," so that we can provide you with customized content, such as your profile and account options.

Keep track of preferences you specify while you are using AutomoneySurf.

Display relevant advertising banners based on your interests and activity on AutomoneySurf.

Estimate and report our total audience size and traffic.

Conduct research to improve AutomoneySurf"s content and services.

Require you to re-enter your AutomoneySurf password after a certain period of time has elapsed to protect you against others accidentally accessing your account contents.



AutomoneySurf does not require users to accept cookies in order to enter or view the site or register. However, if you reject cookies, you will not be able to participate in using the AutomoneySurf members area, register, or to access other features. Please note that some areas or features of AutomoneySurf will be significantly slower, or may not function at all, if cookies are disabled.
