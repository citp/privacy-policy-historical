This Privacy Policy applies only to information you provide to us online or that we collect from you while visiting or using our Website. It does not apply to information we have obtained offline or through other means.

3.0 What Information We Collect and Use.

LegitScript collects (1) personally identifiable information you knowingly provide to us, and (2) general information concerning users gathered at different points on our Website that does not include personally identifiable information:

3.1. Personally Identifiable Information You Provide to Us: In order to subscribe to certain LegitScript products and services offered on this Website, we require that you complete a registration form providing certain contact information, such as name, address, email address, telephone number, company name, and other basic contact information.

3.2. Non-Personally Identifiable Information We Collect: LegitScript may use “cookies” to collect information about how our Website is used. The types of information collected may include: your IP address; a unique identifier assigned to your workstation; the page from which the cookie was initially installed; the time and date of initial visit; the time and date of subsequent visits; and the cookie expiration date.

A cookie is a small amount of data, which often includes an anonymous unique identifier, which is sent to your browser from a website’s computers and stored on your computer’s hard drive. LegitScript may also use cookies to recognize you when you return to our Website.

LegitScript uses the information collected via this Website to communicate with you and to provide you with products, services or information that you have requested. In general, except as provided in this policy, we will not disclose your personally identifiable information to unaffiliated third parties without your express consent. There are, however, some limited circumstances in which we may need to disclose personally identifiable information about a user, including, but not limited to, the circumstances set forth below:

4.1.
