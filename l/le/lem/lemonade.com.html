Use of Cookies and Tracking User Traffic

Some pages on the Services may use “cookies”—small files that the Services place on your hard drive for identification purposes. A cookie file can contain information such as a user ID to track the pages visited, but the only PII a cookie can contain is information you supply yourself. These files are used for Services registration and customization the next time you visit us.
Some parts of the Services may also use cookies to track user traffic patterns. Lemonade does this in order to determine the usefulness of our Services information to our users and to see how effective our navigational structure is in helping users reach that information. Please note that cookies cannot read data off of your hard drive. Your Web browser may allow you to be notified when you are receiving a cookie, giving you the choice to accept it or not. If you prefer not to receive cookies while browsing our Services, you can set your browser to warn you before accepting cookies and refuse the cookie when your browser alerts you to its presence. You can also refuse all cookies by turning them off in your browser, by not accepting cookies, some pages may not fully function and you may not be able to access certain information on the Services.

Web Beacons

We also use Web beacons, sometimes called single-pixel GIFs, that momentarily direct your browser to a server other than Lemonade’s in a way that may not be apparent to you. Web beacons are used to count users who visited specific pages and determine which emails were opened and/or acted upon when we send HTML-formatted email messages. We do not link any PII to Web beacons.
