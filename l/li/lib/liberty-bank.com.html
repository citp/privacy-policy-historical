Privacy Notice

Your Privacy: Our Priority

At Liberty Bank, your privacy is a top priority.

As our customer, you necessarily provide us with certain personal information about yourself. We take very seriously our obligation to safeguard the privacy of that information.

The personal data you give us are used to administer your accounts in a professional and efficient manner, to provide you with excellent service, and to develop and offer financial services designed to meet your needs. We do not sell your personal information to anyone, for any purpose.

On this page, you will find details about our policy on privacy and about how personal information about you is collected and used. If you have any questions, please contact our Customer Service Center at (888) 570-0773.

Liberty Bank has been privileged to serve our customers for 176 years because we have earned your trust. We are committed to maintaining that trust by ensuring the confidentiality of your personal information.

The Liberty Bank Privacy Policy

The privacy policies described on this page apply to both Liberty Bank and its affiliates, Liberty Bank Mortgage Corporation and Community Realty Investors, Inc. Throughout this policy, the phrase “personal information” refers to nonpublic personal information that identifies you.

We do not sell your personal information to anyone.

What do we mean when we say “personal information”?

In the process of providing you with financial products and services, we collect certain personal information about you, including:

Information that you provide to us on applications and other forms (including those on our web site), such as your name, address, date of birth, telephone and Social Security numbers, assets, debts, and income.
Information from credit reporting agencies regarding your credit history or creditworthiness.
Information about you that we acquire from other third parties to assist us in evaluating your application for Liberty Bank products and services; for example, employment status that we obtain to determine your eligibility for a loan.
Information about your transactions with Liberty Bank and our affiliates; for example, your account balances, payment history, and transaction details.

We do not share your personal information with third parties not affiliated with Liberty except under the following circumstances:

We disclose personal information as necessary to facilitate transactions; for example, to collect a check.
We share personal information with companies that perform services for us; for example, check printing, data processing, and mailing.
To keep you informed about products and services, we may also, as legally permitted, disclose personal information to companies that assist us in marketing, including other financial institutions with whom we have agreements.
We also disclose personal information as required or permitted by law; for example, in response to a subpoena or garnishment, or to a credit bureau.

Except in the circumstances above, we do not share your personal information with third parties not affiliated with Liberty unless you expressly permit us to do so. Transaction and experience information, such as your account balance, payment history, and transaction details, may be shared between us and our affiliates, as allowed by the Fair Credit Reporting Act.

Your personal information is kept confidential.

We have developed procedural, electronic, and physical safeguards to protect your privacy in accordance with federal standards. We have designed procedures to restrict access to your personal information to those employees who need to know it in order to provide products or services to you, and they are required to maintain the confidentiality of this information.

The protections of our privacy policy are extended to former customers as well as current ones.

We’ll keep you informed of changes to our privacy policy.

If our policy changes in the future, the new policy will be posted on our web site, and you will be notified in writing of the changes.

Your Privacy Online - for Internet Users

Our privacy policy also safeguards your online interactions with Liberty Bank.

The information age has made online banking easy and convenient. You can visit our web site with confidence, knowing that we are as committed to your privacy and security online as we are in more traditional banking settings.

When you browse our web site’s informational pages, we do not collect any information that identifies you personally.
If you access your Liberty accounts online, you’ll need to supply us with certain information so we can verify your identity.
When you bank online with us, we place “cookies” on your computer. These are files that enable our web site to recognize return visitors. We do not store any of your personal information in our cookies.

LibertyLink Internet Banking Security

Protecting the confidentiality and security of your personal and account information is top priority at Liberty. Months of research and state-of-the-art technology have gone into ensuring that your LibertyLink transactions are secure. But your cooperation is vital to maximize the effectiveness of our security measures. Here are some steps you should take to protect the privacy of information you send and receive when you use LibertyLink:

You must use an Internet browser that supports Secure Socket Layers (SSL). SSL encrypts your data to prevent unauthorized persons from gaining access to it. If you are having difficulty signing on to LibertyLink, you may need to upgrade your browser to one that supports SSL, such as Microsoft Internet Explorer (any version) or Netscape Navigator (version 1.1 or later).
Do not share your Personal Identification Number (PIN) with anyone you do not want to have access to your accounts. Anyone who has your PIN has the same access to your accounts that you have. If you suspect that an unauthorized person has obtained your PIN, please contact us immediately at (800) 622-6731.

In general, be careful of any Internet site with which you are not familiar. Avoid sending information like your Social Security number, credit card numbers, or bank account information unless you are in a secure site like LibertyLink. When visiting new sites, be particularly aware of small windows that may randomly appear on the screen. If you are not comfortable with a newly-created window, close it immediately. The safest next step would be to exit and restart your browser.

Contacting Liberty Via E-mail

Liberty Bank offers several ways of contacting the bank using electronic mail.

Contact Us. Anyone who visits our site can use a link called “Contact Us”, which appears on every page. “Contact Us” is a convenient method of exchanging non-confidential information with the bank. It is not a secure connection and should not be used to convey any account information.
E-mail links. The same cautions that apply to “Contact Us” apply to the e-mail links to bank employees and departments that appear in various locations on our site. Use them for non-confidential information only.
Online sign-up forms are available to allow you to apply for a new account or for LibertyLink Internet Banking. You can be assured of the confidentiality of the information you provide on these sign-up forms, because they communicate via a secure connection.
Bank Mail. LibertyLink customers have an additional way to contact the bank electronically: a secure messaging service called Bank Mail. You can only access Bank Mail after signing on to LibertyLink, ensuring that you are communicating in a secure environment.

Here are some “do’s” and “don’ts” for contacting Liberty Bank electronically:

DO use “Contact Us” to request general information on products, services, branch locations, and hours of operation.
DO use “Contact Us” to offer general comments on our web site.
DO feel free to use individual e-mail links on our site to contact specific bank employees or departments; however, DON’T send financial or personal information via these links; they are not secure.
DON’T use “Contact Us” to apply to open an account or to sign up for LibertyLink. Instead, use the secure forms provided for these purposes.
DON’T use “Contact Us” to send your Social Security number, account number, or other personal information. Sign on to LibertyLink and use Bank Mail when you need to send secure correspondence.

Changes in Our Online Privacy Policy

We are continually reviewing new technological developments in our efforts to provide you with a high level of online security and privacy when you use our web site. If our online privacy protections are revised, we will update this policy to reflect any changes.
