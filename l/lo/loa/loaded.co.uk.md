## a. privacy policy

## a.1. introduction to collecting information

a.1.1. “Loaded” collects Personal Data and Anonymous Data when you visit our sites or when you send us communications.

a.1.2. ‘Personal Data’ means information that allows someone to identify you. This includes your name, address, telephone number and email address. It also includes information about who you are associated with or linked to.

a.1.3. ‘Anonymous Data’ means information that is not associated with or linked to your Personal Data. Anonymous Data does not permit the identification of an individual person or persons. We collect Personal Data and Anonymous Data as described below.

## a.2. accessing your personal data

a.2.1. You are entitled to see the information held about you and may ask us to make any necessary changes to ensure it is accurate and kept up to date. If you wish to see the information we hold about you, please contact us in writing at “Loaded”, 27A Poland Street, London W1F 8QW.

a.2.2. We are entitled by law to charge a fee of £10 to meet costs in providing details of the information we hold about you. We are also legally obliged to request you to provide us with identification so we can be certain you are entitled to receive the requested data.

## a.3. how your data is collected

a.3.1. We collect Personal Data from you such as your name, contact details, country of residence, zip code or post code, gender or birth date when you register on our Service or when you choose to use the various services we provide.

a.3.2. We collect other types of Personal Data you provide to us voluntarily. This can include making a purchase, responding to surveys or entering a competition.

a.3.3. If you provide us feedback regarding our Service via email we will collect your name and email address in order to send you a reply.

a.3.4. In order to improve our Service we may ask you to voluntarily complete confidential surveys. We do not ask you to provide us with any personally identifiable data in association with these surveys, although we do associate your responses with Personal Data. If you do not wish to participate in a survey, simply decline to participate when asked.

a.3.5. If you decide to invite a friend or family member to join our Service, we will collect your name and email address as well as your friend or family member’s email address in order to send them email. We will always ask your permission if we want to mention your details when contacting your friend or family member.

## a.4. how to opt out of receiving promotional material

a.4.1. We offer you choices regarding the collection, use and sharing of your Personal Data.  
When you register on one of our sites or respond to one of our communications or offers we will ask you if you want to receive future communications from us. You may indicate consent to receipt of these communications by entering your personal details and choosing what information you would like to receive, or by not ticking the opt-out box provided.

a.4.2. Should you decide to stop receiving further communications from us you will have the opportunity to opt-out by following the unsubscribe instructions provided in the email you receive.[  
](mailto:editorial@loaded.co.uk)

a.4.3. You shall receive a weekly newsletter, The Loaded Edit, when you register with the Loaded.co.uk Service. You can unsubscribe from this communication by clicking on the ‘Unsubscribe’ option in the newsletter.

a.4.4. If you wish to contact us by email or post to opt out of any of our services, or to amend your details, please include your full name and address to allow us to process your request.

a.4.5. To amend your log-in details but stay part of the “Loaded” service, please email your details and request to admin@loaded.co.uk.

Except as otherwise expressly provided in this Privacy Policy or on this website, only “Loaded” and its affiliate companies collect Personal Data through the services provided solely by “Loaded”. This Privacy Policy applies only to the use and disclosure of Personal Data we collect while you use our Service.

a.6.1. Your Personal Data may be processed both in and outside of your own country. When you submit your details and in whichever country you are situated you agree to the data being transferred to the UK and processed under the terms of the UK Data Protection Act 1998 and the Privacy & Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003 which stem from European Directives 95/46/EC and 2002/58/EC respectively. If you are not in agreement, please do not give us your personal details.

a.6.2. We may also require you to enter a password to access or update your account information. ## b. cookies policy

## b.1. what are cookies and why are they used?

b.1.1. A cookie is a small file of letters and numbers we store on your browser or the hard drive of your computer or mobile or tablet device when you first visit one of our web pages.

b.1.2. We use cookies to distinguish you from other users of our website. This helps us provide you with a good experience when you browse our website and also allows us to improve the site. The cookies we use enable us to recognise your device so you don’t have to give the same information several times during one task.

b.1.3. Cookies recognise when you have given your username and password so you don’t need to do it for every web page you visit. They also measure how many people use our websites so we can make it easier to use and ensure there is enough capacity to make the site as responsive as possible.

b.1.4. Certain cookies contain personal information. For example, if you click on a ‘Remember me’ button when you log in a cookie will store your username.

b.1.5. Most cookies will not collect information that personally identifies you. They will collect more general information such as how users arrive at and use our websites, or a user’s general location.

## b.2. what type of cookies do we use?

b.2.1. Cookies used by “Loaded” perform up to four different functions, as follows.  
          b.2.1.1. Strictly Necessary cookies are required for the operation of our Service. They include cookies which enable you to log in to secure areas of our Service.  
          b.2.1.2. Analytical & Performance Cookies These are cookies allowing “Loaded” to recognise and count the number of visitors to the Service. They enable us to see how visitors move around the Service while using it. This information helps us to improve the way the Service works by, for example, ensuring users can easily find what they are looking for.  
          b.2.1.3. Functional Cookies These are used to recognise your device when you return to our Service. They enable us to personalise our content for you, greet you by name and remember choices such as your language or country.  
          b.2.1.4. Tracking Cookies These record your visits to our Service, the pages you view and the links you follow. We use this information to make our Service and the advertising displayed on it more relevant to your interests. We may also share this information with third parties for this purpose.

## b.3. can third parties use our cookies?

b.3.1. Please note third parties (including, for example, advertising networks and providers of external services such as web traffic analysis services) may also use cookies on our website. For example, advertisers may use a profile they have built on sites you have previously visited to present you with more relevant advertisements during your visit to our website. We have no control over third party cookies.

b.3.2. Cookies used by third parties are likely to be analytical/performance cookies or tracking cookies. Third party cookies cannot be used to identify you personally and can only be used for statistical analysis. For example, they provide you with advertisements more relevant to your interests. If you are based in the European Union and would like to learn more about how advertisers use these types of cookies, or to choose not to receive them, please visit [youronlinechoices.eu](http://www.youronlinechoices.eu/). If you are based in the United States and would like to learn more, please visit [aboutads.info/choices](http://www.aboutads.info/choices)/.

## b.4. can cookies be disabled or deleted?

b.4.1. If you would like to disable third party cookies you can do so by going to the relevant third party website and opting out there. Please note if you opt out of third party advertising cookies, you will still continue to receive advertising when using the website. However, such advertising will no longer be tailored to you.

b.4.2. You can use your browser settings to restrict or block cookies set by “Loaded”. The Help function within your browser should tell you how to activate the setting on your browser that allows you to refuse the setting of all or some cookies. Alternatively, you may wish to visit [aboutcookies.org](http://www.aboutcookies.org/) which contains comprehensive information about how to restrict or block cookies on a wide variety of browsers. You will also find information on that website about how to delete cookies from your machine as well as more general information about cookies.

b.4.3. If you use your browser settings to block all cookies, including essential cookies, you may not be able to access all or parts of our Service.
