Public Records – Privacy and Data Security

Thank you for visiting Maryland Electronic Capital.  We take data security and the privacy of your personal information very seriously.  Maryland law mandates that State agencies shall not create personal records unless the need for the information is clearly established and that personal records shall be relevant to the purposes for which they are collected, be accurate and current, and not be obtained by fraudulent means. State officials who request personal data from citizens must notify the citizens regarding the purposes for which the information is collected; consequences to the citizen for refusing to provide the personal information; and, the citizen’s right to inspect and correct personal records. In addition, citizens must be notified as to whether the information is generally available for public inspection; and whether the information is made available to other entities. Please refer to the eMaryland technology website for additional material on information technology policies in Maryland. The State of Maryland is committed to providing citizens with websites over which business may be securely and confidently transacted.
