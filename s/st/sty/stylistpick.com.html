Stylistpick Cookies Policy

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer’s hard drive when you visit a website. By setting an anonymous, unique identifier, cookies help websites recognise returning visitors and remember their preferences. Many cookies are automatically deleted after you finish using a website.

Cookies cannot harm your computer. They are not programmes and they cannot carry viruses or install malware on your computer.

You can learn more about cookies and how to manage them by visiting the website www.aboutcookies.org/ (opens in a new window – please note that we are not responsible for the content of external websites).

How to control and delete cookies

Stylistpick will not use cookies to collect personally identifiable information about you. However if you wish you can instruct your browser, by editing its options, to stop accepting cookies or to prompt you before accepting a cookie from the web sites you visit. The help function in your browser should tell you how to do this.

Please be aware that restricting cookies may have an impact on the functionality of the Stylistpick website. For instance, to order products at Stylistpick you need to have cookies enabled, which is the default setting for most web browsers like Firefox, Chrome or Internet Explorer.

For information on how to manage cookies on your mobile phone browser, please see your handset manual.

How Stylistpick uses cookies

We use cookies to:

  1. Keep track of what you have in your shopping bag
  2. Remember you when you return to our site
  3. Count the number of visitors to our website and other site usage information
  4. Increase the relevancy of advertising displayed to you

We have an advertising agreement with Google who may set cookies during your visit to be used for remarketing purposes. This is so that we can serve more relevant advertising to you based on what you appear to be interested in. If you would like to opt out of remarketing cookies, including Google’s cookies, please go to the Network Advertising Initiative website (opens in a new window – please note that we are not responsible for the content of external websites).

IP addresses

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect system-related information about your visit, such as the type of internet browser and operating system you use, the website from which you have come to our website and your IP address (the unique address which identifies your computer on the internet) which is automatically recognised by our web server. This information is collected for system administration and to report aggregate information to our advertisers. It is statistical data about our users' browsing actions and does not, of itself, contain any personally identifiable information.

  • Details of your visits to our pages or presences on social networking sites (whether you access them via our website or otherwise).
