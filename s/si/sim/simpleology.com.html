Cookies

A cookie is a small text file that is stored on a user’s computer for record-keeping purposes. It allows information entered in one webpage to be “remembered” when you enter a new webpage.

We use both session ID cookies and persistent cookies. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time.

We use session cookies to remember login information entered in our WebCockpit and DesktopCockpit areas, and our Support Suite as you click through our web pages. The session cookie will expire once you logout. If you voluntarily select for our system to “remember” you next time you login, we will set a persistent cookie on your machine in place of the usual session cookie, and this cookie will store your login information and recall it next time you login.

For the WebCockpit and DesktopCockpit, the persistent cookie simply recalls your login information (i.e. your username and password) and is not linked to your personally identifiable information.

In the Support Suite, your email address is used to form the username part of your Support Suite login, therefore both the session and persistent cookies in this section are linked to that personally identifiable information.

Information about how to clear your forum cookies can be found here:

http://www.simpleology.com/forums/faq.php?faq=vb_user_maintain#faq_vb_clear_cookies If you reject cookies, your ability to use our site will be limited. If your system does not allow cookies for example, you will find that every time you access a new webpage inside our Control Panel, you will be prompted to login again.

For more information about cookies, please see:
