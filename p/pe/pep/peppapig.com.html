Last modified and effective as of 13 July 2018.

Our legal status is that of a "data controller" and in this capacity we will securely store and process your/your children’s personal information which you/your children have provided to us.  Data controller is a legal term used in EU legislation to signify the person who controls what to do with any given personal information.  We are registered as a data controller with the Information Commissioner’s Office (registration number Z9919769).

Where is PII collected on the Peppa Pig Online Service?

The Peppa Pig Online Service may contain websites and mobile apps directed to children, including young children between the ages of 2 and 5.  It contains a children’s section (the “Children’s Area”) and a parents’ section (the “Grown-Up Area”).  In the Children’s Area, children mainly participate only by playing games, reading e-books and watching videos. We generally do not offer any place in the Children’s Area where a child can sign up or enter PII. Typically, the only part of the Peppa PIg Online Service where anyone can enter information is the Grown-Up Area. 

Accordingly, in most cases, we do not anticipate that we will be collecting PII directly from any children.  We anticipate and assume that we will be collecting PII only from parents. If we do learn that a child under the age of 18 has provided PII on the Peppa Pig Online Service, we will not process any PII of that child without the prior consent of that child’s parent or guardian in accordance with COPPA.

How do we use cookies?

A cookie is a small data file that a website transfers to a user's hard drive when a user visits the website.  A cookie file can contain information such as a unique ID that the site uses to track the pages visited, and is a standard method of saving website user preferences.  These preferences are stored in a file on your computer's hard disk drive.  

We use “analytical” cookies to recognize and count the number of visitors to the Peppa Pig Online Service and to see how visitors move around the Peppa Pig Online Service when they’re using it.  This helps us improve the way the Peppa Pig Online Service works, for example, by making sure users find what they need easily.

The Peppa Pig Online Service may also use local shared objects, also known as flash cookies. These are pieces of data that websites which use Adobe Flash Player may store on your computer, such as game preferences or game progress.

You can set your browser to accept all cookies, to reject cookies, or to notify you whenever a cookie is offered so that you can decide each time whether to accept it.  Rejecting a cookie, however, may preclude you from using, or may negatively affect the display or function of, the Peppa Pig  Online Service or certain areas or features of the PJ Masks Online Service.  

To learn more about cookies, including how to enable cookies, change your browser settings and find and delete cookies on your hard drive, please refer to your web browser's help menus or visit https://ico.org.uk/your-data-matters/online/cookies/

We may also use web beacons. A web beacon is an object that is embedded in a web page or email and is usually invisible to the user but allows checking that a user has viewed the page or email.
