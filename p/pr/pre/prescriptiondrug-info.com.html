What Information Do We Collect?

MedsChat is the sole owner of the information collected on the MedsChat.com website. We collect personally identifiable information from our users at several different points on our website.

(i) In order to participate in the discussion threads, we request that you provide your name (it may be a pseudonym for anonymity) and optionally your email address; providing your email address enables us to notify you whenever somebody posts a reply to your message as well as receive our daily health newsletter if you choose to subscribe to it.

(ii) Healthcare professionals who choose to become a Registered Community Expert are required to provide contact information (such as name and email address), their website address, and geographic location. Providing your email address enables us to notify you whenever somebody posts a reply to your messages, receive administrative communications from us, as well as receive our daily health newsletter if you choose to subscribe to it.

If you choose to subscribe to our newsletter, we will use your email address to send it to you. Out of respect for your privacy, we provide you with a way to unsubscribe. Please see "Opting Out" for more details.

We may collect and store certain information automatically. This information will not be shared with anyone beyond the website support staff, except when required by law enforcement investigation (such as to comply with a judicial proceeding, court order or subpoena), and will be used only as a source of anonymous statistical information. This information may include:
  • Internet Protocol (IP) address of the domain from which You access the Internet, either your individual address or the proxy provided by your Internet Service Provider (ISP).
  • Date and time You access our site.
  • Pages You view (recorded by the text and graphics files that compose that page).
  • Web address of the site from which You linked directly to our site.
  • Browser version and operating system of the computer You are using.
We may use statistical data to help us make our site more useful to visitors, such as assessing what information is of most and least interest to visitors, and for other purposes such as determining the site's technical design specifications and identifying system performance or problem areas.

You may also choose to provide us with personal information, by sending e-mail with questions or comments or by filling out an online form and submitting it to us through our website. We use this information to:
  • Gather the requested information.
  • Respond to your message.
  • Follow-up as appropriate.
We do not sell, share, rent or trade transfer any personal information to third parties for their promotional use.

However, your email message, question, or comment may be forwarded to other MedsChat employees who may be better able to help You. Except for authorized law enforcement investigations, we do not share our e-mail with any other outside organizations.

Cookies

Cookies are small files that websites use to store data on your computer. Most browsers are set up to automatically accept cookies. If you choose to block cookies (through your browser settings or other means), you can still use our site, but your ability to use or access some areas may be limited. Cookies are used in conjunction with our service in a variety of ways:

1. User Experience


Cookies can help to make navigation around our site more swift and prevent you from having to reenter certain information that you have previously given us. By using any of our online forms to participate in discussion threads, send us a message, email yourself an rx discount card, or subscribe to our health newsletter, your email address (plus your name if it is collected) is stored in a cookie so that you do not need to reenter it the next time you visit our site. Please note that this cookie is NOT shared with any outside parties. Furthermore, if you select the option "I'm on a public or shared computer" before submitting the form, we will not store this information inside a cookie. If you wish to clear this previously stored information click here.

2. Measuring Our Visitors (Analytics)


We use Google Analytics to measure visits to our website. This records how you arrived here, what pages you view within our site, and some basic information about your computer and location. Please note that this information is anonymized and does not tell us who you are. It simply shows us that somebody visited our site. Collecting data in this way allows us to discover how and when visitors use our site and to determine the most and least useful pages. Ultimately, analytics software allows us to make our website better. If you wish to learn more or opt out of Google Analytics tracking, please visit:
https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

3. Advertising


We work with third-party advertising companies to serve ads when you visit our website. In the course of serving such ads, these companies may place or recognize a unique cookie through your browser. They may use information about your visits to this and other websites in order to provide advertisements about goods and services of interest to you. The companies that generate these cookies have their own privacy policies, and we do not have access to read or write these cookies.
