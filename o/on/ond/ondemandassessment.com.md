    1. Cookies - Criteria uses cookies to make interactions with its sites easy and meaningful. When you visit one of Criteria's Web sites, Criteria's servers place a cookie to your computer. Standing alone, cookies do not personally identify you, they merely recognize your web browser; you remain anonymous to Criteria.There are two types of cookies: session-based and persistent-based. Session cookies exist only during one session. They disappear from your computer when you close your browser software or turn off your computer. Persistent cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser or turn off your computer.
    2. IP Addresses - When you visit Criteria's Web sites, it collects your Internet Protocol ("IP") addresses to track and aggregate non-personally identifiable information. For example, Criteria uses IP addresses to monitor the regions from which users navigate Criteria's Web sites.
