Your privacy is critically important to us.

At Codiqa we have a few fundamental principles:

  • We don’t ask you for personal information unless we truly need it. (We can’t stand services that ask you for things like your gender or income level for no apparent reason.)
  • We don’t share your personal information with anyone except to comply with the law, develop our products, or protect our rights.
  • We don’t store personal information on our servers unless required for the on-going operation of one of our services.
  • In our blogging products, we aim to make it as simple as possible for you to control what’s visible to the public, seen by search engines, kept private, and permanently deleted.

Below is our privacy policy which incorporates these goals: (Note, we’ve decided to make this privacy policy available under a Creative Commons Sharealike license, which means you’re more than welcome to steal it and repurpose it for your own use, just make sure to replace references to us with ones to you, and if you want we’d appreciate a link to Codiqa.com somewhere on your site. We spent a lot of money and time on the below, and other people shouldn’t need to do the same.)

If you have questions about deleting or correcting your personal data please contact our support team.

Drift, LLC (“Codiqa”) operates several websites including codiqa.com. It is Codiqa’s policy to respect your privacy regarding any information we may collect while operating our websites.

Website Visitors

Like most website operators, Codiqa collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. Codiqa’s purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how Codiqa’s visitors use its website. From time to time, Codiqa may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.

Codiqa also collects potentially personally-identifying information like Internet Protocol (IP) addresses for logged in users and for users viewing apps shared with Codiqa. Codiqa only discloses logged in user and commenter IP addresses under the same circumstances that it uses and discloses personally-identifying information as described below, except that blog commenter IP addresses are visible and disclosed to the administrators of the blog where the comment was left.

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information

Certain visitors to Codiqa’s websites choose to interact with Codiqa in ways that require Codiqa to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that Codiqa gathers depends on the nature of the interaction. For example, we ask visitors who sign up for an account at Codiqa.com to provide a name and email address. Those who engage in transactions with Codiqa – by purchasing a paid plan, for example, are asked to provide additional information, including as necessary the personal and financial information required to process those transactions. In each case, Codiqa collects such information only insofar as is necessary or appropriate to fulfill the purpose of the visitor’s interaction with Codiqa. Codiqa does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below. And visitors can always refuse to supply personally-identifying information, with the caveat that it may prevent them from engaging in certain website-related activities.

Aggregated Statistics

Codiqa may collect statistics about the behavior of visitors to its websites. For instance, Codiqa may track views on shared apps to determine usage of the service. Codiqa may display this information publicly or provide it to others. However, Codiqa does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below.

Protection of Certain Personally-Identifying Information

Codiqa discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only to those of its employees, contractors and affiliated organizations that (i) need to know that information in order to process it on Codiqa’s behalf or to provide services available at Codiqa’s websites, and (ii) that have agreed not to disclose it to others. Some of those employees, contractors and affiliated organizations may be located outside of your home country; by using Codiqa’s websites, you consent to the transfer of such information to them. Codiqa will not rent or sell potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information to anyone. Other than to its employees, contractors and affiliated organizations, as described above, Codiqa discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only in response to a subpoena, court order or other governmental request, or when Codiqa believes in good faith that disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect the property or rights of Codiqa, third parties or the public at large. If you are a registered user of an Codiqa website and have supplied your email address, Codiqa may occasionally send you an email to tell you about new features, solicit your feedback, or just keep you up to date with what’s going on with Codiqa and our products. We primarily use our various product blogs to communicate this type of information, so we expect to keep this type of email to a minimum. If you send us a request (for example via a support email or via one of our feedback mechanisms), we reserve the right to publish it in order to help us clarify or respond to your request or to help us support other users. Codiqa takes all measures reasonably necessary to protect against the unauthorized access, use, alteration or destruction of potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information.

Cookies

A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor’s computer, and that the visitor’s browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. Codiqa uses cookies to help Codiqa identify and track visitors, their usage of Codiqa website, and their website access preferences. Codiqa visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using Codiqa’s websites, with the drawback that certain features of Codiqa’s websites may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

Business Transfers

If Codiqa, or substantially all of its assets were acquired, or in the unlikely event that Codiqa goes out of business or enters bankruptcy, user information would be one of the assets that is transferred or acquired by a third party. You acknowledge that such transfers may occur, and that any acquirer of Codiqa may continue to use your personal information as set forth in this policy.

Ads

Ads appearing on any of our websites may be delivered to users by advertising partners, who may set cookies. These cookies allow the ad server to recognize your computer each time they send you an online advertisement to compile information about you or others who use your computer. This information allows ad networks to, among other things, deliver targeted advertisements that they believe will be of most interest to you. This Privacy Policy covers the use of cookies by Codiqa and does not cover the use of cookies by any advertisers.

Privacy Policy Changes

Although most changes are likely to be minor, Codiqa may change its Privacy Policy from time to time, and in Codiqa’s sole discretion. Codiqa encourages visitors to frequently check this page for any changes to its Privacy Policy. If you have a Codiqa.com account, you should also check your Codiqa dashboard for alerts to these changes. Your continued use of this site after any change in this Privacy Policy will constitute your acceptance of such change.
