Information Collection

Cryptomundo is the sole owner of the information collected and displayed on the Cryptomundo Site. Cryptomundo may collect information from our users at several different points on the Cryptomundo Site.

Comments

In order to enhance their use of the Cryptomundo Site, users can choose to post comments on particular articles of interest. To leave a comment, users must submit certain personal information, including a valid email address.  Cryptomundo uses this information to screen users who leave comments in violation of our Terms and will not disclose any readily identifiable personal information to any other organization.

Mailing List

Cryptomundo may launch a mailing list for some or all of the Cryptomundo Site.  To sign up to receive regular emails from the Cryptomundo Site mailing list, users must submit personal information, including a valid email address and demographic information such as location and interests.  This information may be used in aggregate form only to assess general user interests in products and services.  Users who subscribe to a mailing list will receive regular emails from the Cryptomundo Site, including new articles and emails from third party sponsors.  If you do not wish to receive emails from sponsors, do not sign up for the mailing list.

Discussion Group and Cookies

Cryptomundo may launch a discussion board service on some or all of the Cryptomundo Site where users can discuss common topics of interest. Users will have the option of signing up for this service, and to do so must submit certain personal information, including a valid email address.  Cryptomundo uses this information to screen users who leave comments in violation of our Terms and will not disclose any readily identifiable personal information to any other organization.

The discussion board service may use cookies. A cookie is a piece of data stored on the user’s computer tied to information about the user. We may use both session ID cookies and persistent cookies. For the session ID cookie, once users close the browser, the cookie simply terminates. A persistent cookie is a small text file stored on the user’s hard drive for an extended period of time. Persistent cookies can be removed by following Internet browser help file directions.

By accepting a cookie from the Cryptomundo Site, users will not have to log in a password more than once, thereby saving time while on the site’s discussion board. We store a cookie on each user’s machine that contains a username and encrypted password. You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or to notify you when a cookie is set. If you reject all cookies, you may not be able to use Cryptomundo products or services that require you to sign in, and you may not be able to take full advantage of all offerings.

Links

The Cryptomundo Site contains links to other sites. Please be aware that Cryptomundo is not responsible for the privacy and content policies of other sites.  This Privacy Policy applies solely to information collected at the Cryptomundo Site.  Please use care in accessing other web sites.

Third Party Cookies

Some of our business partners may use cookies on our site (for example, advertisers).  Cryptomundo has no control over these cookies.  This Privacy Policy covers use of cookies by Cryptomundo only and not the use of cookies by any advertisers.

Third Party Advertising

The ads appearing on this Web site are delivered by third party providers such as Google®.  Information about visits to this site, such as number of visits (but not username or any personal information), is used to select and display ads to users on this Cryptomundo Site.
