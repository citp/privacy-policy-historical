## Collection and Use Practices for CrowdScience.com

### Information Collection & Use

We collect the following kinds of personal information on our website and from our Clients:

  * Contact Information such as name, email address, mailing address, phone number
  * Unique Identifiers such as user name, account number, password
  * Account and Profile Information
  * Support Request Information



We use this information to

  * Send you requested product or service information
  * Respond to customer service requests
  * Administer your account
  * Send you a newsletter
  * Send you marketing communications
  * Webinar Registration
  * Support Fulfillment



No personally identifiable information is automatically collected from visitors to this website. In the event that Crowd Science requests any such information from you, it will only be collected if you provide it on a voluntary basis, and Crowd Science will alert you in advance of the use to which such information will be put and to whom it may be provided.

### Public Forums

Our website offers publicly accessible blog. You should be aware that any information you provide in these areas may be read, collected, and used by others who access them.

### Customer Testimonials

With your consent we may post your testimonial along with your name.

### Social Media and Widgets

Our website includes Social Media Features, such as the Facebook “Like” button and Widgets, such as the ”Share This” button or interactive mini-programs that run on our website. These features may collect your IP address, which page you are visiting on our website, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly. Social Media features and Widgets are either hosted by a third party or hosted directly on our website. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing it.

### OpenID

You can log into our website using an Open ID provider. This service will authenticate your identity and provide you the option to share certain personal information with us such as your name and email address to pre-populate our sign up form.

## Non-Personally Identifiable Information

Certain non-personal information (such as the domain name of the visitor’s Internet service provider) is collected in the normal operation of Crowd Science’s Internet servers. This information is primarily used for internal purposes, and only in non-personally identifiable form. However, Crowd Science may, from time to time, provide this information in aggregated, non-personally identifiable form to other parties for marketing, advertising or other use.

The Crowd Science website may contain links to certain other websites which enable you to visit other sites through your internet browser. Crowd Science is not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of any other websites. ## General Privacy Practices

### Tracking Technologies

#### Cookies

A cookie is a small text file that is stored on a user’s computer for record-keeping purposes. We use cookies on this website, Service Platform, and within Surveys (see “Note To Survey Participants” below). We do not link the information we store in cookies to any personally identifiable information.

We use both session ID cookies and/or persistent cookies. We use session cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our site. A session ID cookie expires when you close you browser. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time.

We set a persistent cookie to store your passwords, so you don’t have to enter it more than once.  Persistent cookies also enable us to track and target the interests of our users to enhance the experience on our site.

If you reject cookies, you may still use our site, but your ability to use some areas of our site, such as Surveys or the Service Platform, will be limited.

#### Clear Gifs (Web Beacons/Web Bugs)

We employ a software technology called clear gifs (a.k.a. Web Beacons/Web Bugs).  Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of Web users. We use clear gifs in our HTML-based emails to let us know which emails have been opened by recipients.  This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.  If you would like to opt-out of these emails, please see “Choice and Opt-out.”

#### Third Party Tracking Technologies

The use of cookies or clear gifs by third parties is not covered by our privacy statement.  We do not have access or control over these cookies.

If you have any questions about security on our Web site, you can contact [privacy@crowdscience.com](mailto:privacy@crowdscience.com).
