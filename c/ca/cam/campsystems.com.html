Information We Collect

In some situations you may provide us with your personal identifying information (called “PII”). PII could include your name, email address, mailing address, phone number, fax number, job title/position, information about your company or business and any other information we collect about you that by itself is not PII but if combined with PII could be used to personally identify you.


You may provide us with PII when you:

any other transactions between you and us.

Cookies and Other Tracking Technologies

A “cookie” is a small file stored on your hard drive that contains information about your computer. We may use cookies to enable us to track and target your interests for the purpose of enhancing your experience on our Website(s). By showing how and when visitors use the Website(s), cookies help us identify how many unique users visit us, save user preferences and track user trends and patterns.


We use session cookies, which are cookies that are deleted when you leave our Website(s) and persistent cookies, which are cookies that remain after you leave our Website(s) so that you are recognized when you return.


The use of cookies is industry standard so your browser may be set to accept cookies. If you would prefer not to accept cookies, you can alter the configuration of your browser to reject all cookies or some cookies. Note, if you reject certain cookies, you may not be able to access all of our Website(s) features.


Web Beacons

We may use web beacons (also known as clear GIFs) to access cookies and count users who visit our Website(s) or open our HTML-formatted email messages. Web beacons are single-pixel graphic files with a unique identifier, similar to cookies. Web beacons are embedded invisibly on web pages and are stored on a user's hard drive. You can modify your browser settings to control web beacons.
