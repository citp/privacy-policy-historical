If you need to contact us, please do so here. The cafepharma sites are web sites under Cafepharma’s control. The cafepharma sites include a variety of features and services, including an on-line service to post and search employment opportunities. The cafepharma sites also include on-line communities designed to facilitate valuable professional and personal networking among users based on common experiences and interests, and allow you to create individual profiles (and a “Profile Name”), which may include personal information (“Profiles”). Protect your personal information by never providing social security, credit card, or bank account numbers to other parties. Please note that when you link to sites from cafepharma sites, this Privacy Policy does not apply, and we cannot control the activities on those other sites. If any of our sites deal with information not specifically mentioned in this Policy, then such information is not covered by this Policy. For example, this Policy does not cover data that we collect offline, on businesses or legal entities, on our employees, or through other third-party sites. Please realize that personal information you submit through resumes, or other uploaded or transmitted documents, is also not subject to this Privacy Policy. Information You Provide To Us Unless we otherwise described a situation about gathering information from you in this Policy, it will be obvious to you that you are providing information (as we generally seek only to collect personal information that you provide on a voluntary basis). You are allowed to use many features of cafepharma sites without any ‘registration’ or providing of any information, and therefore use such features anonymously. For example, our message boards in the forums. Please understand that even when using the site anonymously, your web browser or client software may transmit certain geographic information, or information regarding your choices while on our websites. For some other activities, we may ask you for additional personal and non-personal information. Either set of information is considered “Bio Information.” We use “cookies” (small pieces of data stored for an extended period of time on your computer, mobile phone, or other device) for individual sessions when you visit the site, in order to facilitate your use of the website. We use such “session cookies” for both anonymous and logged-in users. These session cookies, however, cannot be used to determine a visitor’s actions on our web sites, and we do not use ‘third party tracking’ cookies to track your actions on our or other sites. We do not collect IP addresses of anonymous users, and seek to prevent our ability to determine whom you are when you post anonymously, or your exact location. Information You Provide Others Through Our Site A primary purpose of our site is to allow you to share information with others, including your opinions, facts about your workplace, and facts about yourself. Please be aware that others may use such information to identify you, despite your anonymous posting of such information. When posting under a Profile Name, an option allows others to see all your posts in the aggregate, or see a listing of all posts made under such Profile Name. Visitors to our site can also access and see the Bio Information that you choose to provide when you create your Profile. If you do not want us to store metadata associated with content that you share on our sites (such as photos), please remove the metadata before uploading the content. Sometimes the advertisers who present ads on our sites use technological methods to measure the effectiveness of their ads, and to personalize advertising content. You may use your browser cookie settings to limit or prevent the placement of cookies by advertising networks. We do not share personally identifiable information with advertisers. We highly regard your privacy, and will not share your information except as detailed here. We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes without your explicit consent. Again, we view protection of users' privacy as a very important community principle. We may disclose information pursuant to subpoenas, court orders, or other requests (including criminal and civil matters) if we have a good faith belief that the law requires the response. We may also share information when we have a good faith belief it is necessary to prevent threats against our web services, or to prevent imminent bodily harm. This may include sharing information with other companies, lawyers, courts, or other government entities. Again, however, we do not keep any information on visitors except as expressly detailed in this Policy, and therefore have little-to-no information to share regarding anonymous postings.
