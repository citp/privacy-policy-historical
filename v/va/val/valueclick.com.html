CONSUMER PRIVACY
ValueClick, Inc. serves advertising to websites using two different delivery methods; the first is through ValueClick's standard delivery system, the second using DoubleClick's DART delivery system. Internet consumers may encounter one or both advertising systems as they travel through the Internet. Each system has its own capabilities and purpose. Internet consumers should make note of both systems and the type of information each collects.

Internet Consumer Data Collected by ValueClick, Inc's Standard Delivery System.

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
When an Internet consumer clicks on one of ValueClick, Inc.'s served advertisements originating from ValueClick's standard delivery system, ValueClick, Inc. does not collect any personally identifiable information regarding the consumer who clicked on the ad (e.g. name, address, social security number, e-mail address).

Non-Personally Identifiable Information (Non-PII)
When an Internet consumer clicks on one of ValueClick, Inc.'s served advertisements originating from ValueClick's standard delivery system, ValueClick, Inc. does collect Non-Personally Identifiable Information (Non-PII), which is used solely for the purpose of delivering ads and reporting results. This is known in the industry as "ad delivery and reporting data". Ad delivery and reporting data includes type of browser, operating system, IP address, domain type, page(s) visited, date and time of when an ad has been responded to.

ValueClick does not use the collected NPII for any other purpose than to deliver ads and report results, and by itself this information cannot be used to identify any specific user.

ValueClick, Inc. retains all ad delivery and reporting data indefinitely, for accounting and auditing purposes.

Internet Consumer Data Collected by DoubleClick's DART Delivery System

Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
When an Internet consumer clicks on one of ValueClick, Inc.'s served advertisements originating from DoubleClick's DART delivery system, DART does not collect any personally identifiable information regarding the consumer who clicked on the ad (e.g. name, address, social security number, e-mail address).

Non-Personally Identifiable Information (Non-PII)
When an Internet consumer clicks on one of ValueClick, Inc.'s served advertisements originating from DoubleClick's DART delivery system, DART does collect non-personally identifiable information (Non-PII). This information includes the type of browser, operating system, IP address, domain type, page(s) visited, and the date and time of when an Internet consumer has responded to an advertisement.

Non-personally identifiable data is collected to assist in ad targeting and to measure ad effectiveness on behalf of our advertisers and web publishers who specifically request it.

Consumer Choice Concerning Data Collection

Advertising Originating from ValueClick's Standard Delivery System -
Use of Cookies - Remove and Disable option
When a consumer views an ad served by ValueClick's standard delivery system they are issued a "Cookie". A cookie is a tiny file that contains a number. The number is recorded and stored in ValueClick's system; the cookie is stored on the consumer's computer in the web browser cookie file. ValueClick only uses this information for delivering ads and reporting results. Cookies do not harm Internet consumers' computers and cannot collect any information about a person, only their computer.

Because ValueClick only collects information for delivering and reporting advertising, consumers who wish to "opt-out" or refuse a ValueClick cookie being issued to their computer have the option of disabling cookies within their web browser software, which means that they will no longer receive cookies without their approval. Internet consumers can also delete any cookies that currently reside on their computer. Internet consumers should refer to their respective browser's user manual for instruction on performing this action. It is important to note that disabling the cookie option in your web browser will block your computer from receiving cookies from all websites using this technology.

Advertising Originating from DoubleClick's DART Delivery System -
Use of Cookies - Opt-Out Option
When a consumer views an ad served by DoubleClick's DART delivery System they are issued a DART "Cookie". A DART cookie is a tiny file that contains a number. The number is recorded and stored by DoubleClick, the cookie is saved and stored on the consumer's computer in the web browser cookie file. When a consumer with a DART cookie arrives at any subsequent website using DoubleClick's DART advertising delivering system, DART will recognize the previously issued cookie.

Cookies are used by DART to record what advertising is interacted with so that ad delivery produces information relevant to the consumer's interests.
