Cookies

We may use cookies to obtain non-personally identifiable information from Web site visitors. Such information may include your “Internet Protocol Address,” operating system, browser type and the referring Web site’s address, if any (“Non-Personal Information”). Cookies are files your web browser places on your computer when you visit a website. Depending on your browser, you may be able to reject cookies or indicate when a cookie has been sent. Cookies enable us to customize your user interface, to speed up your interactions with the Website, to improve the Website and to facilitate your navigation through the Website. We may use session cookies (which survive only as long as your browser is open) to perform these tasks. We may also use cookies in order to analyze your Non-Personal Information in order to improve our Website through our analytics provider, as indicated in the “Use and Disclosure” section below.
