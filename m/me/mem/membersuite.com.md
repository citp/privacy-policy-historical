We're the leading provider of association management software for member-based organizations and as such we are committed to the establishment and maintenance of policies and procedures that protect the privacy of personal information that may be collected from customers, prospective customers, partners and others who may visit our website or use our software. This policy does not apply to third-party websites, products, or services even if they link to our services, and you should consider the privacy practices of those third-parties carefully.

### Personal Information 

Any information collected is used by MemberSuite for the purposes stated at the time of collection or as otherwise set out in this privacy statement. MemberSuite collects and uses your information only for the following purposes:

• To respond to your requests for further information about our MemberSuite™ software as a service offerings, our professional services, and our company.

• To register for our online demonstration of MemberSuite™.

• To provide you with subscriptions to MemberSuite newsletters or publications.

• To provide MemberSuite’ Partners with reliable and quality support.

• To support operational processes within MemberSuite.

• To meet legal and regulatory requirements.

Following your request for information about MemberSuite's products, we may request your permission to use your personal information for other informative purposes, for example, to send you an email newsletter or for marketing purposes. You will always be provided the opportunity to opt in to further contact by MemberSuite.

We endeavor to only to collect the personal information necessary in these circumstances. Personal information collected may include your name, address, phone number, fax number, organization, and e-mail address. All personal information collected from the public MemberSuite website is stored in our secure marketing database. Only authorized MemberSuite employees have access to this information.

Some areas of the MemberSuite website may require use of a username and password for access. This username and password allows you to access certain areas such as online support, the product knowledgebase, product purchases, and other restricted areas of the site. When it is necessary to contact you in connection with a particular online activity, this username and password is cross-referenced against the personal information you have provided to MemberSuite.

### Collection of Information

For each visitor to our public web site, our web server automatically recognizes only the visitor’s domain name, but not their e-mail address (where possible). We collect e-mail addresses from web site visitors that choose to opt in when signing up to communicate with us by e-mail. MemberSuite may use this information to contact prospective customers for marketing purposes and is not shared with any 3rd party organization for commercial purposes. If you would like to unsubscribe in the future, please let us know by calling 1 (678) 606-0310 or sending an e-mail with ‘Remove’ as the subject line to [dataprivacy@membersuite.com](mailto:dataprivacy@membersuite.com). 

If you opt in and supply us with a postal address online, you may receive periodic mailings with new product information, upcoming events, or company news. If you would prefer not to receive mailings from us, please call us at 1 (678) 606-0310 and we will remove your name from our mailing list.

If you supply us with your telephone number online, you may receive telephone contact from MemberSuite with new product information, upcoming events, or company news. If you would prefer not to receive telephone calls from us, please send us an e-mail with ‘Remove’ as the subject line to info@membersuite.com, include your name and phone number, and we will remove your name from our call list.

To be removed simultaneously from e-mail, phone, and mail lists, please send us an e-mail with ‘remove from all lists’ in the subject line to [dataprivacy@membersuite.com](mailto:dataprivacy@membersuite.com).

### MemberSuite as a Data Processor

We may collect, use and disclose certain Personal Data about individuals when acting as the customer’s service provider. Our customers are responsible for making sure that the individual’s privacy rights are respected, including ensuring appropriate disclosures about third party data collection and use. To the extent that we are acting as a data processor, we will process Personal Data in accordance with the terms of our agreement with the Customer and the Customer’s lawful instructions.

MemberSuite processes personal information on behalf of its Customers, who use our software to manage memberships, payments, event registration, certifications and professional or educational accreditations, and billing and invoicing purposes. 

The elements of personal data may include:

  * Name
  * Company name
  * Home, shipping and billing addresses, phone and fax number
  * Mobile telephone number
  * E-mail address
  * IP address and browser information
  * Market research data such as customer usage patterns



Other data processed on behalf of our customers includes Transactional data, such as purchases, purchase amount, date of purchase and payment method

### Cookies 

MemberSuite may use a technology called ‘cookies’ to record or track information related to visitors to our public website. Cookies are simple text files that web browsers place on computer hard drives when sites are visited. We may use cookies to deliver content specific to your interests. Cookies, by themselves, do not tell MemberSuite your e-mail address or other personally identifiable information, unless you choose to provide this information when asking for further information from us. For example, when requesting product information. However, once you choose to furnish the site with personally identifiable information, this information may be linked to the data stored in the cookie.

These cookies are specific to the website session and are not used to record personal information or to associate personal information with the use of the MemberSuite™ service. Users may disable their web browser’s ability to accept cookies without affecting their ability to successfully navigate the MemberSuite public website.

To improve access to content based on visitors' browsers and operating system types, and to make our content available to as many visitors as possible, MemberSuite may collect certain technical information from your computer’s web browser or Internet device when you visit www.membersuite.com. This information includes the amount of time spent on the site by a visitor with a unique Internet identifier, the pages visited and returned to, the operating system, and Web browser used, the referring Web site and any keywords used to find our site on a search website. These server logs do not contain any personal information and can only be accessed by employees of MemberSuite. Server log files are used solely to track visits to our Web site and improve the experience, content and layout of our site. MemberSuite makes no attempt to link this traffic information to the individuals visiting our Web site.

Cookies are also used by our software platform for the following purposes: 

  * Identifying returning users and registrants
  * Enabling users to move more easily around the member portal
  * Tracking use of our portal to better develop our product in accordance with our customers’ requirements



As with our MemberSuite website, a user may choose to set their Web browser to not accept cookies.

### Security 

MemberSuite has appropriate measures in place to help ensure that our customers’ data is protected against unauthorized access or use, alteration, unlawful or accidental destruction and accidental loss. When our site is accessed from any browsers, Secure Socket Layer (SSL) technology protects information using both server authentication and data encryption to help ensure that Data is safe, secure, and available only to you. MemberSuite also implements an advanced security method based on dynamic data and encoded session identifications and hosts the Site in a secure server environment that uses a firewall and other advanced technology to prevent interference or access from outside intruders. The server is protected by an enterprise firewall that is immediately updated when new patches and fixes are released.

We employ security measures in our physical facilities to help protect against the loss, misuse, and alteration of the Data under our control. 

Although we make reasonable efforts to protect your Personal Information from loss, misuse, or alteration by third parties, you should be aware that there is always some risk involved in transmitting information over the Internet. 

### Transfer of Data

MemberSuite shares information with third-parties that are based outside the European Economic Area (EEA). We ensure personal data is protected by using companies in the US that have certified to EU-US Privacy Shield, an approved set of privacy standards specifically designed for data sent to the US from the EEA.
