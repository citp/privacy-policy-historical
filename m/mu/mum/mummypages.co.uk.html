Cookies
 
Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer by websites that you visit. They are widely used in order to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the owners of the site. We use cookies to gain a better understanding how visitors use our websites. Cookies help us tailor the website content to your personal tastes. We keep all the information collected from cookies in a non–personally identifiable format. Cookies located on your computer do not contain your name but an IP address.
 
Please ensure that your computer setting reflects whether you are happy to accept cookies or not. It is possible to set some browsers to warn you before accepting cookies, or you can simply set it to refuse them, although you may not have access to all the features of this website if you do so. See your browser 'help' button for how you can do this. You do not need to have cookies on to use or navigate through many parts of our websites.
 
The MummyPages website currently includes the use of the following first party cookies:
 
PHPSESSID – cookie which identifies user session. It allows user to provide relevant content by his browsing experience. Also, it is required for login. Disabling it will make some functions not to work properly, and login will not work.
home_tab_active – cookie, which remembers, which tab in title page (tools, popular baby names, etc.) user has clicked. Next time user will come to title page, last viewed tab will be shown. Disabling it will always display default tab.
user_info – cookie, which stores unique identifier for user. It is required to ensure security of logged-in user, and to allow automatic login next time user visits website (if user wants to do so). Cookie is unique for user’s computer, so malicious users will not be able to simulate . Disabling it will make login impossible.
remember_me – cookie, which remembers if user has requested automatic login next time he visits website. Disabling it will make automatic login impossible.
already_show – flashbox cookie, stores if flashbox was already shown.
mummypages_count – required for flashbox
 
In addition, the MummyPages wesbite uses certain third party cookies which are set by third parties we use for different services (for example, website analytics or advertising).
