Security

"Clearance" and a "need to know" form the basis for our security policy. "Clearance" is not enough to view secure information; an employee must also have the "need" to see that information. Clearance means that an employee has gained the trust of our organization through dedicated service. By enforcing both "clearance" and a "need to know" we limit those who have access to your personal information and we can easily enforce accountability.

Mammoth Mountain has gone to great lengths to ensure the security of your financial information such as credit card and other booking information. All credit card transactions are handled on separate secure servers. Only authorized employees have access to these servers and your personal information that resides on those servers.

Personal Information Collection and Use

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with outside organizations. Mammoth asks you to provide information that will enable us to enhance your site visit, process your order correctly, and if necessary, follow up with you before or after your visit.

We will request information from you when you:

  • Register on MammothMountain.com
  • Plan a trip to Mammoth Mountain
  • Place an order with Mammoth Mountain
  • Provide feedback in an online survey
  • Participate in a sweepstakes or other promotional offer
  • Subscribe to a newsletter or a mailing list
  • Create your online guest profile
  • Participate in loyalty or club card programs
  • Purchase specific services such as rentals, lessons, and lodging

In each of these instances, we may ask for any of the following types of information: name, e-mail address, phone number, address, birthdate, customer and trip preference information, customer ID and password, as well as other similar personal information that is needed to register or subscribe you to services or offers.

Additionally, we may collect data to better understand your preferences. This information allows us to improve our products and services, and to offer you specially tailored deals when appropriate.

We may ask for an age or birth date when applicable for guest identification and pricing purposes (for instance, children and seniors qualify for discounts on lift tickets) and when creating your online guest profile. Federal law prohibits discrimination in public accommodations based on age, so when we send out information on lodging bookings we are required to treat all site members equally, regardless of age.

Changing Profile and Opting Out

If you chose to not receive promotional material or special offers from us including but not limited to e-mail, direct mail or telephone, we ask that you tell us by opting out if given the choice or by calling us at 760-934-0745. You have the ability to create a guest profile by clicking on manage profile in the footer below and creating a personal preferences profile. Creating a guest profile enables you to customize your interaction with Mammoth by choosing or changing your preferences, updating your information, opting in or out of direct mail and e-mail communication at any time.

Please note that in the case of newsletters or mailing lists, you will be able to unsubscribe from these mailings at any time by updating your preferences in your guest profile.

When you register and create your account using the internet, we will confirm your registration by sending an email to the address that you supplied to us. All future emails to your account will contain information on how to remove you from our distribution lists.

We may also use personal account information to generate statistics and aggregate reports for internal use and for sharing with affiliates. These statistics and aggregate reports will not contain any personally identifiable information. Your identity is kept anonymous.

Aggregate information may be used to design programs and promotions that we think would be of interest to an identified group of customers. For example, if an analysis of purchase data shows that 30% of our guests are intermediate skiers who rent equipment, we may want to develop a special rental/lesson program for them to test new gear.

Internet Tracking using Cookies

Depending on how you configure your browser, when you visit MammothMountain.com a small file (a "cookie") is stored on your hard drive. Whenever you log onto MammothMountain.com our server looks for your cookie and may make adjustments in order to customize what appears as you browse our site. If your browser is set to reject cookies, none of this customization is possible.

Non-Use and Disclosure

We do not and will not sell, rent, or share your information to any outside entity. Please remember that anything posted on our forums becomes public information and you should exercise caution about disclosing personal information on those pages.

Mammoth Mountain reserves the right to cooperate with local, provincial, federal, and international officials in any investigation requiring either personal information (including any information provided on-line through MammothMountain.com) or reports about lawful of unlawful user activity on this site.

Mammoth Mountain also reserves the right to change this Privacy Policy at any time.

Children's Privacy

Mammoth Mountain does not knowingly solicit data from, or about, people under the age of 16 years for the purposes of selling directly to these individuals. Mammoth Mountain assumes that visitors to our site are adults.

Legal Liability

Under no circumstances, including, but not limited to, negligence, shall Mammoth Mountain be liable for any direct, indirect, special, incidental or consequential damages, including, but not limited to, loss of data or profit, arising out of the use, or the inability to use, the materials on this site, even if Mammoth Mountain or a Mammoth Mountain authorized representative has been advised of the possibility of such damages.

Fishing Report Details

Hot Creek  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: Fishing has been good in the late morning and mid afternoon. Trico's, Midge's and small Baetis midday. Best action is in the afternoon as the fish key on the Caddis emergance. Water levels are low. Still possible to fish small dry fly patterns with success. The stream flows are now under 15 cfs. High temps in the 50's and lows in the 30's or colder. Winter is quickly approaching and the fishing season is winding down. Be prepared for snow and dangerous weather any time.
The last day of fishing season is Nov. 15

Nymphing Patterns: Trico, Surface emergers, scuds, Caddis emerger patterns. San Juan Worms

Dry Fly Patterns: Bwo's, Caddis Trico's Hoppers patterns smaller sizes.

Streamer: Small streamers can work well in the afternoon if you want try something different.

Fishing Report Details

Upper Owens  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: The Upper Owens river has been fishing well. It looks like a few more fish have moved up the river out of the lake. Best action is in the midmorning and late afternoon some spinner fall in the mornings and caddis in the evenings. Plenty of stocked fish are still being caught. Casting small dry flies to rising fish can be successful midday. If you are in the area above the Benton Crossing Bridge and see people using Bait remind them it is not allowed or call CA Dept of Fish & Game. Plenty of water to fish with nobody really fishing the upper river. Flows are holding at 122 cfs and the area down near the lake is holding some larger fish as the lake has flooded the river channel making it very easy for the lake fish to access the river. Try pulling streamer over the channel or use Callibaetis patterns in the morning. The area above the Hot Creek Tribs is fishing well for those willing to stalk the clear water. We have hooked some nice resident Brown Trout along with the stockers 10-14" Rainbows that move up from the campground area. Nymphing the riffles and slower pool has produced the best results. If the evenings are still or windless you can have some great results on the dry fly for the larger Brown trout as there is good Caddis activity.

The Upper Owens River is under special regulations. It is best to look at your CADFG regulation booklet for current regulations.

High temps in the 50's and lows in the 30's or colder. Winter is quickly approaching and the fishing season is winding down. Be prepared for snow and dangerous weather any time.
The last day of fishing season is Nov. 15

Nymphing Patterns: Prince Nymphs, Pheasent Tails, Hares Ears, San Juan worms, Are all good flies to start with.

Dry Fly Patterns: Adams, Orange Stimulators,PMDs and, caddis patterns in size 16-20s

Streamer: Bead Head Black or Olive Wolly Buggers, Muddler Minnows you will need some weight to get them down no dip and strip necessary. Size 8.

Crowley Tributaries

Special regulations are in effect on the tribs at different times of the year - read your DFG pamphlet to be sure of which areas are open or closed.

Streamer: Larger streamers 6-8s.

Fishing Report Details

Lower Owens  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: Flows are up to 600 cfs which make for difficult wading. Midges, Caddis, Tricos Mayflies and Baetis late Morning are the productive for the first half of the day. Try Caddis in the evenings along with the Trico Mayflies emerging at dusk. Daytime temps are comfortable now that it is fall. The Lower Owens fishes best when water levels are less than 300 cfs. The Gorge above the Pleasant Reservoir is a great spot with plenty of opportunities for wild Brown Trout 12-16. You will have to hike to reach this area. It is mostly pocket water and pools. Beware - rattlesnakes may be present and there is only limited access. Take plenty of water, you will need it. This is a year round fishery and can very productive all winter long.

Nymphing Patterns: Try nymphing in the mornings with WD-40s and Brassies, then go to Bead Head Pheasant Tails, Olive Surface Emergers, Barr's wet emergers or RS Emergers. Sizes 18-20s epoxyback Trico's 18-22s.

Dry Fly Patterns: Small midge patterns, tricos and caddis pattern 18-20s.

Attractor Patterns: Small Stimulator, Trudes, Wulffs.

Fishing Report Details

East Walker  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: The Flow continue to drop and it might be time to fish the Nevada part of the river it's 5 mile of Catch and release. Current flows are down to 29cfs. The average size is around 13" with larger fish possible at any given drift. I think the trout up in the miricle mile have rubber lips with lots of short takes and hard to keep on the line. Water temps in the mid 60s mid day. Fishing the pocket water is possible in the areas below the bridge to Murphys Pond. More fish are in the 12-15" range. Flows are at the lowest level since opening week. Use Midges, Caddis, and small pale Mayflies and Caddis in the evenings. Levels of the river will still go up and down as the farmer's down stream will call for more liquid gold to water Alfalfa crops. Check flow before departing!
High temps in the 50's and lows in the 20's or colder. Winter is quickly approaching and the fishing season is winding down. Be prepared for snow and dangerous weather any time.
The last day of fishing season in Ca. is Nov. 15. You can fish Nevada all winter, for licenses and info go to Kens in Bridgeport.

Nymphing Patterns: Pheasant Tails, Olive Surface Emergers, Brassies, Red Copper Johns, Bead Head Black and Olive WD-40s, Disco Midges, Barr's Wet Emergers, Zebra Midges, Olive & Natural Birds Nests, Prince Nymphs, and Green Nori's Caddis. San Juan Worm (red) size 14-16.

Dry Fly Patterns: BWO, PMDs, Hemingway Caddis, EC Caddis, Schroeder's Para Caddis and Adams in sizes 16-18, Orange and Yellow stimulators sizes 14-16.

Streamer: Try Muddler Minnows, Wool Head Sculpins, Bunny Leech's, Woolly Buggers and Dave's Chubs. Use size 6s and see what happens.

Fishing Report Details

Crowley Lake  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: CADFG has planted most of all the trout around 400,000 for next years crop. Good fishing can still be found by the mouth of the Owens in the river channel. But McGee Bay is picking up as the water has starting to clear. The good news is some of the DFG fish planted look to be larger than years past could be some great late Fall action and figuered out how to eat. The new planters have changed the dynamic on the lake as they feed on anything and everything. Some of the little guys have made it to McGee Bay and the North end. The algae bloom is fading fast with the cooler temperatures. Look for the areas of clear or clearer water to have the best result in general. The lake is on its way down it's dropped 8' and Lake levels will continue to drop. The weed beds have become more defined.
Regs have changed since August 1st to the trophy Trout Season as only single barbless flies or lures maybe used and only 2 trout may be taken and must be 18" or bigger. It is still crowded on the weekends fishermen are getting their last licks as the end of fishing season approaches. With more areas clearing of algae people are now spreading out over the lake. You'll also find fish in Green Banks, Six Bays, Sandy Point and Sometimes Bay. Midging has picked back up. Stripping has been working well for some anglers and it may be better to strip than Midging for those with the right patterns.Some fisherman have had some great success on stripping perch fry patterns in the early mornings and late evenings or through out the day if weather conditions permit.

Use common sense when driving on the backside of the lake, as there are areas where the ground is very soft, and a tow truck will be costly.

High temps in the 60's and lows in the 30's or colder. Winter is quickly approaching and the fishing season is winding down. Be prepared for snow and dangerous weather any time.
The last day of fishing season is Nov. 15

The Crowley Lake docks are now closed for the season.

Nymphing Patterns: Black or Gray Midges with copper or red and silver wire have been the best, size 16-18s. Callibaetis Nymphs size 16. Brown and Olive leechs getting some good strikes!!

Dry Fly Patterns: Hemmingway Caddis size.14-18s. size 16-14 Adams

Streamer: Olive leeches and Wolly Buggers, Damsel fly Nymphs. Callibaetis Nymphs all have been working. Try your Perch fry patterns!!!!!

Try pulling 2 Hares Ears size 12 or 14 with a 10-20 sink tip line.. hang on!

Mammoth Lakes Basin  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Lenny Yee: Cold weather is here. The Brook and Brown Trout are showing their Spawning colors.Try pulling streamer near the back of Lake Mary. Basin has been well stocked. Fishing Report Details

Bridgeport Reservoir  

Last Updated Nov 3, 2006 by Tom Petersen: Very few people fly fishing. Try the East walker Cannel and Rainbow Point. Large red midges and brown midges Hatching mid Morning. Water temps are running in the low 60s depending on what part of the lake your on. There are some small Browns that have just been planted from the Bridgeport Fish Enhancement Program handle with care and you could see some monsters in a few years. Water is a little dirty on the south end. Fish are keying on the Callibaetis, Midges and perch fry. locating the pods of fish is the ticket. Our watery friends are holding 3-15 feet of water. Lake has dropped to near winter levels. Witnessed fish feeding on the surface from 9:00am on. If you're looking to heave it and leave it (Midge fishing as some call it ) use your Crowley tactics, as both the Midging and striping will produce. Talk to Eric up at Paradise Shores RV Park and check out the Brown Trout Fry for the Bridgeport Fish Enhancement Program. You can make a donation to help with the care of these young monsters. Call 769-932-7735.
High temps in the 50's and lows in the 20's or colder. Winter is quickly approaching and the fishing season is winding down. Be prepared for snow and dangerous weather any time.
The last day of fishing season is Nov. 15

Nymphing Patterns: Black/copper wire, brown midge patterns are working the best. around the 5-10 ft depth. Try Hares ears and PTs patterns as the calibaetis have been hatching

Dry Fly Patterns: Adult Midge Some Large(red) ones and Calibaetis Patterns to rising fish.

Attractor Patterns:

Streamer: Large brown or Black leech patterns or hornbergs with a Hares Ear dropper.

Volcom Peanut Butter & Rail Jam


The Peanut Butter and Rail Jam is Volcom Stone's amateur snowboard series. Instead of having three rails in a row, we put them side by side to create a rail zone. The contest runs in a jam format. When we say jam, we mean a real jam where there are no rules. You can hit the rails as many times as you want, and in whatever order you choose. There are two divisions, 16 and under and 17 and over. The event is absolutely free, on a first come first serve basis, and boasts tons of free prizes and food. Everyone who enters walks away with something, so show up early to ensure you get in and enjoy a great day of snow and heaping peanut butter and jam sandwiches. Registration will be located on the 3rd floor of the Main Lodge.
