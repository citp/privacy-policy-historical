MPI Privacy Policy

Commitment to Privacy

The Migration Policy Institute (MPI) respects the privacy of every individual who uses this website. This privacy policy outlines the information MPI will collect and how we will use that information. By using our website, you signify that you agree with the terms of our current privacy policy as posted in this area of the website. If you do not agree with any term in the privacy policy, please do not use this website.

Personally Identifiable Information

When you visit www.migrationpolicy.org, we will not collect any personally identifiable information about you (such as your name, address, telephone number, or e-mail address) unless you provide it to us voluntarily.

Some of our interactive pages may invite visitors to submit names or e-mail addresses in order to join a mailing list or submit comments. If you opt not to provide us with this information, no material consequences will result, although you may be unable to receive certain information sent out electronically by MPI. Parties wishing to join the mailing list or submit a comment or question are required to provide MPI with a variety of necessary and personally identifiable information, such as name, address, phone number, and e-mail address.

Use of Personally Identifiable Information

MPI may store and process your personally identifiable information to better understand your needs and improve our website, and we may use your personally identifiable information to contact you. For example, at your request, we may send you event invitations, policy briefs, news updates, or other notices. In addition, we may use your information in order to respond to your comments or requests for information.

MPI does not sell, rent, loan, trade, or lease to any outside entity any of your personally identifiable information collected at our website. However, nothing in the privacy policy shall be deemed to prohibit MPI from disclosing your identity if MPI is required to do so by law.

Security

We will take reasonable steps to keep all information as secure as possible against unauthorized access and use. All information will be protected by our security measures, which are periodically reviewed.

Non-Personally Identifiable Information Collected Automatically

In some cases, we may collect information about you that is not personally identifiable. We may use computer technology in order to broadly track your movement on this website. This information tells us how you use this website and it allows us to tailor our website to better meet your needs. MPI makes no attempt to link this non-personal information with the individuals who browse this site.

Terms of Use

While we have worked diligently to provide you with accurate and updated information on this website, we cannot be absolutely certain that everything on this website is free of errors or omissions. Therefore, we will not be liable for any direct, indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages arising out of your access to or use of this website. Under no circumstance will MPI be liable for any loss or damage caused by your reliance on information obtained through this website. It is your responsibility to evaluate the accuracy, completeness, or usefulness of any content available through this website.

Links to Other Websites

Our website may contain other links to other websites for your convenience in locating related information and services. We are not responsible for and do not maintain these other sites, and have no control over the content, accuracy, or opinions expressed in such websites. Furthermore, MPI has not investigated, monitored, or checked the accuracy or completeness of any such website. Please note that the inclusion of any linked website does not imply approval or endorsement by MPI of the linked website.

Accordingly, MPI expressly disclaims any responsibility for the content of these other websites, the accuracy of the information on these websites, or the quality of products or services provided by the organizations that maintain them. The existence of such links should not be construed as a recommendation, endorsement, approval, or verification by MPI of any content available on third-party sites. MPI reserves the right to require written consent for, or request the removal of, any links to our website.

No Liability for Acts of Third Parties

Although MPI will use reasonable efforts to safeguard the confidentiality of your personally identifiable information, transmissions made by means of the Internet cannot be made absolutely secure. MPI will have no liability for disclosure of information due to errors in transmission or unauthorized acts of third parties. In addition, MPI will release specific information about you to comply with any valid legal process such as a search warrant, subpoena, statute, or court order. We also will release specific information in special cases, such as if there is an attempted breach of security or a physical threat to you or others.

Contacting Us Regarding Your Personally Identifiable Information

If you have submitted personally identifiable information through this website, or if someone else has submitted your personally identifiable information through this website, and you would like to have that information deleted from our database, please contact us at the below address. We then will use reasonable efforts to remove your personally identifiable information from our files, unless we are entitled to retain it pursuant to an agreement that you have entered into with MPI. If you have any questions about this privacy policy, please feel free to contact us using the information below.

Because of changes in Internet technology and practices, this privacy policy may change from time to time. By using the website after MPI posts any changes to this privacy policy, you agree to accept those changes, whether or not you have reviewed them, and such acceptance shall be deemed legally conclusive.

Acceptance of Terms

By using this website, you have agreed to and accepted MPI's privacy policy. If you do not agree to this policy, please do not use this website.

In the Spotlight

Immigration Data Matters

This easy-to-use online guide compiles some of the most credible governmental and authoritative nongovernmental data sources pertaining to immigrants and immigration in the United States and internationally. The guide directly links users to more than 220 data sources, organized by topic and region, and offers tips on how to access more complicated datasets.

Get Started

Asylum Recognition Rates in the EU/EFTA by Country

This map illustrates asylum recognition rates in individual European Union Member States and European Free Trade Association (EFTA) countries (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, and Switzerland) by country of origin and year, from 2008 through 2016. This innovative tool also shows the total number of decisions as well as the type of protection status granted.

Get Started

The DACA Program and its Recipients

The Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program was begun in 2012 to provide a temporary reprieve from deportation to qualified unauthorized immigrants brought to the United States as children. The research and data resources collected here offer a detailed demographic portrait of this population, as well as legislative scenarios to extend protections to these DREAMers now that DACA is slated for termination.

Read More

Frequently Requested Statistics on Immigrants and Immigration in the United States

Immigration to the United States is the subject of significant debate, with questions ranging from the size of inflows, the role of immigrants in the labor market, and the nature of enforcement, to humanitarian admission policies and the sociodemographic characteristics of new arrivals. As such, it is important for the public to have access to accurate current and historical information. This article offers the top statistics on immigrants and immigration to the United States.

Use this data tool—referred to as “one addictive interactive map”—to examine immigrant populations by country of origin and destination. Find out how many Americans live in Mexico, how many Ukrainians in Russia, or Filipinos in Saudi Arabia, for example.
