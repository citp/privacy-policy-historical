The technologies used on the Sites and Services to collect Usage Information include:

_Cookies and Flash Cookies_ -Cookies and Flash Cookies are small data files (text or alphanumeric strings) placed on a computer, mobile device, browser, application, technology or other device (collectively, 'System') when it is used to visit the Sites or access content that uses the Services. Cookies are also used as part of the promotion request and we may use Cookies to collect and store certain information about your interactions with our Sites or content that uses the Services such as the number of prints and shares. Cookies also allow us to provide you with additional promotions that are most likely to appeal to you based on your use of the Sites and Services. We may use session cookies and persistent cookies. Persistent cookies remain after you close your browser. You always have the option to [disable our cookies using standard methods for your browser](https://www.google.com/#q=how+to+clear+browser+cookies). To personalize your experience, we may utilize third-party technologies, such as plug-ins, that use local shared objects, also known as Flash Cookies, to store your preferences or to display customized content based upon what you view on our Sites, including certain types of display advertising. Flash Cookies are different from browser cookies because of the amount of, type of, and how data is stored. Cookie management tools provided by your browser may not remove Flash Cookies. To learn how to manage privacy and storage settings for Flash Cookies, please visit this website: <http://www.macromedia.com/support/documentation/en/flashplayer/help/settings_manager.html#117118>. Please be aware that disabling these functions may negatively impact your use and enjoyment of the Sites and Services, including preventing you from viewing and/or printing a promotion.

_Pixels and Web Beacons_ \- We may also include small graphic images or other web programming code called web beacons (also known as Internet tags, pixel tags, tracking beacons and clear GIFs) on the Sites (including in promotions using the Services), which we may use to collect information regarding your interaction with content on the Sites. Pixels and Web Beacons may also be placed by third parties on content on the Sites. This privacy policy does not govern the use of such third party Pixels and Web Beacons as they are not in our control.

_Referral Header_ \- A Referral Header is a piece of data that identifies the address of the webpage that linked to the Sites being requested. We may utilize a Referral Header to understand where the request for the Sites originated.

_Unique Identifiers_ \- A Unique Identifier is a number that is automatically assigned to your System when you access a web site or its servers, and our systems may identify your System by its Unique Identifier. A Unique Identifier may include your IP address or other unique identifier.

_Executable File_ \- An Executable File is a downloadable software application file that contains instructions that perform tasks on a computer. We may use an Executable File to control the print limits of a promotion.

_Java Applet_ \- A Java Applet is a downloadable software file that contains instructions that perform tasks on a computer initiated by your web browser. We may use a Java Applet to control the print limits of a promotion.
