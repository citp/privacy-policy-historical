# remine · Real Estate Intelligence

## Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 2016

This Privacy Policy applies to the information that Remine, LLC (the “ _Company_ ,” “ _we_ ,” or “ _us_ ” or “ _our_ ”) collects or receives through its website http://www.remine.com (“ _Website_ ”), and from our interactions through [telephone, correspondence, electronic communications, newsletters, interviews, surveys, questionnaires, and] the services (“ _Services_ ”) that we provide through our web-based application (including all related documentation, the “ _Application_ ”). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share and protect the personal information of Authorized Users and visitors (each defined below), and outlines the choices of Authorized Users and visitors regarding the collection and use of their personal information.

This Privacy Policy is part of, and is governed by, the terms and conditions set forth in our Terms of Use Agreement located at <http://legal.remine.com/terms>.

### 1 Consent to Privacy Policy

In this Privacy Policy, we describe the information we collect when an individual (“visitor”, “you” or “your”) uses or accesses our Website or Application, or otherwise engages with the Company. By accessing or using the Website or Application, you consent to the information collection, disclosure and use practices described in this Privacy Policy. Therefore, please read this Privacy Policy carefully.

**> > If you do not agree with the terms of our Privacy Policy, you are not authorized to use the Application or Services.**

### 2 Information We Collect

2.1 We collect information from a visitor when she or he accesses or interacts with the Website or Application. We collect both personal information and non-personal information depending on the level of interaction of a visitor with the Website or Application. The manner in which this information is collected falls into two categories: (i) information that a visitor provides directly; and (ii) information that is automatically collected through a visitor’s use of the Website or Application.

2.2 Information you provide to us directly:

a. _Registration Information:_ We collect the information that a visitor provides to us when activating an account. This includes identifying information about a visitor, such as name, password, [mailing address, phone number,] and email address. A visitor who has activated their account and who has used a password to access the Application is deemed an “ _Authorized User_ ”. All provisions of this Privacy Policy that apply to a “visitor” also apply to an “Authorized User”. Some provisions apply only to Authorized Users.

b. _Payment Information:_ The Website provides the ability to make online payments for access to certain Services. However, we do not directly access, collect, or store your credit card information. All credit card information and other customer details required to process credit card payments are collected directly by our service provider that processes online payments on our behalf. Our service provider processes this information in line with its own privacy policy and data handling practices. We are not responsible for the handling of this payment information by the service provider.

c. _Electronic Communications:_ We may collect information that a visitor or Authorized User chooses to share with us via electronic communications, such as support or administrative requests. We use this information to understand and respond to a visitor’s questions and to improve our processes and customer experience.

d. _Surveys and Questionnaires:_ We may invite visitors and Authorized Users to participate in surveys or to respond to a particular questionnaire. Participation in such surveys and questionnaires is voluntary, but may require that a visitor provide certain personal information, such as name, email address, or zip code. We will use this information for analytics purposes. 

2.3 Information automatically collected through your use of the Service:

We automatically collect information from visitors who use the Website or Application in order to understand how the Website or Application is used, and how to improve them. The information we automatically collect through the Website or Application includes:

a. _In general:_ We record a visitor’s and Authorized Users IP address, the time of the visit, and information regarding the browser and operating system of the device used. We collect certain information about a the use of the Website, including actions taken on the Website, webpages visited, and time spent on specific webpages, and the like.

b. _Mobile Device Users:_ If a mobile device is used to access the Website or Application, we may collect, in addition to the general information above, the type of mobile device used and other technical information about the mobile device (e.g., the manufacturer of the mobile device, its location, and etc.). 

2.4 Technology used to collect information through your use of the Service:

The technology we use to collect information automatically from visitors and Authorized Users includes the following:

a. _Cookies:_ The information we collect using cookies allows us to monitor how many visitors are using our Website or Application and for what purposes. Cookies are also used to facilitate an Authorized User's log-in, as navigation aids, and as session timers. Cookies do not retain personal information about a visitor but do retain certain information about an Authorized User, such as to allow navigation from page to page in a restricted access area of the Website.

You may choose to block certain categories of cookies. Most browsers contain settings that allow you to control or delete cookies. These settings will typically be found in the “options” or “preferences” menu of your browser. Please note that blocking or disabling certain cookies may interfere with certain functionalities of our Service. Further, while disabling a cookie may prevent the future collection of information by that cookie, it does not prevent the use of the information collected prior to the cookie being disabled. For more general information about cookies you can visit http://www.allaboutcookies.org.

b. _Embedded Scripts:_ Embedded scripts are used to collect information about a visitor’s and Authorized User’s interactions with the Website and Application, such as the links clicked. Embedded scripts are temporarily downloaded onto a visitor’s or Authorized User’s computer or mobile device and are deactivated or deleted when a visitor or Authorized User disconnects from the Website.

c. _Third-Parties' Content and Widgets:_ Our Website may include third-party widgets and other content from third-parties. These third-parties may use their own cookies or web beacons to track the actions of visitors online over time and across different websites or platforms to deliver targeted electronic advertisements to a visitor. Please note that the Company does not have access to or control over the cookies and/or web beacons that third-parties may use. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these third-parties. For more information about how tracking works for online advertising purposes you can visit http://www.aboutads.info/choices. In addition, some third-party advertising companies may provide a mechanism to opt-out of their tracking technologies. For more information about this opt-out process, you can visit the Network Advertising Initiative website, available at http://www.networkadvertising.org/choices/.

### 3 How We Use Information

In addition to some of the specific uses that we describe in this Privacy Policy, we may use the information we have collected to:

a. Facilitate the use of the Website or Application;

b. Improve, test, and monitor the effectiveness of the Website or Application;

c. Develop Website or Application features that may be of interest;

d. Send electronic communications about the Service or to respond to inquiries or requests for information;

e. Maintain a record of contact information and correspondence;

f. Optimize our marketing efforts, for example to compile statistics regarding the use of the Website or Application, to identify usage trends, and to determine the effectiveness of our promotional campaigns;

g. Perform data analysis;

h. Conduct audits, and fraud monitoring and prevention;

i. Conduct other functions as otherwise described at the time when the information is collected; and

j. Perform any other function that we believe in good faith is necessary to protect the security or proper functioning of the Website or Application.

### 4 Sharing and Disclosure of Information

We may share certain information with third-parties in the following circumstances:

4.1 Employees, Third-Party Processors and Third-Party Service Providers: We will share information with our employees, contractors, affiliates, distributors, dealers, vendors, and suppliers (“Service Providers”) who provide certain services to us or on our behalf, such as operating and supporting the Website or Application, conducting data analytics, providing customer service, or performing marketing, or consulting services. These Service Providers will only have access to the information needed to perform the functions that we have requested them to carry out on our behalf.

4.2 In Connection with Business Transfers or Bankruptcy: We may disclose or transfer information to a third-party in the case of a sale, merger, joint venture, assignment, transfer or other disposition of all or any portion of our business, assets or stocks, corporate restructuring or bankruptcy. This transfer or disclosure may occur both as part of these transactions, and as part of any due diligence process.

4.3 Responding to Legal Requests and Preventing Harm: There may be times when we use, share, or disclose information (a) if required to do so by law, or in response to a search warrant, subpoena, or a court order and we believe that such request is facially valid; (b) if we believe that disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect against suspected illegal or fraudulent activity, to protect the safety, property and other rights of the Company, our service providers, other visitors or Authorized Users or the public; and/or (c) to enforce our Legal Terms, or to defend against legal claims, or to allow us to pursue available remedies or limit the damages that we may sustain.

4.4 Aggregate Information _:_ We may share, aggregate information relating to visitors or Authorized Users with third-parties for research, analytical, or strategic purposes.

### 5 Information Storage and Retention

We will retain information about visitors and Authorized Users as needed to provide the Service, or as necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes and enforce our agreements unless a longer retention period is required or permitted under applicable law.

### 6 Information Security

We use reasonable technical, organizational and administrative measures to protect the information we collect and store against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction and/or damage. We believe that these measures are reasonably adapted to the nature of the information in our custody. However, as no method of transmission over the Internet, or method of electronic storage is 100% effective, we cannot guarantee and do no warrant the security of any information we may have collected.

7.2 By accessing and using this Website and/or the Application you consent to the collection and use of your information, as described in this Privacy Policy, outside of your country of residence, including the transfer of your information across international boundaries.

**> >If you do not want your information to be transferred to, or processed, or maintained outside your country of residence, you should not access or use the Website and/or Service.**

11.1 We will not share any personal information about you with outside third-parties for their direct marketing purposes to the extent prohibited by California law, unless you agree otherwise as authorized by your prior written consent.

11.2 Do Not Track: Browsers provide the ability to inform operators of online services that a visitor does not want certain information about his/her online activities to be collected over time or across websites or online services. However, the Internet industry has not yet agreed on a definition of what “do not track” means, on how compliance with “do not track” would be measured or evaluated or on a common approach to responding to a “do not track” signal. Consequently, due to this lack of standardization, we have not yet developed features that would recognize or respond to browser-initiated “do not track” signals. To find out more about “do not track”, please visit http://www.allaboutdnt.com.
