We are serious about protecting your privacy and recognize that you care how information about you is used and shared. From time to time, Onvia may change its privacy practices because of changes in relevant and applicable legal or regulatory requirements, the business or business practices of Onvia, or in its attempts to better serve your needs. Notice of such changes to our privacy practices will be given and a revised Privacy Policy will be posted at this location.

**What Information Do We Collect?**

The information collected by Onvia is not ordinarily acquired in a form or manner that permits the identification of an individual's Personal Information by Onvia. Notwithstanding the above, Personal Information may be supplied to Onvia by our clients' employees or representatives and Registered Users. Onvia collects various types of information from our Registered Users. During the registration process, Registered Users supply us with basic contact information (such as name, address, telephone number, e-mail address and company name), as well as billing information. This information is generally entered into fields in the registration and subscription forms and enables the Registered User to take full advantage of the products and services that we offer. Should you purchase any Onvia products or services on or through the Site, the information that you supplied during the registration process may be used to track details about those purchases. "Personal Information" means any information or set of information that identifies or could be used by Onvia, its customers or agents of its customers, to identify an individual. Personal Information does not include information that is encoded or anonymized, or publicly available information that has not been combined with non-public personal information. "Sensitive Personal Information" means personal information that reveals race, ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, or trade union membership, or that concerns health or sex life. Onvia does not require that Registered Users supply Sensitive Personal Information as part of the registration process or in any registration or subscription form. Onvia will treat as Sensitive Personal Information any information received from a third party where that third party treats and identifies the information as sensitive.

As with many other Web sites, the Web servers used to operate the Onvia Site collect certain data pertaining to those that access the Site and the equipment and communications method that are used to access the Internet and the Site. Without combining these data with other sources of information, they do not readily identify the individual user. They do reveal such things as the Internet protocol ("IP") address assigned to your computer, pages you accessed on the Site, or immediately prior to visiting the Site, and the length of time you spent at the Site. This information is collected to, among other things, facilitate Site operation and system administration, to generate aggregate, non-identifiable statistical information, and to improve performance of the services made available on the Site.

Onvia may also retain the content of and metadata regarding any correspondence you may have with Onvia or its customer service representatives, regardless of the mode of communication by which such correspondence was made. This information helps Onvia to improve the Site and the materials, products and services offered on the Site, and to more effectively and efficiently respond to both current and future inquiries.

Onvia uses "cookies" (small text files stored on your computer) to help track and customize your access and use of the Site. Cookies store and retain information that helps us recognize you on subsequent visits to the Site. Cookies may also store any login or ID assigned to you by Onvia and the associated password, this information is stored in encrypted form. Most popular Internet browser packages allow you to configure the browser so as not to accept cookies. However, setting the browser to reject cookies may prevent you from taking full advantage of the Site and the materials, products and services that Onvia makes available on the Site.

**How Do We Protect Your Information?**

Onvia places a high value on the corporate data and document data you entrust with us. For this reason, we secure your information at three levels: **physical, application, and network layers**. The **physical** layer is protected by need-to-know authorization by only the appropriate operations individuals. Additionally, the servers are caged within a data center only accessible through a three-factor security model. The **application** layer is entirely secured using Thawte's 128 bit SSL encryption technology so applicable communication with the web site is locked. The **network layer** is protected by industry-leading firewall technology and administered real-time to ensure timely security patching. This layer is also watched by intrusion-monitoring systems to ward off unauthorized access. These safeguards all are put in place to protect our users' information, prevent unauthorized access, safeguard user data, and ensure the proper and legal use of the Site. Onvia takes commercially reasonable measures to secure and protect customer-specific information transmitted via or stored on our Site. However, no security system is impenetrable. We cannot guarantee that information user's supply will be totally secure.
