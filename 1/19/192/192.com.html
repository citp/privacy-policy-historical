Privacy Policy

About 192.com

192.com has been providing online directory services since 1997. We are committed to protecting the privacy of our users by keeping all personal information that we collect secure and by only using such data for the purpose(s) for which it was collected and for which we have your consent. While our objective is to provide the most comprehensive UK directory of people and businesses we also respect the choice of people to be removed from particular published databases. We are registered with the Office of the Information Commissioner under the Data Protection Act 1998.

The Free Directory Enquiry Database

This is the national directory enquiry database maintained by BT. Your telephone provider licenses your data to BT who then sublicense it to all directory enquiry providers. If you wish to amend or remove your data from this database you need to contact your Telecom Provider who can then update your information with BT, and BT will then distribute the corrected information to all Directory Enquiry providers. If you are concerned to prevent marketing calls the best way to do this is to sign up to the Telephone Preference Service rather than going x-directory.

Electoral Roll

There are two versions of the Electoral Roll. The Full Electoral Roll can only be used for electoral and certain other restricted purposes. The Edited Electoral Roll can be used for a variety of other purposes including directories such as 192.com. Everyone has the right to opt-out of the Edited Electoral Register. 192.com publishes Electoral Roll data from 2002 through to the current Edited Electoral Roll. We obtain this data from a credit reference agency. If you wish to opt-out of the Edited Electoral Roll you can contact your local council who will advise how to do it. The Electoral Roll is used by companies for a variety of different purposes from identity checking to direct marketing. We would recommend only coming off the Electoral Roll if you have a specific need to. If you are concerned about preventing junk mail the best way to do this is to sign up to the Mail Preference Service rather than opting-out of the Edited Electoral Roll.

Directors Database

The Directors Database is a public database collated by Companies House. Only under very unusual circumstances will Companies House allow a director’s details to be made anonymous. Should you wish to investigate this possibility please contact Companies House on +44 0845 3032 400.

Information Collected With Registration

If you register with 192.com we will ask you to provide a small amount of personal information including, but not limited to, your name, address and email address. This information helps us to ensure that your account is protected and allows us to display private information to you about your account. Your e-mail address is used as your 192.com username, but it also provides us with a means of contacting you about your account, about the service we offer and to promote our products and services to you.

Personally Identifiable Information

We do not share personally identifiable Information with Third Parties.

Choice/Opt-out
We provide you with the opportunity to ‘opt-out’ of having your personally identifiable information used for certain purposes, when we ask for this information. For example, if you purchase a product/ser vice but do not wish to receive any additional marketing material from us, you can indicate your preference during the purchase process.

Reunite Stories
When an individual supplies any personal information for use in our reunited stories we are obligated to make you aware that this PII could be read, collected or used by others.

The 192.com Newsletter

When you register with 192.com we will seek your consent to send you our monthly email newsletter through which we will keep you informed about our service and provide offers and promotions. If you do not wish to receive our newsletter, you can unsubscribe at any time. If you no longer wish to receive promotional communications you may opt-out of receiving them by following the instructions in each email communication or by contacting us. Alternatively you can unsubscribe by going to ‘Your Account’, and selecting ‘change e-mail preferences’. Or you can write to: Customer Service, 192.com Ltd. Units 8-10 Quayside Lodge, William Morris Way, London, SW6 2UZ to opt-out.

Access to Personally identifiable Information

If your personally identifiable information changes, or if you no longer desire our service, you may correct, update or delete it by making the change on our member information page, ‘Your Account’, or contact us. Alternatively you can write to us at the above address.

You will be notified when your personal information is collected by any third party that is not our agent/service provider, so you can make an informed choice as to whether or not to share your information with that third party.

Supplementary Information

We may also collect supplementary information about you from other sources such as public records or information obtained by other companies and legitimately supplied to 192.com.

Does anyone else have access to my information?

Except where detailed elsewhere in this policy 192.com does not disclose your personal information to any other person or organization except when we may disclose personal information to our employees and companies acting as data processors on our behalf. In addition we may disclose information if required to do so by law or in the good faith that such disclosure is necessary to (i) comply with the legal process, (ii) respond to claims of a violation of the rights of third parties or (iii) protect the rights, property or safety of 192.com, our users or the public.

Information that we collect about your usage of the service

We keep automatic logs of usage by all users (whether registered or not) including the IP (Internet Protocol) address from which a user session was conducted, the date and time of each search made and the results provided in response to a search. We may use this information to assist in improving the nature and quality of the service we offer; we may, if requested by data subjects, provide them with details of searches conducted on them; and we also reserve the right to pass on such logs to the police in the event that it might assist with an investigation. We do link this automatically-collected data to personally identifiable information.

Cookies

Most web sites automatically recall information about their users through the use of cookies. These are small pieces of information that we will deposit on your computers hard drive when you use 192.com. Each time you return to 192.com this information will be automatically sent back to our servers enabling us to identify the computer that is connected to the site and to recall your personal settings for use of our site. Cookies do not contain personal information.

Some of our business partners (e.g. advertisers) use cookies on our site. We have no access to or control over these cookies. See Third Party Advertisers below.

This privacy statement covers the use of cookies by 192.com only and does not cover the use of cookies by advertisers.

Data Security

The security of your personal information is important to us. When you enter sensitive information (such as a credit card number) on our registration or order forms, we encrypt that information using secure socket layer technology (SSL).

Personal Information that you provide is stored on secure servers compliant with the strict procedures required by the Data Protection Act 1998. These cover the storage and disclosure of information you provide and the prevention of unauthorized access. Credit card transactions are all encrypted before transactions take place to protect your personal details.

We follow generally accepted industry standards to protect the personal information submitted to us both during transmission and once we receive it. No method of transmission over the internet, or method of electronic storage, is 100% secure however. Therefore, while we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

To ensure that your credit, debit or charge card is not being used without your consent, we will validate name, address and other personal information supplied by you during the order process against appropriate third party databases. By accepting these terms and conditions you consent to such checks being made. In performing these checks personal information provided by you may be disclosed to a registered Credit Reference Agency which may keep a record of that information. You can rest assured that this is done only to confirm your identity, that a credit check is not performed and that your credit rating will be unaffected. All information provided by you will be treated strictly in accordance with the Data Protection Act 1998.

If you have any questions about security on our web site, please use our contact form.
