Privacy Statement for www.kaiserpermanente.org
Kaiser Permanente has created this privacy statement in order to demonstrate our firm commitment to privacy. The following discloses our information gathering and dissemination practices for this Web site: www.kaiserpermanente.org.

Site Visitorship Data
Kaiser Permanente routinely gathers data on Web site activity, such as how many people visit the site, the pages they visit, where they come from, how long they stay, etc. The data is collected on an aggregate, anonymous basis, which means no personally identifiable information is associated with the data. This data helps us improve site content and overall usage. This information is not shared with other organizations.

Collecting and Using Personal Information
Kaiser Permanente only collects personally identifiable information from site visitors, when site visitors voluntarily and explicitly provide it (see the section below titled "About Cookies for California Small Business and Personal Advantage" for the exception). This information is collected when site visitors use our Email Us form, Class Registration form and Physician Selection for, and online surveys. This information is used to determine what the issues are, enable us to contact our members/prospective members and service them in an expedient manner.

Our online surveys only ask visitors for demographic information, and will not request that you provide medical information. The online survey asks for information on gender, age, education, location, ethnicity, employment, health insurance coverage and health plan, and Web use. No personally identifiable information is collected on the online survey. The purpose of the survey is to track a variety of data for research and development purposes. (This includes research that helps Kaiser Permanente to improve information and services offered through the Kaiser Permanente Web site.)

Visitors may request a subscription to our Partners in Health Topics. Users may opt-out of receiving future mailings.

About Cookies for California Small Business and Personal Advantage
When Web site users visit our California Small Business and California Personal Advantage sections, we may place Internet "cookies" on their computer hard drives. The cookie consists of a unique identifier that does not contain information about you or your health history. When a visitor who has one of our cookies on his or her computer revisits our site, we are able to recognize that computer. This information in aggregate will help us to tailor our site to be more helpful and efficient for our visitors. They also help us measure the success of our marketing campaigns by allowing us to analyze the activities of visitors to our site.

You may have software on your computer that will allow you to decline or deactivate Internet cookies, but if you do so, some features of this site may not work properly for you. For instructions on how to remove cookies from your hard drive, go to your browser's Web site for detailed instructions. In addition, further information regarding cookies may be available on other Web sites or from your Internet service provider. Here are the names of two browsers: Netscape and Internet Explorer.
