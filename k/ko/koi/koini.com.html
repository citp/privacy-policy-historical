KOINI PRIVACY POLICY



I. Our Privacy Philosophy


Annes Diary, Inc. ("Annes Diary") is committed to protecting your and your children's online privacy on our websites and services.  We created the Koini children's online safety platform and its related social networks, including, without limitation, the Mammasandpappas.com, Annesdiary.com, whatssupkids.com and tomsscrapbook.com websites (collectively, as we may add websites from time to time, "Our Websites") to bring you and your children a more secure interactive on-line experience.  We therefore take your privacy very seriously and strive to only collect, handle and secure your and your children's personal information appropriately and in accordance with applicable law, including the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998, a United States law that gives you as parents control over what information is collected online from your children aged 13 and under and how such information is used, maintained and disclosed to third parties (we'll refer to children aged 13 and under in this Privacy Policy as your "COPPA Kids"), Canadian federal and provincial privacy legislation and all other applicable law.

We urge you to monitor your children's activities online and to discuss with your children appropriate restrictions relating to the online communication of personal information. Please take a moment to review the following resources to learn about how to appropriately safeguard your and your children's privacy when surfing the Internet:

Visit www.ftc.gov/privacy

for information from the Federal Trade Commission about protecting children's privacy online.Visit www.OnGuardOnline.gov for social networking tips for parents and youth

Visit www.wiredsafety.org for animations and printable educational materials relating to a wide variety of Internet risks and safety issues concerning tweens and teens.

This Privacy Policy describes the information that we may collect on Our Websites, the purposes for which the information collected is used, the rights you have regarding personal information collected from you and your children, and to whom and under what circumstances we may disclose your and your children's personal information. If at any time you have a question about anything contained in this Privacy Policy, please contact us here. 

II. What This Privacy Policy Covers


This Privacy Policy applies only to personally identifiable information ("PII") that Annes Diary collects from you and your children on or through Our Websites and describes how we collect, maintain and use this PII.  For the purpose of this Privacy Policy, PII means any individually identifiable information about you or your children collected online that can identify or be used to contact, or locate you or your children, including, without limitation: 
(a) your or your children's first and last name;
(b) a home or other physical address for you or your children (including street name and name of a city or town);
(c) your or your children's e-mail address or other online contact information, including, without limitation, an instant messaging user identifier or a screen name that reveals an individual's e-mail address or other PII;
(d) your or your children's telephone number;
(e) your or your children's Social Security number;
(f) a persistent identifier (such as a customer number) relating to you or your children held in a "cookie" (explained in more detail below) where such identifier is associated with PII;
(g) a combination of your  or your children's last name or a photograph with other information such that the combination permits physical or online contacting; or
(h) non-personally identifiable information ("NPI") concerning you or your children that we collect online when it is combined with your or your children's PII.
For the purpose of this Agreement, NPI might include certain data that does not by itself individually identify you or your children â€“ for example, web usage information (e.g., browser type) and demographic information (e.g., age range, gender, certain preferences, etc.).
When we refer to "aggregate information" in this Privacy Policy, we mean NPI about you and your children that is combined with other individuals information for statistical evaluation.
Please note that this policy does not apply to the practices of companies that we do not own or control, or to people that we do not employ or manage.

We do not collect PII from casual visitors to publicly available pages of Our Websites unless such visitors contact us. When visitors do contact us, we collect and store their communications in order to respond to their requests and may use their PII again (except in the case of your COPPA Kids, where we must obtain your consent) to send newsletters and/or promotional messages, unless they choose to opt out of receiving any further correspondence from us by following the opt-out instructions contained in each communication.

Only you as a parent or legal guardian may register your COPPA Kids for membership subscriptions on Our Websites and/or authorize the collection of PII from them in connection with games, activities, contests, surveys, promotions, etc. in which they may wish to participate on Our Websites.

B. What information we do collect

In order to access the enhanced subscription services and community features offered by Our Websites, you must first register with us and create an account for you and for at least one of your children. When you register, we will ask you for certain of your PII including, without limitation, your full name, mailing address, e-mail address, phone number, birth date and credit card number and/or other payment information. As part of the registration process, we may also ask you for certain NPI, including, without limitation, your communication and marketing preferences.
We will also ask for PII about the children you wish to have listed on your account, including, without limitation, their full name, and certain NPI, such as their age and gender in order to better personalize our service. Also, in order for your children to participate in certain interactive community features of Our Websites such as instant messaging, we require a "digitized fingerprint" of each child so that when they access Our Websites we can authenticate them as one of our child members. This enables us to better verify that persons logging into any of Our Websites are, in fact, who they say they are. When a child's fingerprint is submitted, we immediately convert a template image of it representing certain points on the fingerprint (not a picture, copy or scan) into an encrypted numerical code that is associated with that child's account (the fingerprint template itself being immediately deleted). We do not store any copies, pictures or scans of your children's fingerprints. The encrypted numerical code into which a child's fingerprint is converted cannot be reverse engineered back into a copy or scan of your children's fingerprint. At the time you register your children for an account with us, you will need to create a user name and password for each child. PLEASE DO NOT USE ANY PII IN OR AS YOUR CHILDREN'S USER NAMES AS THIS INFORMATION WILL BE VISIBLE TO OTHERS ON OUR WEBSITES.

We may also collect PII from you and your children (including your COPPA Kids, subject to obtaining your verifiable consent) when you or your children use Our Websites to interact with others in the community (e.g. by posting comments, photos and/or video) and when you or your children participate in certain promotions, contests, surveys or sweepstakes where collection of such information is reasonably required in order to participate in such activities, or if you or your children send us an e-mail. D. Information We Collect Through "Cookies" and "Clear GIFs"

We use so-called "cookies" and "clear GIFs" to enhance your experience on our websites. These cookies and clear GIFs provide us with statistical and usage data that allows us to remember your preferences and customizations, personalize and update our content and improve navigation of our websites.

A cookie is a small information file that we place on your computer to help us to keep track of your visits to our websites and to remember your activities. For instance, we use cookies to remember user customizations and user screen names to avoid the need for multiple log-ins. We do not store any PII into our cookies nor do we link or combine information collected through cookies to any PII that you or your children submit to us when using Our Websites.

A clear GIF (also known as a "web beacon" or "web bug") is an electronic file placed on a web page, usually in the form of a transparent one pixel by one pixel GIF (Graphics Interchange Format file, a common file format for images used on the internet) that allows our websites to collect anonymous and aggregate metrics such as counting users who have visited certain pages, advertising responses or to deliver or access our cookies.

You can configure your browser to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify you when a cookie is set (each browser is different, so check the "Help" menu of your browser to learn how to change your cookie preferences). However, certain areas of Our Websites can only be accessed in conjunction with cookies and you should be aware that disabling cookies might prevent you from using some or all of our features and content.  Third party companies other than Annes Diary may show advertisements on Our Websites.  If you or your children click on a third party advertisement, the third party may set and access its own cookies on your computer. Please be aware that this Privacy Policy does NOT cover these other companies' cookies, which are subject to their own privacy policies.  Advertisers do not have access to our cookies.  For more information on third party ad servers, please see http://www.networkadvertising.org and the section entitled "Collection of Information by Third Party Sites and Sponsors."

VII. Children's Privacy

Enabling parents to understand and control the scope of their children's activities on the Internet is why we created Koini.  Your account dashboard is where you can create, review, update or delete your children's profiles, review and approve friends they can communicate with and the websites they will be permitted to visit.
Social networking websites typically encourage a great deal of personal information to be shared and exchanged so it is important that parents and children understand the risks inherent in sharing this information online.  We encourage parents to learn about and to discuss online safety with their children and to advise them never to share their PII online in screen names, blogs, on message boards, in IM, chat rooms or otherwise
We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). COPPA is a United States law aimed at protecting the privacy of your COPPA Kids (those aged 13 and under) online.  Generally, COPPA requires us to:

Post a privacy policy on the homepage of our Websites and link to the privacy policy on every page where PII is collected from or about COPPA Kids.
Provide notice about Our Websites' information collection practices to you and obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting, using or disclosing PII from your COPPA Kids.
Give you a choice as to whether your child's PII will be disclosed to third parties.
Provide you access to your children's PII and the opportunity to request deletion of such PII and opt-out of future collection or use of the information.
Not condition a child's participation in a game, contest or other activity on your children's disclosing more PII than is reasonably necessary to participate in that activity.
Maintain the confidentiality, security and integrity of PII collected from children.

We collect and maintain PII about your COPPA Kids only from you, in connection with your registration of that child with us and/or from your COPPA Kids with your verifiable consent (unless previously obtained) in connection with other activities on our websites when reasonably necessary for participation in such activities.

We may collect your COPPA Kids' name and your and your children's online contact (e.g. email address) information without your prior consent under the following circumstances:

We may collect your COPPA Kids' name and your or your children's online contact information for the sole purpose of obtaining your consent or providing you with notice of our Privacy Policy, which information we will delete if we do not obtain your consent.
We may collect your COPPA Kids' online contact information to be used for the sole purpose of responding directly on a one-time basis to a specific request from the child, after which we will delete this information.
We may collect your COPPA Kid's online contact information to be used for the sole purpose of responding directly more than once to a specific request from the child (e.g., in the case of a request for a newsletter, or to announce the winner of a contest). In this case, we will notify you immediately after our initial response to the child and before contacting the child a second time in order to provide you with the opportunity to request that we do not make any further use of the information provided by your COPPA Kid.
We may collect your COPPA Kid's name and online contact information to the extent reasonably necessary to protect the safety of the child, so long as it is not used for any other purpose, in which case we will notify you as soon as possible after the collection.
We may collect your COPPA Kid's name and online contact information, so long as not used for any other purpose, to the extent reasonably necessary to (i) protect the security or integrity of Our Websites; (ii) to take precautions against liability; (iii) to respond to judicial process; or (iv) to the extent permitted under other provisions of law, to provide information to law enforcement agencies or for an investigation on a matter related to public safety.

Each time your COPPA Kids wish to log into our websites, we collect from them their user name and password or their fingerprint scan (if you have chosen to subscribe them for membership on tomsscrapbook.com, annesdiary.com or whatssupkids.com).  We do not directly or actively collect any other PII from your COPPA Kids while they are visiting our websites.  However, please note that if your COPPA Kids participate in instant messaging, internal mail, live chatting, sending e-mails from our site, posting messages, photos or videos, etc., they may also provide others with additional information about themselves contained in their communications, including PII. Please discuss this risk with your COPPA Kids and make sure they understand the dangers of sharing PII online. 

We only share or disclose your COPPA Kids' PII to third parties in the manner described the Section entitled "How We Use Your and Your Children's PII" above.

You may, at any time, even after you have given us permission to collect and use your COPPA Kids' PII: (a) review, correct or request that your COPPA Kids' PII be deleted and/or; (b) revoke your consent to our continuing collection or use of your COPPA Kids' PII. See the section above entitled "Updating and Modifying Your Information and Removing Children from Your Account," for how to access, change, or request deletion the PII of your COPPA Kids. We reserve the right, subject to applicable law, to terminate any service provided to you and your children where you have refused to permit our further collection or use of your children's PII or have instructed us to delete your children's PII.

If we learn that the PII of your COPPA Kids has been collected without verified parental consent, we will take the appropriate steps to delete this information as soon as practicable.
