KBC WEBSITE PRIVACY POLICY

Introduction


We at KBC Bank Ireland plc (including our subsidiaries) (“KBCBI”) comply with our obligations under the Data Protection Acts, 1988 and 2003. The purpose of this Privacy Statement is to outline how KBCBI deal with any personal data you provide to us while visiting our website.

By visiting this website, you are accepting the terms of this Privacy Statement.

Types of information collected

We retain two types of information from visitors to our Website:
1. “Personal Data”: This is data that identifies you or can be used to identify or contact you and may include your name, postal address, email address, telephone number and birth date. The information you provide will be used for the purpose for which it was intended /submitted.

2. “Non-Personal Data”: Like most websites, KBCBI gather statistical and other analytical information collected on an aggregate basis of all visitors to our website. This Non-Personal Data comprises information that cannot be used to identify or contact you, such as demographic information regarding browser types and other anonymous statistical data involving the use of our Website. KBCBI collects this information to help us monitor traffic on the website and manage the website’s performance, efficiency and capacity etc. This information also helps KBCBI understand what parts of the website are effective/well used and what parts are not being fully utilised.

Telephone calls

Please note that any telephone calls to KBCBI may be recorded to ensure excellent customer service and to verify customer instructions.

Purposes for which we hold your Information.

Non-Personal Data:
(i) Non-Personal Data:
KBCBI use the Non-Personal Data gathered from visitors to our website in an aggregate form to understand the demographics of the users of our website and to help us better design and organise our website.

(ii) Cookies
The Website uses “cookie” technology (refer to definition below). KBCBI may use cookies to deliver “information” specific to your interests and to save your personal preferences so you don't have to re-enter them each time you connect to/access the “website. You are always free to decline our cookies, if your browser permits, or to ask your browser to indicate when a cookie is being sent. You can also delete cookie files from your computer at your discretion. Note that if you decline our cookies or ask for notification each time a cookie is being sent, this may affect your ease of use of the KBCBI website.

(iii) Web Browsing
By simply visiting our website you do not disclose, nor do KBCBI collect personal data on you. All that KBCBI may know about your visit may be limited to technical data. By visiting our website KBCBI may collect information regarding your visit such as
1. the top level domain name from which you access the internet (for example .ie, .com, .org, .net)
2. the previous website address from which you reached us
3. the type of web-browser you used
4. web traffic data.

This data shall be used for administrative and statistical purposes and may be shared with our internet service provider. KBCBI will use this information to help us to improve our website.

(iv) Personal Data
KBCBI will process any Personal Data you provide to us for the following purposes:
(a) to provide you with information about the services of KBCBI; and
(b) to contact you if required in connection with your account or enquiry or to respond to any communications you might send to us(c) to help us improve our services and systems.

KBCBI do not currently conclude online applications on the Website for loans, mortgages or other financial services. Any applications submitted online or by email will be subject to the same terms and conditions as those completed by hand in paper form including additional terms and conditions relating to credit checking, money laundering and regulatory notices. If any online application forms are included in the website, you will be notified of any additional specific data protection provisions at the time you complete the relevant application form.

Please note that by using this website, you are giving us your consent to process your personal data as outlined in this Privacy Statement. Your consent extends to any successor or assignee of KBCBI and/or any of its businesses.

Disclosure of Information to Third Parties

KBCBI may provide Non-Personal Data to third parties, where such information is combined with similar information of other users of KBCBI’s website. For example, KBCBI might inform third parties regarding the number of unique users who visit KBCBI’s website, the demographic breakdown of our community users of our website, or the activities that visitors to our website engage in while on our Website. The third parties to whom KBCBI may provide this information may include our website design, development and hosting contractors.

KBCBI will not disclose your Personal Data to third parties outside of the KBCBI Group unless you have consented to this disclosure or unless the disclosure is required in order to process an application or other communication from you or to perform the services you have applied for or to efficiently manage your account. KBCBI will however disclose your Personal Data if KBCBI believe in good faith that KBCBI are required to disclose it in order to comply with any applicable law, a summons, a search warrant, a court or regulatory order, or other valid legal process.

Sale of Business

KBCBI reserve the right to transfer information (including your Personal Data) to a third party in the event of a sale, merger, liquidation, receivership or transfer of all or substantially all of the assets of our company provided that the third party agrees to adhere to the terms of the Privacy Statement.

Security

Unfortunately, no data transmission over the Internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. As a result, while KBCBI will take all reasonable steps to protect your Personal Data, the nature of the Internet is such that KBCBI cannot guarantee or warrant the security of any information you transmit to us via the Internet. Any information that you do provide to KBCBI is at your own risk. KBCBI do not currently seek your bank account or credit card details on the Website or via email and you should not send us such data by email unless expressly agreed in person with an authorised individual in KBCBI.

Updating, Verifying and Deleting Personal Data

If KBCBI hold incorrect information about you which was originally submitted by you through this website, you have the right to have the data amended. Further you have the right to have any information you have sent to us via this website erased. To request your right to rectification and/or erasure please send your request to us in writing at The Compliance Department, KBC Bank Ireland plc, Sandwith Street, Dublin 2. together with:
1. Your name and address.
2. A description of the specific personal data you wish rectified.

If you request an erasure of your personal data all your data will be erased subject to the following important notice.

KBCBI are not required to rectify or erase your data where to do so would prevent you from meeting your contractual obligations to us or where KBCBI are required to process (including retaining) your personal data for a lawful purpose in accordance with the Data Protection Acts or other laws.

Right of Access

Where you have provided us with your personal data you have a right to be given a copy of your personal data in accordance with section 4 of the Data Protection Acts subject to certain exceptions. To request a copy of your personal data please visit our Access Request Procedure at www.kbcbank.ie where you can download our Access Request Form for completion before sending it to us at The Compliance Department, KBCBI plc, Sandwith Street, Dublin 2 together with the prescribed fee of €6.35. Please note the following important points:
1. we reserve the right not to process an access request that is not made in our Access Request Form format;
2. we shall not process an access request unless the prescribed fee of €6.35 is received by cheque or postal money order made payable to KBC Bank Ireland plc; and
3. we do not accept access requests via telephone, email or text message


Data Protection Policy

In addition to this Privacy Statement, you may wish to read our Data Protection Policy which is also available on our website at www.kbc.ie.

Changes to the Privacy Policy

Any changes to this Privacy Statement will be posted on the Website so you are always aware of what information KBCBI collect, how KBCBI use it, and under what circumstances, if any, KBCBI disclose it. If at any time KBCBI decide to use Personal Data in a manner significantly different from that stated in this Privacy Statement, or otherwise disclosed to you at the time it was collected, KBCBI will notify you by email, and you will have a choice as to whether or not KBCBI use your information in the new manner.

This Privacy Statement is published by KBCBI and its subsidiaries, Sandwith Street, Dublin 2, Ireland and was last updated on 27 November 2008.

Glossary of terms used

Cookies: Small pieces of information, stored in simple text files, placed on your computer by a website. Cookies can be read by the website on your subsequent visits so that you can access information in a faster and more efficient way. The information stored in a cookie may relate to your browsing habits on the web page, or a unique identification number so that the website can "remember" you on your return visit. Generally speaking, cookies do not contain personal information from which you can be identified, unless you have separately furnished such information to the website.

Personal data: means information about you which can identify you as a living individual and which is within the possession of KBCBI (e.g. your name and address)

Web browser: The piece of software you use to read web pages. Examples are Microsoft Internet Explorer, Netscape Navigator, Firefox and Opera.
