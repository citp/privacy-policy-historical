Do we use "Cookies" ?

A cookie is a small amount of data sent from our server and stored on your computer's hard drive.

Cookies and other such similar devices allow us to compile anonymous, aggregated statistics of the usage of the Website by users (i.e. you), so that we can understand how users (i.e. you) use our Website. This includes allowing us to understand such things as how popular certain web pages or advertisements are.

Cookies allow us to improve the structure of our Website, and please note that we cannot identify you personally by using Cookies, as all data collected by the Cookies is anonymous apart from our referral cookie for the Kerchoonz Kwidget - this cookie is required to identify you so that we may pay you for your referrals (please see Terms of Use) and cannot be removed. Cookies and other such similar devices can also assist and facilitate your use of the Website by:

  1. allowing you to carry information across pages of our Website and thereby avoiding having to re-enter information;
  2. allowing you to access stored information within your registration area;
  3. allowing us to tailor content and / or advertisements to match your preferred interests or to hopefully assist in avoiding us showing you the same adverts repeatedly.

How can I remove Cookies ?

It is common for most internet browsers (e.g. Firefox or Windows Explorer) to be automatically set up to accept Cookies, but you can set your browser to refuse a cookie or ask your browser to show you where a cookie has been set up.

However please note that certain services on the Website are only activated by the presence of a Cookie and therefore if you choose to refuse cookies, particular features of this Website may not be available to you. The Kerchoonz Kwidget cookie may not be removed.

If you wish to remove Cookies by disabling them, please click on the 'Manage Cookies' section of the Interactive Advertising Bureau website http://www.allaboutcookies.org/
