Tracking and Use of Cookies

Special computer code allows our system to track user activity on our site. This computer code, when implemented, is called a "cookie."  Our system uses cookie technology through our localization options.  When you enter your zip code and other relevant information, a cookie will assign you a unique number (with no meaning whatsoever outside the customization process) that recalls this information each time you visit.  As a result, you are able to customize your browsing experiences for your interests. At no time will this cookie reveal to others your name, e-mail address, street address, or telephone number (even if you have entered these in other areas of our system). You will always have the choice of opting out of any cookie-based activity on our site.

Securing the Transmission and Storage of Information

If and when we ask you for credit card data when making a reservation or purchasing any item or service including and not limited to gift certificates, it is transferred over a Secured Sockets Layer (SSL) line provided you are using a SSL enabled browser such as Microsoft Internet Explorer or Netscape Navigator.  This ensures that your information is encrypted as it travels over the Internet. This secure mode is enabled before any such information is transmitted from your computer. You will know you are in secure mode when the padlock or key icon in the lower right-hand or left-hand corner of the computer screen appears in the locked position. In addition, when accessing a secure server, the first characters of the site address will change from "http" to "https." After information reaches us it is stored on a secure server that resides behind firewalls designed to block unauthorized access.
