Cookies, Locally Shared Objects, and Other Technologies

In order to track your use of our websites, "cookies" are employed. We, and our affiliates and service providers, use two types of cookies -- so-called "browser cookies" and "Flash cookies". Browser cookies (also called "HTTP cookies") are pieces of information that are sent by websites and stored in your computer's hard drive. We and our affiliates and service providers may place browser cookies on your computer, and may access browser cookies and information about them. Browser cookies allow us and our affiliates and service providers to learn how visitors use our websites. Browser cookies are used for tracking purposes, and they allow for the collection and use of information including what website pages were viewed throughout our websites, as well as information such as the Internet Protocol ("IP") addresses, browser types, and general geographic origin of the computer or other device used access to our websites and similar usage data. Flash cookies (also called "Locally Shared Objects" or "LSOs") may also used for all of these same purposes and/or to remember settings and preferences. However, Flash cookies are stored differently on your hard drive and may be managed through a different interface than the one provided by your web browser. In general, Flash cookies are more persistent and can store more data that browser cookies.

This website may use other technologies such as pixel tags, web beacons, or other forms of local storage, including HTML5 local storage (collectively, "other technologies") in order to collect and track information (including the types of information described above) about individuals who access our websites. Information collected from these types of technologies, and from cookies and Flash cookies, is collectively referred to as "Website Usage Data."

Website Usage Data enables us to tailor our content to our visitors' interests and to help our affiliates better understand the preferences of our audience. In general, we do not use Website Usage Data to personally identify you.

If you do not want to receive browser cookies, you may be able to set your browser to reject them. If you wish to disable or delete Flash cookies, you should consult the Help file for your Flash application, and/or visit http://helpx.adobe.com/flash-player/kb/disable-local-shared-objects-flash.html. However, if you turn cookies off (whether browser or Flash cookies), you may not have access to some WWE features and services. The other technologies that our websites use may not be able to be disabled; if you are concerned about this, you should not use our websites.
