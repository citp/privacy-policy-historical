Dear Friends,

I ran this column about a year ago, and decided to run it again, before the holiday shopping frenzy kicks in. My reasons for doing so are simple—kids are bombarded daily with violent images on TV, on their computers and everyplace they look. As you begin your holiday shopping this year, please remember that you do have choices—you can choose to not purchase DVDs, music, and computer games that are overtly sexual, explicit, and that show violence towards women.

And it’s more than just video games and music that are to blame for these disturbing trends. Every morning when I turn on my computer, I find myself becoming increasingly irate over the torrent of unsolicited email that greets me. You can bet that if your children are online, they see some of it too.
