A Whole30 story from Instagrammer @realfoodchronicles, who found fibromyalgia relief through the Whole30

These pictures were taken about 6 months apart. However there are so many changes between them that you can't see.

My husband and I did our first #Whole30 in February 2014, and experienced a myriad of health benefits. My fibromyalgia symptoms all but disappeared, my skin was glowing, I finally had the energy to start exercising, and I lost 16 lbs.

A few weeks later we went on vacation to New York and thoroughly indulged. When we got back I intended to get 'back on track' but there never seemed to be a 'good time.' Weeks turned in to months and before I knew it, (I was living with) fatigue and extreme muscle pain and regaining almost all the weight I had lost.

We finally began another Whole30 in March 2015 (when the picture on the left was taken). When we started I was sleeping 15-18 hours most nights just to function. Within a week that began to change and I felt like I had 'me' back. We learned a lot more the second time around about how to handle social gatherings and eating out, both of which helped our long term changes (life after Whole30) to be more sustainable.

Since March I've successfully avoided the main foods that trigger my fibromyalgia flare-ups (sugar and gluten), with the exception of a few worth-it indulgences. I have less pain, way more energy, and I've lost over 25 lbs. I no longer experience the unbelievably strong cravings I had assumed were just a part of life. I am free. Thank you. ...
