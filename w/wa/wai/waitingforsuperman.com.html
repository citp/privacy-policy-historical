Information Collection and Use by TakePart

In general, you may visit the Site and view content on the Site without revealing any Personal Information or other individual user information (other than cookies as described below).  If you want to upload content, join a group or otherwise actively participate in the Service and the Site, you will need to become a registered user of the Site. 

To become a registered user of the Site, TakePart will need to collect your email address and date of birth and may collect other Personal Information.  You will also need to select a user name and password.  TakePart collects such Personal Information to authenticate you as a user and to be able send notifications you.  TakePart may also collect other profile data including but not limited to, personal interests, gender, education, location, mobile phone number, and occupation to help in serving content recommendations. 

In connection with certain portions of the Site and Services, TakePart allows users to set up unique personal profiles that can be linked together through groups, friends and other organizations. Each registered user can change his/her profile information at any time.  Selected user names are used when communicating with users or posting content to the Site.  A user’s email address is not shared or displayed in connection with the Site unless authorized by such registered user.  Users can communicate on the Site and with each other through the Site, without disclosing their email addresses.

TakePart may also log non-personally-identifiable information from users of the Site, including IP address, profile information, aggregate user data and browser type. This data is used to manage the Site, track usage and improve the Service.  User IP addresses are recorded for security and monitoring purposes.  TakePart may aggregate this non-personally-identifiable information, such as the number of users that clicked on a particular link and may share such aggregated information with third-parties to provide more relevant content, services and advertisements to users.  Such information will not identify you individually.

Use of Cookies and Other Technology Related Information by TakePart

Cookies are small files containing a string of characters sent to your computer that uniquely identifies your browser.  TakePart uses cookies to store visitors' preferences and to record session information.  These uses include serving content recommendations specific to your interests.  We do not link the information we store in cookies to any Personal Information you submit while on the Site, although your password will be link to cookies so your password does not need to be re-entered when you visit the Site. Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies, but you can reset your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, you must enable cookies from TakePart in order to use most functions on the Site.

TakePart may also use web beacons (also known as "pixel tags" or “clear GIFs”) which are small graphic files that allow TakePart to monitor the use of the TakePart website. A pixel tag can collect information such as the IP (Internet Protocol) address of the computer that downloaded the page on which the tag appears; the URL (Uniform Resource Locator) of the page on which the pixel tag appears; the time the page containing the pixel tag was viewed; the type of browser that fetched the pixel tag; and the identification number of any cookie on the computer previously placed by that server. You can disable pixel tags by changing your browser settings to omit images and disable Javascript; or there are commercial software packages available that can omit pixel tags and most advertisements.

Instead, the information is used to serve you with targeted advertisements on the Site and other websites. You can choose to opt-out of the ad-serving cookie and not have your information used to serve you ads by following the procedure outlined on the third-party ad server’s website. You can also reset your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. TakePart has no control over, and assumes no responsibility for, the content, privacy policies, practices, products, advertisements or services of DoubleClick or any other third-party ad server that serves ads on the Site.
