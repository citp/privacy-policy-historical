# bridges.org

**our privacy policy**

This website is provided as a public service by bridges.org. The following policies apply to this website, and any newsletters, discussion lists and announcement lists which we may own and operate.

**Bridges.org does not and will not collect any information about you through this website without your permission.**

Currently, we request information from anyone who [downloads our free tech.guide](https://web.archive.org/web/20051210204016id_/http%3A//www.bridges.org/techguide/download.html). We request your name, your organization's name, your email address, and information about your intended use of the guide. We may ask you if it is okay to tell others about your use of the guide. We may also ask you if it is okay to publish or post information about your use of the guide as an announcement in our newsletter or in a list on our website. We will not do this without your permission, and we will never release any other information about you to third parties for any reason.

When you request a subscription to the [bridges.news](https://web.archive.org/web/20051210204016id_/http%3A//www.bridges.org/newsletter/index.html) newsletter, we ask for your email address so that we can send it to you. You can also access [bridges.news](https://web.archive.org/web/20051210204016id_/http%3A//www.bridges.org/newsletter/index.html) by visiting the archive page on our website. The bridges.news mailing list is used only to mail the newsletter and to send notices about bridges.org activities. We do not sell, rent or share our mailing list. We will also use the list once annually to solicit contributions during our fund-raising drive.

When inquiries are e-mailed to us, we store the question and the e-mail address information so that we can respond electronically. Unless we ask you, we do not and will not identify publicly anyone who sends questions or comments to our website. We will never share your information with third parties for any reason. 

If you would like to update or delete an email address in our possession at any time for any reason, please contact [info](https://web.archive.org/about/contact.html) and we will make the changes.

We do not use cookies. A "cookie" is a small piece of data that is sent to your browser from a web server and stored on your computer's hard drive. It is often used to customize your web service in some way, but cookies can also be used to track your activities as you surf the web. The bridges.org website does not use cookies, however, many of the sites to which we link to may. You can set your browser software to disable cookies or to prompt you before a cookie is accepted. 

If you have any questions about our privacy policies, please contact [info](https://web.archive.org/about/contact.html). 

For general information regarding privacy you can visit the [Electronic Privacy Information Center](http://www.epic.org/) (EPIC), the [Electronic Frontier Foundation](http://www.eff.org/) (EFF), [BBB Online](http://www.bbb.org/), or the [US Federal Trade Commission](http://www.ftc.gov/).
