WHAT INFORMATION WE GATHER

To use Binverse’s services, you must register as a user of the site. During the registration process we require you to provide us with information that personally identifies you, such as your name, address, telephone number, e-mail address and an account password. This information is collectively referred to as “personal information”. We also ask that you provide us with credit card or other payment account information (“payment information”) which we maintain in encrypted form on secure servers.

We receive and store any personal information and/or payment information you enter anywhere on the site, as well as any communications you send to us.

Additionally, we automatically receive and record information on our server logs from your browser, including your Internet Protocol (“IP”) address, browser type and language, cookie information and the page you request. This information is helpful for marketing purposes and for improving your experience on the site.

To protect against fraud and other misconduct, we may collect information about your interaction with our services, and your posts will contain account identifying information which only Binverse can decode.

We may retain e-mail or other communications you send to Binverse in order to process your inquiries, respond to your requests and improve our services.

Binverse’s services are supplied by third parties. Personal information may be sent to those third parties to deliver the services. Third parties providing services to Binverse may have different privacy policies.

Your account usage is only monitored for the purpose of tracking your download allotment and does not include tracking of specific downloads or posts, although your posts can be traced to you for abuse purposes through account identifying information inserted in your post which only Binverse can decode.

Binverse’s downloadable Usenet browsing software does not collect any personal information or payment information.

We review our data collection, storage and processing practices to ensure that we only collect, store and process the personal information needed to provide or improve our services.

HOW WE USE "COOKIES"

A “cookie” is a small file stored on your hard drive containing information about you. Binverse, like many other companies, uses cookies to collect information

Binverse uses cookies to uniquely identify your browser, store your preferences and “remember” you. Cookies can also help Binverse see which areas of the site are popular and which are not, allowing Binverse to improve and update the site based on data such as total number of visitors and pages viewed.

We do not store any personal information or payment information in cookies.

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies but allow you to instruct your browser to prevent the use of cookies. (The “Help” portion of the toolbar on most browsers will tell you how to prevent your browser from accepting cookies, how to have the browser notify you when you receive a new cookie, or how to disable cookies altogether). If you disable cookies, you may still use our site, but certain aspects of the experience may be impacted.
