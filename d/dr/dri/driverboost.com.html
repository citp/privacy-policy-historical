Cookies and Other Technologies

Following common e-commerce practices, DriverBoost uses "cookies" and other technologies to collect non-personal data that enables us to better understand and improve the usability, performance and effectiveness of DriverBoost’ Web site, and for reporting purposes to track sales. Cookies are unique text files sent to your browser and stored on your computer. We use both session ID cookies and persistent cookies. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser. We use session cookies to maintain a login session. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time. We use persistent cookies to help remember user preferences. We do not store any personally identifiable information with these cookies. Use of these third party cookies by our tracking utility company or service providers is not governed by this Privacy Policy. We do not have access or control over these cookies.

If you do not want DriverBoost to send cookies to your browser, you can set your browser options to reject cookies or notify you when a Web site tries to put a cookie in your browser software. Rejecting cookies may affect your ability to use some of our Web site services.

This information is stored in anonymous profiles and is not associated with a user’s account. Our current partners help us to track our website usage. No personally identifiable information is collected.
