Use of Cookies

dekeOnline collects non-personally identifiable information through the use of a software technology called "cookies," through pixel tags, and through members' voluntary submissions to dekeOnline and/or upon member requests. A cookie is a text file that is placed on a hard disk drive by a web page server. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to a member’s computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to a member, and can only be read by a web server in the domain that issued the cookie to the member.

Among other things, the non-personally identifiable information collected through cookies allows dekeOnline to know which areas are favorites, which areas need a bit of improvement, or what technologies and Internet services are being used by members so that it may continually improve the online experiences of members of dekeOnline.

Most web browsers give a member a way to refuse or disable most cookies (but not Flash cookies). If a member chooses to disable, refuse, reject or delete cookies, some of the functionality of dekeOnline may no longer be available.
