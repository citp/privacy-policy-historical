Privacy Policy

Privacy and Information Practices
Throughout its existence, Deluxe Corporation ("Deluxe") has been a trusted custodian of sensitive consumer data. Financial services companies and consumers rely on that trust. The operating subsidiaries of Deluxe created a formal privacy policy in 1997 to ensure that the clients and customers of Deluxe understand Deluxe's commitment to protecting consumer privacy.

Deluxe's privacy policies reflect its commitment to respond to the changing needs and concerns of consumers, financial services companies, and regulators. Most importantly, Deluxe is committed to maintaining the high standards and reputation for privacy and the trust it has earned since its formation in 1915.

Deluxe Corporation owns several businesses. Each business serves customers who have specific and unique privacy concerns, and each business has implemented a privacy policy that reflects the applicable legal requirements and customer expectations for that business. Regardless of the business model, each of the Deluxe companies has built its privacy policy around the following guiding principles:

Guiding Principles on Privacy
Accuracy - A commitment to use reasonable procedures to ensure that information collected, used, and exchanged will be accurate and complete

Security - A commitment to utilize appropriate security measures for protecting information in any form from unauthorized access, use, and disclosure

Compliance - A commitment to collect, use, and exchange consumer data in accordance with applicable laws, including the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, and contractual obligations

Confidentiality - A commitment to collect, use, and exchange information in accordance with appropriate confidentiality standards

Privacy - A commitment to self-regulation in recognition of consumers' expectation of privacy and consistent with leading industry standards

Responsiveness - A commitment to respond to consumer inquiries in a timely and accurate manner

Employee education - A commitment to educate employees regarding information practices and the proper handling and use of information

Self-auditing - A commitment to periodic auditing of internal activities to ensure adherence to established information practices, policies, and procedures

Deluxe Financial Services
Deluxe Financial Services Web sites are directed or intended for U.S. residents only. This Privacy Policy, effective May 31, 2001, applies to all consumer-identifiable information Deluxe Financial Services obtains when a consumer visits our Web site(s). Deluxe Business Checks and Solutions, a separate division, follows the Privacy Policy provided on their Web sites.

Protecting privacy is not new to Deluxe Financial Services. We have a long history of recognizing and protecting the privacy of consumers. Deluxe Financial Services is committed to protecting your privacy and utilizing technology that gives you the safest and most powerful online experience available. We want to assure you that when Deluxe Financial Services asks for customer information, our goal is to improve our customer relationships. We also participate in the Council of Better Business Bureau's BBBOnLine Privacy Program, and comply with all BBBOnLine Privacy standards. Further information about this program and its dispute resolution program is available at www.bbbonline.org.

The following statements will help you understand how Deluxe Financial Services collects, uses, and safeguards the information you provide through our Web sites.

Information Collection and Use
When you browse Deluxe Financial Services Web sites, you do so without revealing your identity. Personal information is not collected without your awareness. For each visitor to our Web sites, our servers recognize the Web site you came from and the Web site you visit after you leave.

In the order-capture process, Deluxe Financial Services may track the order preferences of visitors to our Web site(s). Information tracked and retained by Deluxe Financial Services is generic and not individually identifiable. We track order-preference information by using cookies.

Cookies
Cookies are small pieces of information that are stored by your Web browser on your computer's hard drive. A cookie may contain information (such as a unique user session ID) that is used to track the pages of the Web sites you've visited. Deluxe Financial Services uses cookies, but we do not store personally identifiable information in your cookies. The Deluxe Financial Services Personal Check Reordering Web site uses cookies to improve your shopping experience. The information stored in cookies helps us serve you better by keeping track of your order as you shop at our site. This information also helps us improve our site design.

Most Web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually set your browser to decline cookies. Even if your Web browser is set to decline cookies, you can use the majority of the features within our sites.

Disclosure of Individual Consumer Information
Deluxe Financial Services is committed to protecting your private information. We use the information collected about you to process orders and to provide a more personalized shopping experience. Names and other consumer information that are received by Deluxe Financial Services through a financial institution or via our Deluxe Financial Services Web sites are not shared with or rented to nonaffiliated third parties except for the purpose of fulfilling our contracts with financial institutions or as otherwise permitted by law.

Personal Information You Choose to Provide
At times, Deluxe Financial Services may request that you voluntarily supply personal information for purposes such as receiving correspondence, registering on a Web site, making purchases, participating in online surveys, or receiving notice of employment opportunities. If you elect to participate, Deluxe Financial Services may require personally identifiable information, including your name, mailing address, phone number, financial institution information, credit card information, or e-mail address. When you submit personal information to Deluxe Financial Services, you understand and agree that Deluxe Financial Services and its subsidiaries may access, store, and use your information as allowed by contract. Information you provide will be safeguarded according to strict standards of security and confidentiality. Because Deluxe Financial Services recognizes and appreciates the importance of responsible use of the information you choose to provide, we do not share e-mail addresses with third parties.

Security of Information
Security measures are in place on Deluxe Financial Services Web sites to protect against the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under our control. We offer industry-standard security measures available through your Web browser. When you are on our Web sites, the information you provide is encrypted and scrambled en route and decoded once it reaches Deluxe Financial Services. Please remember that e-mail, which is different from the encrypted sessions above, is not secure. Therefore, we ask that you do not send confidential information such as social security or account numbers to us via an unsecured e-mail message. Send confidential information to us via U.S. mail or phone.

Deluxe Financial Services takes steps to require that our vendors, consultants, suppliers, and contractors observe our Privacy Policy with respect to security and the collection, use, and exchange of our customers' information. They are expected to abide by the Privacy Policy when conducting work for us.

Choice to Opt-Out
Deluxe Financial Services will not provide confidential information to third parties for marketing purposes, except as authorized by the financial institution or otherwise permitted by law. Because Deluxe Financial Services does not sell or share customer information with marketers outside our company, you do not need to do anything to prevent your personal information from being shared with them.

Children Visiting our Web Sites
Our Web sites are directed at adult consumers and guests. We do not knowingly collect or use information from children. Should children access our Web sites, we are confident that parents will not judge any of the information provided as objectionable for viewing. If you are under 18, you may use our Web sites only with the involvement of a parent or guardian.

Other Web Sites Linked to Deluxe Financial Services Web Sites
Deluxe Financial Services is not responsible for the content or information practices employed by other Web sites that are linked to or from our Web sites. In most cases, links to non-Deluxe Financial Services Web sites are provided solely as pointers to information on topics that may be useful to the users of the Deluxe Financial Services Web sites.

Enforcement
Periodically, our operations and business practices are reviewed for compliance with corporate policies and procedures governing the confidentiality of information. These reviews are conducted by internal staff, internal auditors who report directly to the Deluxe Board of Directors, external auditing and accounting firms, and government regulators. Included in these self-assessments and examinations are reviews of the controls and safeguards related to consumer privacy described in our Privacy Policy.

We reserve the right, at our discretion, to change, modify, add, or remove portions of this policy at any time. Your continued use of Deluxe Financial Services Web sites following the posting of changes to these terms means that you accept these changes.

Our Ongoing Commitment Under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act
Deluxe Financial Services continues to work with its clients and customers to make sure that consumer data is used appropriately and within the scope of applicable laws. Information is made available for fraud prevention and detection purposes and for authorized criminal or government investigations. This information is made available in order to protect consumers and financial services companies; these disclosures are permitted by law.

Deluxe Financial Services expresses its commitment to appropriate data use in its check printing agreements with its financial institution customers. The Deluxe Financial Services standard agreement states:

Deluxe Financial Services agrees it will not provide to third parties for marketing purposes confidential information delivered to it under this agreement except as authorized by the Financial Institution. This agreement does not apply to disclosures of nonpublic personal information as defined by the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act:

1) As necessary to effect, administer, or enforce a transaction requested or authorized by a consumer or to service or process a financial product or service requested or authorized by a consumer;
2) With the consent or at the direction of the consumer;
3) To assist in protecting against or preventing actual or potential fraud or unauthorized transactions;
4)a) To a consumer reporting agency in accordance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act; or
b) From a consumer report reported by a consumer reporting agency;
5) To comply with Federal, State or local laws, rules and other applicable legal requirements; to comply with a properly authorized civil, criminal or regulatory investigation, or subpoena or summons by Federal, State or local authorities; or to respond to judicial process or government regulatory authority having jurisdiction over a Financial Institution for examination, compliance or other purposes as authorized by law.
