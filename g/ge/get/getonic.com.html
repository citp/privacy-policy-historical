COOKIES AND WEB BEACONS

The website, like many websites, uses cookies to prepare customized web pages for you. A cookie is a file containing a string of code that we transfer to your computer's hard drive, to be kept there for later use. With cookies in place, whenever you use your web browser to connect to the website, our systems are able to recognize you. This enables the website to present you with custom web pages. So, for example, instead of seeing just a generic welcome page, you may see a welcome page with your name on it and information that relates to where you live and Goods or services that reflect your interests.

In addition to the website's cookies, when you visit the website your computer may receive cookies from our advertisers and from other sites displayed as search results or links. The cookies inserted by those third parties are not covered by the website's Privacy Policy, but rather, by the policies of those advertisers and outside websites.

You can set up your browser to reject cookies or tell you when a cookie has been sent. However, cookies allow you to take full advantage of the website's interactive features, and we hope you will leave them turned on.

The website also uses web beacons (also known as action tags or single pixel gifs) to access the website's cookies inside and outside our web pages and in connection with the website products and services. A web beacon is a transparent image placed on a web page that sends a signal when a person visits that page. This enables us to assess the popularity and effectiveness of the material posted on the website. We may also use web beacons in connection with e-mails sent to those who have registered on the website, to let us know whether and when the e-mail was opened. No personal information is transmitted through web beacons.
