Privacy Policy

The last update to our Privacy Policy was posted on August 14, 2014. This Privacy Policy governs the privacy terms of our website, www.GoodTherapy.org. We are committed to maintaining the privacy of GoodTherapy.org users, visitors, and members.

 

Information Collection

GoodTherapy.org collects information from our users at several different points on our website.

 

(A) GoodTherapy.org records the name, phone (optional), email, and message when a site visitor sends an email to a therapist through the GoodTherapy.org messaging center. This information is kept confidential and no one except trained GoodTherapy.org support team members (for the purpose of moderating for spam or other content that is in violation of GoodTherapy.org terms of service) and the therapist(s) to whom a site visitor sends the email to can see this information. GoodTherapy.org never rents, sells, or gives this information to any third party. When a site visitor emails a therapist through our internal messaging system, the site visitor is conveniently and automatically emailed a notification when the therapist has been contacted. Additionally, when site visitors email a therapist through our internal messaging system, they may be automatically subscribed to our monthly email newsletter. Users may unsubscribe from this newsletter by completing the unsubscribe form found here.

 

(B) GoodTherapy.org collects information entered by therapists when they register (or try to register) for membership or public membership. The information about the therapist's practice is made public and can be viewed in any GoodTherapy.org profile. The other information (including but not limited to username, email address, password, etc.) is kept private in a database and may be used by our internal departments if needed (such as debugging errors or analyzing outcome of marketing campaigns). GoodTherapy.org never rents, sells, or gives this information to any third party.

 

(C) GoodTherapy.org collects site visitor email addresses when site visitors subscribe to the blog. This email is used to send site visitors blog post or blog comment summaries and site visitors can unsubscribe anytime using unsubscribe option(s) found in the email and/or on the site. GoodTherapy.org never rents, sells, or gives this information to any third party.

 

(D) GoodTherapy.org collects information entered by site visitors when they register (or try to register) for a public web conference or public non-professional membership. This information (including but not limited to name, username, email address, password, etc.) is kept private in a database and may be used by our internal departments if needed (such as debugging errors or analyzing outcome of marketing campaigns). GoodTherapy.org never rents, sells, or gives this information to any third party.

 

(E) GoodTherapy.org does not store credit card information on our servers. All credit card transactions are processed through our SSL FirstData payment page and all data is stored on the FirstData server. You can read the Privacy Policy of FirstData here: http://www.firstdata.com/en_us/privacy

 

(F) GoodTherapy.org uses Google Analytics which collects information about your browsing experience on our site. This information is used in an anonymous way and not associated with a specific individual. You can read about Google Analytics' Terms of Service and Google's Privacy Policy on the following pages, respectively: http://www.google.com/analytics/tos.html and http://www.google.com/privacypolicy.html

 

 

GoodTherapy.org uses Google Analytics Advertising Features. This includes, but is not limited to, user-directed marketing, geographic-directed marketing, remarketing with Google Analytics, Google Display Network Impression Reporting, DoubleClick Platform Integrations, and Google Analytics Demographics and Interest Reporting.

 

You can opt out of Google Analytics Advertising Features here: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout/

 

No personally-identifiable information collected by GoodTherapy.org will be merged with non-personally identifiable information collected through any Google advertising product or feature.

 

(G) GoodTherapy.org uses WhosClickingWho.com to prevent click fraud. This service collects IP address and other data when someone clicks on GoodTherapy.org ads from Google or its partner sites. You can read the Privacy Policy of WhosClickingWho.com here: http://www.whosclickingwho.com/privacypolicy.html

 

(H) GoodTherapy.org may use other web analytics services and/or software to collect information about users’ browsing activities. This information is used to analyze the performance of the website and associated marketing campaigns. Some of the services/software like Google Analytics may store this information on their own servers.

 

Information Use

GoodTherapy.org does not sell or otherwise provide the information given to them to third party sources. All information is confidential unless:

 

a. The user creates a listing/advertisement in the GoodTherapy.org Directory. The information contained within a public profile may be viewed by third parties.

 

b. The information is subpoenaed by officials of the United States government, or if GoodTherapy.org deems that retaining said information may cause a direct physical threat to one or more individuals.

 

Additional Information:

 

Cookies
GoodTherapy.org uses cookies to identify which areas of our website you have visited, so the next time you visit, we can better personalize the content that you see on our website. In addition, our website uses third-party cookies (such as Google Analytics cookies) to optimize and personalize the advertising content on our website. Most web browsers can be set to disable the use of Cookies. However, if you disable Cookies, you may not be able to use certain functionality on our website correctly, or at all. GoodTherapy.org never places your personal information in Cookies.

 

Log Files
Like most standard website servers, GoodTherapy.org uses log files. This includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, platform type, date/time stamp, and number of clicks to analyze trends, administer the site, track users’ movement in the aggregate, and gather broad demographic information for aggregate use. IP addresses etc. are not linked to personally identifiable information contained in user profiles.

Additionally, in very rare cases user GoodTherapy.org reserves the right to break privacy if it is determined that retaining said information may cause a direct physical threat to one or more individuals.

Our Security Policy
Our website takes every precaution to protect users' information. When users submit sensitive information via the website, their information is protected both online and off-line. When our registration/order form asks users to enter sensitive information (such as credit card number), that information is encrypted and is protected with SSL. While we use SSL encryption to protect sensitive information online, we also do everything in our power to protect user-information offline. All of our users' information, not just the sensitive information mentioned above, is restricted in our server. Only GoodTherapy.org administrators or employees who need the information to perform a specific job (for example, our billing clerk or a customer service representative) are granted access to users' personal information. Our employees must use password-protected logins to access this server. Each time they return, they must re-enter their password to regain access to user information. All employees are kept up-to-date on our security and privacy practices. Any time new policies are added, our employees are notified about what they can do to ensure our users' information is protected. Finally, the servers that store personally identifiable information are in a secure environment in a locked building.
