Privacy Policy

The PFA and its client advertisers/sponsors consider Internet user privacy to be of paramount importance. The information below outlines The PFA's commitment to protecting users' privacy and to ensuring the Internet's integrity.
The PFA does not know the name, phone number or home address of individuals to whom we deliver advertisements on the Internet. The identities of all users who see an ad from a The PFA server/client remain completely anonymous to us.
In the course of delivering an ad to you, we do not collect any personally-identifiable information about you (e.g. names, addresses, etc.). Therefore, we have none to sell, rent, or give away to third parties. The information we collect includes ads served to and clicked on by users. We also collect data indicating whether or not a user visits specific pages of a client advertiser's Web site (e.g. a registration or checkout page). This information is useful only to the specific advertiser who commissioned us to deliver its ads, and only in the context of creative selection and effectiveness reporting. In the unlikely event that a third party gained access to user numbers and non-personally identifiable information, this data would be meaningless and useless.
The Site uses cookies for collecting user information and the data collected in this way may be processed in the same way as other data submitted to The Professional Footballers Association (and outlined above) and for the same purposes. In addition, The PFA may combine this information with other users' information and review it in an aggregate form to understand the type of individuals who view a specific type of ad or visit a particular kind of website. The PFA stores a random user number in a "cookie" on each user's computer. A The PFA cookie stored on a user's hard drive only contains a random user number (most of the sites you visit use some form of cookie). Cookies are not programs and cannot cause damage to your system. The PFA uses this number to track advertising effectiveness. One example of this is measuring ad frequency; this helps us ensure that a user is not bombarded with the same creative over and over again. While we believe that cookies enhance the Web experience for the user by limiting the repetitiveness of advertising and increasing the level of relevant content on the Web, they are not essential to our delivering the Internet's most effective advertising. If you do not want the benefits of allowing cookies to be stored on your computer please block all cookies by globally or selectively disabling cookie use in the browser configuration
In some cases, you may voluntarily provide personal information in response to a specific ad (this ad may ask you to fill out a survey or provide for example your post code). If this does occur, The PFA may collect this information on its own behalf or on behalf of an advertiser. In this case the following applies.
