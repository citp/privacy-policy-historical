# nationalparks.org Privacy Policy

****Privacy Policy**  
The National Park Foundation, publisher of nationalparks.org and its related content and services, believes that electronic privacy is crucial for the ongoing success of the Internet as a communications medium. On occasion, The National Park Foundation will make available to potential partners aggregated results from research it has conducted on the nationalparks.org visitor base. The National Park Foundation will only provide such demographic information on an aggregated basis and does not disclose individual reader information.**

**E-mail**  
Any/all e-mail addresses sent to us as the result of correspondence with the visitors of nationalparks.org will not be used or collected for the purposes of reselling to a third party. 

**Access**  
The National Park Foundation does not currently collect or archive any/all personal information sent to us on an individual basis. All information collected and analyzed solely on an aggregated basis. If -- in the future -- The National Park Foundation collects or archives personal information an individual basis and you wish to correct/add/remove the personal information submitted to our site, please contact the us at [ask-npf@nationalparks.org](mailto:ask-npf@nationalparks.org). 

**Security**  
All consumer transactions and purchases on this web site are secured using VeriSign 128-bit SSL encryption. This ensures that all transactions and purchases you send to us via the World Wide Web will be encrypted. If any other internet user intercepts the communication he/she will only be able to see it in an encrypted (garbled) form. 

**Cookies**  
The nationalparks.org web site uses cookies to improve its visitor’s experience. The use of cookies is common throughout the Internet. Cookies are small data files that are written to a user’s hard drive to maintain session information or identify a user. The use of cookies allows us to make improvements and updates to our site based on actual usage. They are used to maintain general session information and are not used for identification purposes. Our site does not access any existing cookies from our visitor's systems that may be shared by advertising servers, etc. 

If you prefer, you can set your browser to refuse cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent to your hard drive. The user’s manual or online help that comes with your browser software should provide you with information on how to configure your browser software to detect cookies. Please be aware, however, that cookies are necessary to use some of the features offered on nationalparks.org. If you have any questions regarding this statement, please contact us via email at [questions@nationalparks.org](mailto:questions@nationalparks.org). 

**Traffic Data**  
Each time a visitor comes to the nationalparks.org site, information is collected to improve the overall quality of the web site. The National Park Foundation collects the visitor's domain name/IP Address (e.g., whether the user is logged on from netscape.com or yale.edu or 255.255.255.0), referral data (e.g., we record the address of the last URL a user visited prior to clicking through to nationalparks.org), and browser/platform type (e.g., a Netscape browser on a Macintosh platform). 

The National Park Foundation also counts, tracks, and aggregates the visitor's activity into our analysis of general traffic flows at nationalparks.org (e.g., tracking where traffic comes from and how traffic flows within the site). The National Park Foundation may merge this information into group data, which may then be shared on an aggregated basis with potential partners. The National Park Foundation will not disclose your individual identity or personal data. When The National Park Foundation does present aggregated information to potential partners, no one will be able to identify you or contact you. 

**Contact Us**  
Any and all questions regarding this statement should be directed to [ask-npf@nationalparks.org](mailto:ask-npf@nationalparks.org).
