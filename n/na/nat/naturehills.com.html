Find Your Growing Zone close[x]
Plants have varying growing habits, so you want to find those best suited for your local climate. The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) has divided the country into 11 growing zones. These growing zones are based upon the minimum winter temperatures in each area. The USDA Hardiness Zones provide an excellent method of determining which plants are cold hardy in your local climate.

The USDA zones are shown by primarily horizontal bands that stretch from west to east across the U.S. The lowest-numbered (coldest) zones are in the north, and the zone numbers get progressively higher toward the southern (warmer) parts of the country. Each climatic zone hosts a unique variety of plants that can impact shade trees make.

Finding a viable plant for your area is simple. Enter your zip code in the box provided and the USDA Hardiness Zone you reside in will be displayed. All of the plants on the Nature Hills site have a climate zone recommendation displayed on each page. If your zip code-generated climatic zone falls within the plant´s recommended growing area, that plant should tolerate your area´s winter temperatures.

Our Shipping Process

close[x]

Being passionate about plants means that we have to be passionate about their safety when they are shipped. We take into consideration all aspects of shipping live plant material, including transit time, weather, packaging material, and boxing. That way, the plants that we take such pride in growing will be something that you will be proud to put in your garden.

We start by placing our hand-selected plants into first-rate containers that have been thoroughly “stress tested”. We have taken the time to make sure that our boxes can hold up to any abuse that can occur during the transit process. This ensures that your plants arrive safely at your doorstep.

When we ship potted material, the plants are well hydrated and shipped in ventilated boxes with special bracing designed for each container's size. This secures the plants and helps to keep them protected, even if the ride on the truck is a little bumpy. When shipping our bareroot material, we package the trees tightly in long narrow boxes after the roots have been pre-moistened, and then secure the item(s) to the box to prevent extra movement during shipping.

We also pay special attention to transit time and weather conditions. We start our spring and fall shipping when the conditions are right for planting in your specific area. In addition, we consider spikes in temperatures in the spring and fall, and ship only when the weather conditions won't damage your new plants.

In the spring, warmer temperatures can cause moisture to evaporate more quickly than it does when temperatures are cooler. We apply a moisture-retaining gel to bareroot material, and we make sure that potted material is well watered when warm weather hits. Cooler weather can also be a factor when shipping, as we want to make sure that plant material will not freeze in transit.

Rest assured, if the conditions are not safe to ship your plants, whether it is too hot or too cold, then Nature Hills will delay shipping until we are able to safely deliver our quality products to you.

We at Nature Hills strive for excellence in shipping standards, so that our customers receive nothing but the best in plant material. If something does happen to your order during shipping, (despite all of our efforts!), please notify us within 48 hours of your order's arrival.

Nature Hills will replace, at our cost, any item that arrives damaged from shipping.
