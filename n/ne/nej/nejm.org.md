**PRINCIPLES GOVERNING THIS SITE**

This is a Web site of the _New England Journal of Medicine_ , owned and published by the Massachusetts Medical Society. The _New England Journal of Medicine_ maintains this Web site in conjunction with Stanford University’s HighWire Press (see <http://highwire.stanford.edu/about/privacy.dtl>) and together we offer online services according to the principles below.

We take privacy issues seriously and are committed to protecting your personal information. Please take a moment to review our privacy policy, which explains how we collect, use, and safeguard information on our Web site. This privacy policy applies only to information collected by the __New England Journal of Medicine__ through its Web site.

**WHAT INFORMATION DO WE COLLECT?**

**Information You Provide to Us**

We will request information from you if you establish a personal profile on our site to gain access to certain content or services, if you ask to be notified by e-mail about online content, or if you participate in surveys we conduct. This requires the input of personal information and preferences that may include, but is not limited to, details such as your name, address (postal and e-mail), telephone number, or demographic information. If you request paid content, including subscriptions, we will also ask for payment information such as credit card type and number.       

**Information That Is Automatically Collected**

We collect general data about the movement of visitors through our site, but this does not use any personally identifiable information. This may include IP address, host name, domain name, browser version and platform, date and time of requests, and pages requested. This information is used to measure and analyze traffic and usage of the site.

> _Cookies  
>  _Cookies are small files stored on your computer that we use to recognize you and your access privileges and preferences. Cookies allow you to store your username and password so you don’t have to log in each time you visit. We store minimal personally identifiable information in cookies and protect this information. We do not store complete records or credit card numbers in cookies. Most Web browsers automatically accept cookies. Most browsers can be configured to prevent this, but you may not be able to access some parts of our site without cookies.
> 
> _Clear Gifs (Web Beacons/Web Bugs)  
>  _Clear gifs are tiny graphics with unique identifiers that function similarly to cookies and allow us to track site activity. We do not use these to collect personally identifiable information.
> 
> _Advertisement Servers  
>  _CheckM8 serves advertising on the _New England Journal of Medicine_ Web site. CheckM8 does not collect any personally identifiable information regarding consumers who view or interact with CheckM8 advertisements. CheckM8 solely collects non-personally identifiable ad delivery and reporting data. For further information, see CheckM8’s privacy policy at [www.checkm8.com/?section=27](http://www.checkm8.com/?section=27).

**HOW IS THIS INFORMATION USED?**

Information that you provide to us will be used to process, fulfill, and deliver your requests for content and services. We may send you information about our products and services, unless you have indicated you do not wish to receive further information. 

Information that is automatically collected is used to monitor usage patterns of the Web site in order to help us improve the site and services.

**We do not release individual information about your use of our site, including e-mail address, to any third party.  We do not sell or rent your e-mail address to any third party. You may unsubscribe from our e-mail services at any time.**

We may report activity about our Web site to third parties, including partners and advertisers. We will only use aggregate information about the types of visitors to our site in such reporting. Please also see our [Internet Advertising Policy](http://www.nejm.org/aboutnejm/adpol.asp).

**WHAT SECURITY MEASURES ARE USED?**

We take every precaution to protect our users’ information. When you submit sensitive information via the Web site, your information is protected both online and off-line. We use the latest Secure Socket Layer (SSL) technology, which encrypts all your credit card and personal information. It is unreadable as it travels across the Internet.

**HOW TO MAKE CHANGES TO YOUR INFORMATION**

Once you have created an identity or paid account on our site, you can update your information at any time. You may also choose to stop receiving offer information from us about content or services. You can do this by visiting our online customer service center (<https://secure.nejm.org/services/>) and clicking on the Change Your Profile link.

You can also contact our Customer Service Department at (800) 843-6356 or (781) 893-3800, extension 5515, (outside the USA), [nejmcust@mms.org,](mailto:nejmcust@mms.org) or Customer Service, _New England Journal of Medicine_ , 860 Winter Street, Waltham, MA  02451-1413, USA.
