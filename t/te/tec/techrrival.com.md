# Privacy Policy - Tech Arrival

We use them to compile data regarding user interactions to sell that data to interested parties.

We also use the following advertising networks. For more information on what data they collect, process and more information, kindly refer to their respective privacy policies.

We use a tool called “Google Analytics” to collect information about the use of this site. Google Analytics collects information such as how often users visit this site, what pages they visit when they do so, and what other sites they used prior to coming to this site. We use the information we get from Google Analytics only to improve this site. Google Analytics collects only the IP address assigned to you on the date you visit this site, rather than your name or other identifying information. We do not combine the information collected through the use of Google Analytics with personally identifiable information. Although Google Analytics plants a permanent cookie on your web browser to identify you as a unique user the next time you visit this site, the cookie cannot be used by anyone but Google. Google’s ability to use and share information collected by Google Analytics about your visits to this site is restricted by the [Google Analytics Terms of Use](https://www.google.com/analytics/terms/) and the [Google Privacy Policy](https://www.google.com/policies/privacy/). You can prevent Google Analytics from recognizing you on return visits to this site by disabling cookies on your browser.

We use Facebook Messenger Chat Plugin to let users chat with us if they need any help or support from us. We can access only the information that you have made public on Facebook. We may use the chat for quality and training purpose in the future and to improve our services. We request users not to share any sensitive information over the chat. For more information refer to [Facebook Privacy Policy](https://www.facebook.com/policy.php).

### Facebook Like button and social widgets

The Facebook Like button and social widgets are services allowing interaction with the Facebook social network provided by Facebook, Inc. For more information refer to [Facebook Privacy Policy](https://www.facebook.com/policy.php).

### Twitter Tweet button and social widgets

The Twitter Tweet button and social widgets are services allowing interaction with the Twitter social network provided by Twitter, Inc. For more information refer to [Twitter Privacy Policy](https://twitter.com/privacy).

### OneSignal

We use push notification service called “OneSignal”. All the data that is required to do so is collected and stored by OneSignal. We use OneSignal platform only to provide push notification directly to your device. You can find more information on [OneSignal Privacy Policy](https://onesignal.com/privacy_policy).

### Hotjar

We use Hotjar to track how visitors of our website interact with the content provided by us. This helps us figure out where users click frequently, their behavior with the content and other things that help us make the content better and increase our conversion rate. For more information refer to [Hotjar Privacy Policy](https://www.hotjar.com/legal/policies/privacy).

### Comments

When visitors leave comments on the site we collect the data shown in the comments form, and also the visitor’s IP address and browser user agent string to help spam detection.

The user may use Login with a Social Media Account, more about this discussed in “Collection of Personally Identifiable Information From or Through Social Media Sites”.

An anonymized string created from your email address (also called a hash) may be provided to the Gravatar service to see if you are using it. The Gravatar service privacy policy is available here: <https://automattic.com/privacy/>. After approval of your comment, your profile picture is visible to the public in the context of your comment.

If you leave a comment, the comment and its metadata are retained indefinitely. This is so we can recognize and approve any follow-up comments automatically instead of holding them in a moderation queue.

If you leave a comment on our site you may opt-in to save your name, email address and website in cookies. These are for your convenience so that you do not have to fill in your details again when you leave another comment. These cookies will last for one year.

### Emails and Newsletters

You may always opt-out of receiving future e-mail marketing messages and newsletters from us by following the instructions contained within the emails and newsletters, or by e-mailing, calling or writing us at the addresses given in the Privacy Policy or Contact Us page.

### Embedded content from other websites

Articles on this site may include embedded content (e.g. videos, images, articles, etc.). Embedded content from other websites behaves in the exact same way as if the visitor has visited the other website.

These websites may collect data about you, use cookies, embed additional third-party tracking, and monitor your interaction with that embedded content, including tracking your interaction with the embedded content if you have an account and are logged in to that website.

### Viewing, Changing & Erasing Personal Data

If you want to know what Personal Data we stored about you, you can contact us, with reasonable frequency, by sending an email to the email address given in the contact section of Privacy Policy. We will send you an overview of all your stored Personal Data within four (4) weeks.

After receipt of this overview, you may request us to correct, supplement, delete or block Personal Data that is factually incorrect, incomplete or irrelevant for the purpose of processing, or if the data is in violation of a legal provision. We will inform you within four (4) weeks whether it will comply your request.

If you wish to delete your entire data you can do so by sending an email to the email address given in the contact section of Privacy Policy.

After deleting of your Account, we may store your Personal Data and Account for a further period of time for a number of reasons, including but not limited to backup reasons, legal obligations concerning the retention of accounting information and the prevention of malicious actions resulting from identity theft or other offences or crimes such as fraud.

All retained Personal Data continues to be subject to this Privacy Policy.
