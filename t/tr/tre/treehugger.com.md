# Treehugger: Privacy Policy

###  Privacy Policy

## July 30, 2004 12:55 PM - [Nick Aster, San Francisco](http://www.treehugger.com/authors/index.php?author=Nick)

**TreeHugger.com Privacy Statement**

This statement covers the site www.TreeHugger.com (“TreeHugger”). Please read this statement carefully before using proceeding to access TreeHugger. Your use of TreeHugger indicates your agreement to abide by the Terms in effect.

**Information Collection**

TreeHugger Media is the sole owner of the information collected on www.TreeHugger.com. TreeHugger may collect information from our users at several different points on our Web site.

**Comments**

In order to enhance their use of this Web site, users can choose to leave comments on particular articles of interest. To leave a comment, users must submit information including a valid email address. TreeHugger uses this information to screen out users who leave comments prohibited by our terms and conditions of use, and will not pass this information to any other organization.

**Mailing List**

TreeHugger may launch a mailing list in 2004. In order to sign up to receive regular emails from this mailing list, users must submit information including a valid email address and demographic information such as location and interests. This information will be used in aggregate form only in order to assess general user interest in various internal and third party products and services, and your personal and contact information will not be passed to any other organization. Users who choose to sign up for the mailing list will receive regular emails from TreeHugger including new articles added to the site as well as selected emails from third party TreeHugger site sponsors. If you do not wish to receive emails from third party advertisers, please do not sign up for the mailing list. Note that if we do send third party emails, these will not come more often than 1x/week.

**Discussion Group and Cookies**

In order to further enhance TreeHugger user experience, TreeHugger may launch a discussion board service where users can discuss common topics of interest.
