Truveo Privacy Policy
At Truveo, your privacy is paramount to us as we strive to serve you better with our new and existing services. We are committed to user privacy and our policy below describes our principles in maintaining user trust and confidence and protecting your personal information.
Information Collection and Use
Truveo does not collect or use any personally identifying information when you visit our website. Truveo may, however, from time-to-time, collect non-personally identifying information available from your browser when you request a page from our website. This information includes your IP address, your browser type, the URL requested by your browser and the date and time of your query. We use this information to monitor the usage of our website and to improve the quality of our service.
On occasion and in the future, non-personally-identifiable information we collect may be shared with third-parties to provide more relevant services and advertisements to our visitors. For instance, we may share the number of users that have searched for a specific video or clicked on a specific advertisement featured on our site.
We adhere to valid legal processes and may provide information as required by law to protect and defend the rights of our company, and in certain instances, to protect the personal safety of users and the public.
Cookies
Cookies are small files that are created when you visit specific websites. When you visit Truveo, a cookie is sent to your computer to identify your browser. Cookies cannot be used to run programs or deliver viruses to your computer. Cookies are uniquely assigned to you, and can only be read by a Web server in the domain that issued the cookie to you. We use cookies to store any preferences that you specify on our website and to enhance the quality of our searches.
One of the primary purposes of cookies is to provide a convenience feature to save you time. The purpose of a cookie is to tell the Web server that you have returned to a specific page. For example, if you personalize a Web page, or navigate within a site, a cookie helps the site to recall your specific information on subsequent visits. This simplifies the process of delivering relevant content, ease site navigation, and so on.
Most browsers are set up to automatically accept cookies. You can configure your browser to accept or reject all or some cookies or notify you when a cookie is set. To benefit from all Truveo features, you will need to use cookies for the Truveo site.
Links to Other Sites
Sites listed as search results from Truveo are developed by entities over which Truveo has no control. We are not responsible for the content or the privacy practices undertaken by these sites and encourage users to read the individual privacy statement for each site. Some of theses sites may place cookies on your computer, collect usage data, or ask for personal information.
Children
Truveo's policies adhere to the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), which defines a set of rules for the collection of information from minors. For more information, please visit the FTC website.
Changes to this Policy
From time to time, we may make changes to our privacy policy to better reflect our business and the marketplace, and we will keep you informed of these changes on our website.
