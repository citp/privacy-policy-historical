Section 8. How Do We Use Cookies & Other Tracking Technologies?

When we interact with the Site, we strive to make your experience convenient and meaningful. We may use or engage others to use cookies, clear gifs, and flash track your activity and collect data via the Site. We may combine this data with other information we collect from you. We use cookie data to recognize you as a particular user when you visit the Site, to aggregate user traffic flow and to aid our Site’s features and functionalities.

Cookies. When you visit the Site, our web server places a cookie on your computer. Cookies are small fields that can be sued as record-keeping identifiers that we place onto your computer’s hard drive through via our Site. There are session-based cookies and persistent-based cookies. In addition, we also may permit certain third party cookies to be placed on the Site as well.

Session Cookies. Sessions cookies exist only during your online sessions with the Site. Session Cookies automatically disappear from your computer when your browser is closed or when your computer is turned off. We use session cookies to allow our systems to uniquely identify you as a user while you are using the Site. This allow us to process your online transactions and requests and also to verify your user identify, as you Move through our Site.

Persistent Cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your computer after your browser is closed and your computer is turned off. We may use persistent cookies to track aggregate and statistical information about your online activity, which may be combined with other user information as well.

Third Party Cookies. We also may engage with third parties in order to track and analyse non-personally identifiable information in the form of Site data. We use the data collected by such third parties to help us administer and improve the quality of the Site and to analyse usage statistics. We do not have access to or control over such third party cookies nor dos this Privacy Policy cover such third parties’ use of such data.

Disabling Cookies. Most web browsers will automatically accept cookies. However, if you prefer to block cookies, you may do so by selecting the appropriate cookie disabling action in your web browser settings. The Settings or Help portion of the menu bard on most browsers will provide instructions on how to prevent our computer from accepting new cookies, how to notify you via the browser when you receive a new cookie and/or how to disable cookies altogether.

Clear Gifs (aka Web Beacons and/or Web Bugs). We (or the third parties) may also engage to track and analyse Site data by employing clear gifs (e.g. Web Beacons and/or Web Bugs) to help us better manage content on the Site. Clear gifs are small graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to that of cookies, and are used to track online movements of users on the Site. As opposed to cookies, which are stored on your computer, clear gifs are invisibly embedded on the Site.
