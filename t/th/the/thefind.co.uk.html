    TheFind.com Privacy Policy

    This Privacy Policy outlines the privacy practices we follow at the web sites, applications, and services operated by TheFind, Inc., including TheFind.com, Green.TheFind.com, Local.TheFind.com, TheFindBuzz.com, Glimpse.com and the “Glimpse by TheFind” application. It also sets forth our privacy practices in connection with our blogs known as TheFindBlog (Blog.TheFind.com) and TheClick (TheClick.TheFind.com). We refer to all of these sites, services, and applications together as the "sites" in this Privacy Policy.

    How do We Collect and Use Information About You?

    Browsing and Searching: We collect certain information from you when you browse and place searches on our sites. For example, this information may include the web site that you just came from and identity of the web site you next go to, the type of browser you are using, and your Internet Protocol ("IP") address. An IP address is a number that is automatically assigned to your computer whenever you are surfing the web, allowing web servers to locate and identify your computer. Computers require IP addresses in order for users to communicate and browse on the Internet. We may also collect the date and time of your visit, the number of visits and searches you make, your server type, your operating system type, the country, state and telephone area code where your servers are located, the pages and page titles of our sites that you viewed during your visit, whether your computer is enabled for Java or cookies or other applications and plug-ins, and any search terms you enter on our sites. We use this information on an aggregated basis to determine our visitor's preferences and interests and to research our visitors' demographics and pages viewed. We will use this information to improve our marketing efforts, to analyze and track the use of our sites, to administer our sites, to improve our sites' offerings and to customize our sites' offerings, content, layout and services. We may also place Cookies on your computer when you visit our Sites. How we use Cookies is described below.

    Account Setup or Registration: If you set up an account with us, or register to use any of our applications or services, we may ask you for personally identifiable information. We may use your information to contact or identify you, as well as to administer your account or the services provided to you. The kind of personally identifiable information we may collect and store include: email address, password, name, address, telephone number, credit card information, as well as information about your use of and interests in various products and services. We may also use your email address and other personal information to send you promotional materials.

    Blogs: If you leave comments on any of our blogs, we may ask you to provide us your email address. We may use your email address to send you promotional materials and to communicate with you concerning our sites.

    Social Networking, Third Party Services, and Applications: You may engage with our sites, our content, and our services through 1) third party social networking sites such as Facebook, 2) third party social media plug-ins and applications, and 3) our own social media plug-ins and applications, such as “Glimpse by TheFind,” that may be available through our sites or through third party sites. You may also choose to link your account with us to third party social networking sites. When you use third party social networking sites, social media plug-ins or applications, we may have ongoing access to certain information from your social media profile. This information may include your photo, name, user ID, email address, birthday, gender, your location, your list of friends, people you follow, people who follow you, the posts or the 'likes' you make, content viewed, information about advertisements you are shown or clicked on, and other similar information. We may store this information about you and other users of the third party sites may be able to see this information. This information may help us personalize your and your friends’ online experience with us within and outside our applications or sites. It may also help us contact you or your friends directly or through the social networking sites with special offerings and personalized marketing information. By using any of our media plug-ins or applications, you consent to the use of such information as described. Your use of any functionality made available to you by a third party through our sites or through the third party service may result in information being collected about you the third party and we may share your information with the third party services. The third party's use of that information may be subject to its own restrictions. We make no promises as to the third party's intended uses or security. The actual information that we may obtain from the social networking sites may depend on what privacy settings selections you have made on those social networking sites.

    If you have an account with a third party service that has features available on our sites, such as Facebook, and you later terminate your account with the third party service, we may retain information about you that we obtained through your use of the third party service and we may continue to use your information to send you and your friends promotional materials or to communicate with you and your friends about our sites.

    Favorites and Recently Viewed Items: As a feature of our sites, you may have the ability to add products, stores, brands and other offerings to your "favorites" so that you can more easily retrieve them later. We will also keep track of items, stores and brands that you have recently viewed so that you can more easily view them again. We may use this information to serve relevant ads to you. If you have an account with us, we may send you promotional materials for products, stores and brands that are similar to those you have viewed in the past or added to your favorites.

    Mobile Services: If you access any of our sites from mobile devices or use any of our mobile device applications, we may collect information about your actual location through GPS or related data. We may store or use that information to provide you with location-based information and advertising or communicate with you concerning our sites or mobile applications. Our mobile products and services may allow you to interact and share personal information and data with others. If you have an account with us and log in through your mobile device or with Facebook Connect, certain information and services from our products and/or personal content you share on your mobile device may be saved by us and associated with your online account to personalize your experience online. Also, mobile devices may have a unique ID that we will collect from you and save on our servers for future use. For example, we may use the unique IDs we collect to serve relevant advertising to you and to track your usage of device applications. We may also use information we collect from your mobile device to provide information about user habits, statistics about how to improve the use of our services and to provide a better user experience. Requests may be sent through your mobile carrier's network and your carrier may have access to it. For information regarding your carrier's treatment of your information, please consult your carrier's privacy policies.

    Feedback or Contact with Our Offices: If you provide feedback to us through the sites or otherwise contact us, we may use the information you provide to us, including your name, telephone number, email address, and mailing address to communicate with you and to send you promotional materials.

    Who Collects Information About You?

    In addition to our collection of the information listed above, others may also collect your information as you use our sites:

    Advertising Companies and Content Servers: Advertisements, pictures, product descriptions, search results and other content on our sites may be served by third party ad servers or content servers. We may also use third party website analysis companies to analyze the usage of our sites. Those third parties may collect and pass along to us the same information listed that is listed under Browsing and Searching above. They may also place their own Cookies or Web Beacons on your computer to help those ads and other content to run, analyze site usage and to provide you with personalized on-line advertisements. We receive reports from third party ad servers, content servers, or web site analysis agents that tell us how many ads were presented and clicked upon or what content was served or viewed. How they use Cookies and Web Beacons is described below.

    Email Services: We may use a third party company to serve promotional emails that we send to our users. Images contained in the emails may be served by third parties. They collect certain information about you such as your IP address, browser type, your operating system, the country and server your computer is logged onto, where the servers that your computer is logged onto is located, the time and date that a particular email was viewed, the area code and zip code associated with your server, and whether you responded to a particular ad or email. They may use their own Cookies as well.

    Third Party Websites and Social Network Sites: to the extent you engage with our sites, our content, and our services through 1) third party social networking sites such as Facebook, 2) third party social media plug-ins and applications, and 3) our own social media plug-ins and applications, such as “Glimpse by TheFind,” those third party websites connected with such services may collect and use your personal information and place their own cookies on your computer.

    Who do We Share Your Information With?

Those third parties include authorized contractors, temporary employees, consultants, ad servers and content servers, email service providers and other companies working with us (collectively, "agents"). Where we believe it to be appropriate (in our sole discretion), we will ask our agents not to disclose or use your personal information for purposes other than to provide services to us or to you.

    We may share aggregated information concerning our users and their use of the sites to other merchants and advertising companies.

    We may disclose data about you to the government if we are required to do so or if we think the disclosure is appropriate; and you authorize these disclosures. We may also disclose data about you in connection with actual or potential lawsuits (a) if we are required to do so, (b) if such disclosures may potentially mitigate our liability, or (c) to enforce our rights.

    We may disclose personally identifiable information about you to companies whose practices are not covered by this Privacy Policy such as other marketers, publishers, retailers, and other organizations that want to directly market products or services to you.

    To the extent you engage with our sites, our content, and our services through social networking sites or social media plug-ins and applications, we may share personally identifiable information with them in connection with such uses.

    Cookies and Web Beacons

    When you visit the sites, we along with our ad servers, content servers, and web site analysis agents will place small text files called "cookies" on your computer hard drive that we can retrieve when you visit the sites again to make your visit more relevant and to retrieve information from prior visits. The cookies store information about the searches you have made, the items you have viewed, the favorites you have saved, your user name and other information. A cookie is a small file on your hard drive that allows us to display your search preferences and notification preferences whenever you visit our sites. When we send you promotional emails through our email services providers, they may also place a cookie on your computer. You can block cookies by changing the settings on your browser, but doing so may prevent us from delivering certain services to you in an effective manner. Cookies generally do not permit us to personally identify you.

    A Web Beacon or action tag (also known as a clear gif or a pixel tag) is a method used to track responses or actions by users who view certain advertisements or other information on our sites. Web Beacon are generally 1x1 pixel images embedded in a website page that are used to transparently collect information. Our ad servers, content servers, website analysis agents and email service providers may use Web Beacons in our sites and advertisements to count the number of times that visitors click on a particular advertisement or visit the pages of our sites and to provide information about what products are viewed or searched for or other similar actions taken. Without collecting any personally identifiable information, they use that data to tell us (and other advertisers across the internet) which ads are the most effective on particular sites or emails. They also use the information for the purpose of targeting future campaigns across the internet and to you and for upgrading visitor information used in reporting statistics. For this purpose, we and our ad servers, content servers, and web site analysis agents, and third-party e-mail distributors note some of the pages you visit on our sites through the use of Web Beacons.

    Choices Regarding Your Personal Information

    You may "opt-out," or unsubscribe from our emails or promotional materials by following any unsubscribe instructions in any e-mail, newsletter or personalized on-line advertisement you receive from us. If you open a new account or otherwise subsequently sign up to receive marketing materials, your opt-out will end unless you opt-out again by following the opt-out instructions in the promotional materials.

For example, when you click on a particular product in search results you have obtained from our sites, you will be taken to a third party site that will collect information about you. TheFind.com has no control over External Web Sites and they will independently collect and maintain information about you according to their own policies and practices. This Privacy Policy does not apply to those External Web Sites. If you have concerns about how other web sites collect and use information about you, make sure to read that site's own privacy policy and contact them.

This right is reserved whether or not the customer database is sold independently of any continuing operations of our company.

    Children

    In compliance with the Federal Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 (COPPA), the sites are not intended for anyone under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

We will post our new Privacy Policy, along with the date of its last revision, so that you will always know what information we gather, how we use that information, and whether we will disclose it to anyone.
