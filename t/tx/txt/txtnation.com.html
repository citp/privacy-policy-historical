4. Cookies
4.1 A cookie is a small file that your web browser places on your computer's hard drive to store information. Our site uses cookies to provide visitors with a personalised experience. We use two types of cookies on our site. The first kind ensures that once our users log on to the site and service, they don't have to log in a second time while navigating the site. These cookies are deleted from your hard disk at the end of each session. The second kind of cookie is set only at a user's request, and used to save the email and password one uses to log on to the site and service. It remains on your hard drive once you have closed your browser. Once the cookie is in place, we can log you on automatically by accessing this piece of information. Session-based cookies must be turned on in your browser settings to use the site and service. To save your email and password, all cookies must be turned on.

4.2 Some of our business partners may use cookies on our website (for example, advertisers). However, we have no access to or control over these cookies.
