Privacy and Personally Identifiable Information

Online privacy concerns focus primarily on the protection of "personally identifiable information." As the term suggests, "personally identifiable information" is information that, when associated with an individual, identifies that individual. The types of information that may constitute personally identifiable information include:

Registration and account information, including your name, home, e-mail and work address, screen names, telephone numbers, social security number, drivers license number, billing, service, payment and credit records, credit card or bank account information, customer correspondence, complaint and service history, and records of violations or alleged violations of the terms, conditions and policies that govern your use of the Insightbb Internet Service;

Account usage information, including the services you subscribe to, service preferences and settings you indicate through your voluntary interaction with the system, certain transactions you make through the Insightbb Internet Service, how often you access and how long you use the Insightbb Internet Service, and the number and name of additional e-mail addresses or accounts set up through the Insightbb Internet Service;

Technical information, including the types and number of devices you use to connect directly or indirectly to the system (e.g., converters, cable modems, or other equipment), the location and configuration of these devices in your residence, the delivery and routing of our service to you, and maintenance and repair records for the devices you use for the Insightbb Internet Service; and

Other information, including research records containing information obtained through voluntary customer interviews or surveys, information provided to Insight from receipt of cable or other services, publicly available information, home ownership or rental information so we can obtain any permission required prior to installing or servicing our equipment, or other information we need to render our Insightbb Internet Service to you.

It is common practice and often a necessity for companies, government, or other organizations to collect and use personally identifiable information in order to conduct business and offer services. For example, consumers signing up for an Internet access service are usually asked to provide name, address, telephone number, credit card and other information that is necessary when the consumer orders and uses the service or related products.

Collection and Use of Personally Identifiable Information

Insight will collect personally identifiable information about you or authorized users of your Insightbb Internet Service account when: (a) you voluntarily provide information to us; (b) it is necessary to provide our services to you, including to operate efficiently; or (c) to prevent unauthorized reception of our services.

We collect and use personally identifiable information for billing and customer service purposes, including to provide and enhance our service to you; anticipate, identify and resolve problems with your service; communicate with you about service issues, billing and collections, enhanced features and new product or service offerings; protect the security of our equipment and services; ensure compliance with applicable laws and the terms, conditions and policies governing the Insightbb Internet Service; and to maintain our tax and accounting records.

This means that Insight may use your personally identifiable information, in conjunction with information available from other sources, to market new services or products to you that we think will be of interest to you. If you do not want to receive Insightbb Internet Service promotional or marketing information calls, e-mail, or direct marketing other than bill stuffers, please contact us at privacy@insight-com.com.

In order to protect the security of the Insightbb Internet Service network and our services, we may operate certain network protection features to scan proper customer use of the Insightbb Internet Service. We will not collect additional personally identifiable information while utilizing such network protection features.

We may also combine personally identifiable information we have obtained in accordance with these policies with other publicly available information (such as census and household information) or information obtained from our affiliated entities or other third parties. We may use this information to create an enhanced customer database, to research and/or update our service features, or we may segment personally identifiable information to create separate customer lists or aggregate information lists. Aggregate information lists that we may collect or create do not identify individual customers.

When you access Insight Web sites, such as Insightbb.com or Insight-com.com, Insight, its affiliates and advertisers may use various kinds of software devices to collect and use information to allow participation in certain Web site activities. In addition, Insight collects information about Internet use, separate from contests or other individual site activities, in an aggregate form for review of service offerings in order to provide the most desired services to customers. This means that we may record certain "hits" to sites or links visited; however, we do not collect personally identifiable information with this aggregate data.

Insight, its partners, affiliates and advertisers may also use cookies, and/or small bits of code called "one pixel gifs" or "clear gifs" to make cookies more effective. A cookie is a small data file that contains a unique identification number that a web site places on your hard drive. Cookies simplify and improve Web navigation and personalize your Web experience by allowing a Web site server that hosts a site to recognize your browser when you visit certain web pages. Cookies also allow you to use shopping carts, check your billing history, set preferences, and participate in contests or save passwords. Cookies do not allow sites to access personal information that you have not already voluntarily provided. You can also refuse to use cookies by turning them off in your browser. Please contact your software manufacturer for questions on how to disable your cookies. Also, please review Insight's Web site privacy policies to fully inform yourself of additional Insight Web site privacy practices. See Insightbb.com and Insight-com.com.

Disclosure of Personally Identifiable Information

We disclose personally identifiable information about you to others if you provide written or electronic consent in advance or it is permitted by federal law. Specifically, federal law allows us to disclose personally identifiable information to third parties when (a) it is necessary to render, or conduct a legitimate business activity related to, our services provided to you; or (b) when disclosure is required by court order or other legal process.

Parties that may have authorized access to your personally identifiable information include our employees, billing and collection services; credit reporting agencies; installation, repair and customer assistance subcontractors or agents; sales representatives; attorneys, consultants and accountants; software vendors; service suppliers; auditors of service providers; marketers of Insightbb Internet Service products and services; Insight subsidiaries and operators; strategic partners offering or providing products or services jointly or on behalf of Insight; consumer and market research entities; merchants or other third parties from whom you make purchases or other requests; a purchasing entity if we sell or transfer ownership; and authorized representatives of government.

The frequency of disclosures to these parties varies according to business or legal needs. For example, information for billing purposes is generally provided on a monthly basis. Information for other purposes may be provided on a daily basis.

Disclosures necessary to render or conduct a legitimate business activity include, but are not limited to, disclosures to connect and utilize your Insightbb Internet Service, fulfill requested transactions, personalize your online experience, properly service and bill your account, to help research, develop and offer new and enhanced services provided by Insight, or disclosures pursuant to a transfer in ownership of all or a part of the Insightbb Internet Service.

Consistent with the requirements found in the Cable Communications Policy Act of 1984 ("Cable Act"), the Electronic Communications Privacy Act ("ECPA"), the Digital Millennium Copyright ("DMCA") and other federal laws, we may disclose personally identifiable information to representatives of government or other authorized persons. We will disclose such information pursuant to a warrant, court order, administrative subpoena, other legal process or voluntarily if we reasonably believe that an emergency involving immediate danger of death or serious injury to any person justifies disclosure of the information.

A private party may also use a subpoena under the DMCA to require our disclosure of a customer's personally identifiable information when the party believes the customer has infringed on the copyright of such party. Upon receipt of the subpoena, we are required to identify the alleged infringer, without any notice to such subscriber.

The ECPA also authorizes, and other laws may require, the disclosure of customer content or other communications made via our Insightbb Internet Service if we become aware of evidence of child pornography, we inadvertently obtain content or communications and it appears to pertain to the commission of a crime, or disclosure is to law enforcement if we believe there is an emergency involving death or serious injury that requires disclosure. Disclosure of content and communications (i.e., e-mail content) is also permitted to an addressee or intended recipient; when it is necessarily incident to providing our Insightbb Internet Service or to protect our rights or property; or to others with your consent or the consent of the addressee or intended recipient of such communications.

If federal law requires, we will provide you with notice of any disclosures identified above.

Privacy Protection for Children

Insight joins the industry in recognizing that children, including young teens, may not be able to make informed choices about personal information requested in online environments.

Insight does not collect personally identifiable information from children under thirteen years of age to connect and provide the Insightbb Internet Service. If, as a part of future service offerings, we decide to request personally identifiable information from children under thirteen, we will require verifiable parental consent before we collect this information, except in circumstances authorized by law and by the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act.

Further, Insight encourages parents and guardians to take an active role to protect the privacy and security of their children. Insight supports the development of technologies that help parents and guardians control the collection and use of personal information from children who use online services in their households.

  Use of Cookies and Tracking Devices

Insight, its parents, partners, affiliates and subsidiaries may use cookies and/or other tracking devices. These tracking devices record certain "hits" to our site or links visited. Such devices do not record or collect personal information.

A cookie is a small data file that contains a unique identification number that a website places on your hard drive. Cookies simplify and improve Web navigation and personalize your Web experience by allowing a Web site server that hosts a site to recognize your browser when you visit certain web pages. Cookies also allow you to use shopping carts, check billing history, set preferences, and participate in contests or special events or save passwords. Cookies do not allow the site to access personal information that you have not already voluntarily provided. You can refuse to use cookies by turning them off in your browser. Please contact your software manufacturer for questions on how to disable cookies. You do not need to have cookies turned on to use most of this site. However, you may find that some areas on the website will be slower, may not function at all, or you may not be able to participate in certain site activities if the cookies are disabled.

This site may also use clear gifs [Graphics Interchange Formats]. A clear gif is a tiny line of code only 1 by 1 pixel in size that is programmed to collect certain information about a visit to a particular website. We may use gifs to gather non-personal, aggregate information on visits to our website and track usage of website links.

Although we do not intend to use cookies or gifs to collect personal information, some personal information you voluntarily provide over our site may be linked to non-personal information collected through cookies or gifs. We do not disclose personal information that may be linked to our cookie and gif collection practices to third parties.

Other websites may also use cookies and clear gifs. Insight, its parents, partners, affiliates and subsidiaries are not responsible for the use of tracking devices by other website operators. Visitors must review other website operators' privacy policies to determine the type and extent of tracking device use.
