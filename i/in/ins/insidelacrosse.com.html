March 01, 2005

INSIDE LACROSSE PRIVACY POLICY

The internet is an exceptional resource for news and information. However, it may also contain personal user data, which is why we have developed the insidelacrosse.com Privacy Policy.

This policy was designed because Inside Lacrosse believes in protecting the privacy of persons visiting its web site and using its online services. By using Inside Lacrosse.com's online services, you agree to these terms.

KIDS: Be sure to ask your parents for permission before you send any information about yourself to us or anyone else over the Internet.

The policy of Inside Lacrosse is to protect and respect the privacy of our users. The information you provide through the Inside Lacrosse.com survey will help us with our goals to make the site as user-friendly and enjoyable as possible. However, we will not disclose your personal information to third parties without your consent.

No disclosure of individual user information without consent:

Inside Lacrosse.com. will not disclose to third parties information that a Customer provides about himself or herself in registering for services, responding to surveys, or purchasing goods or services, unless the Customer expressly authorizes dissemination of such individual user information.

No disclosure of individual usage patterns without consent:

Inside Lacrosse.com will not disclose to third parties information about the usage patterns, purchases, or habits of individual Customers, unless the Customer expressly authorizes dissemination of such individual user information.

Use of aggregated information and usage patterns:

Inside Lacrosse.com may aggregate information concerning its Customers and, based on such aggregate information, prepare demographic profiles of its Customers and summaries of usage patterns for advertisers, content providers, and companies that monitor use of Inside Lacrosse.com's online service.

Uses of information:

Please note that all messages, information, letters, and other comments submitted to us through this form or otherwise become the property of Inside Lacrosse. We hold the right to publish or reprint this information on the web or elsewhere. For example, an on-line ‘Letter to the Editor’ may be printed in the ‘Letters to the Editor’ section of the print version of Inside Lacrosse.

As for the user’s personally identifying information, this may also be used by Inside Lacrosse.com for editorial purposes. Inside Lacrosse.com may use such information for marketing and promotional purposes and may share the information with companies that it has pre-screened. For example, Inside Lacrosse may tell an advertiser that ‘x’ percent of its readers are in their twenties, and ‘y’ percent of its readers are in their thirties. A user's personally identifying information will not be transferred to any other party unless otherwise stated at the time of collection. Individuals always have the ability to stop their information from being used for such purposes.

This information is collected from all of our online customers. However, in our desire to respect our readers' privacy wishes, we welcome your input on how your personal information is used. While Inside Lacrosse.com will seek to require third parties with whom it has agreements to follow appropriate privacy policies, Inside Lacrosse.com does not bear any responsibility for any actions or policies of such third parties.

Insidelacrosse.com collects no personally identifying information about individuals except when specifically and knowingly provided by such individuals. In addition, il.com may place a "cookie" in the browser files of a user's computer. Cookies are pieces of information that a Web site transfers to an individual's hard drive for record-keeping purposes.

Cookies make Web-surfing easier for you by saving your preferences while you're at our site. We never save passwords or credit card information in cookies. The use of cookies is an industry standard -- you'll find them at the great majority of major Web sites. Visitors to Inside Lacrosse.com always have the option of disabling cookies via their browser preferences.

You may occasionally get cookies from our advertisers. Inside Lacrosse does not control these cookies. The use of advertising cookies sent by third-party servers is standard in the Internet industry.

No disclosure to advertisers without consent

By clicking on an advertisement appearing on our service, a Customer will not reveal his/her identity or e-mail address unless the Customer expressly gives the authority to do so.

Broadcast E-Mail

Inside Lacrosse.com may send private "broadcast e-mail" to Customers about new features and special offers, but will not disclose Customers' e-mail addresses to third parties.

U.S. Law / Storage in U.S.

Inside Lacrosse.com will process and store information about its Customers in the United States of America. In the event you are accessing Inside Lacrosse.com from outside the United States, please be aware that United States law governs Inside Lacrosse.com's activities with respect to its online services.
