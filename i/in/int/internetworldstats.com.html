1 - INFORMATION COLLECTED
While visiting the Miniwatts Marketing Group Web sites (collectively, the "Site"), or participating in a promotion or newsletter subscription sponsored by any Miniwatts Web site, we may ask you to register and/or provide information that personally identifies you ("Personal Information") for purposes of interacting with the Site. Such Personal Information may include, but is not limited to, your name, phone number, street address, e-mail address, city and country. On any page that collects Personal Information, we will specifically describe what information is required in order to provide you with the product or service, enter you in the promotion you have requested, or respond to your inquiry or comment.


IP ADDRESSES
Miniwatts records and logs IP addresses, or the location of your computer on the Internet, for systems administration and troubleshooting purposes and to determine site usage. Your IP address does not contain personal information about you.
COOKIES AND BEACONS
What are Cookies? A cookie is a small data text file that a Web site sends to your browser, which is stored on your system. The Site can only access the information from a cookie sent by the Site. We cannot access cookies sent by other Web sites or the information contained therein. Additionally, we cannot learn your e-mail address or any other information about you through the use of a cookie. The only way we would learn such information is if you specifically and voluntarily submit that information to us, for example, through a registration system, or by entering a sweepstakes or promotion. Internet
WHY DOES THE SITE USE COOKIES?
Miniwatts uses cookies to track usage of the Site and further personalize your Internet experience when you are visiting the Site. By tracking usage, we can best determine what features of the Site best serve the users.
WHAT BENEFITS DO I RECEIVE FROM COOKIES?
Overall, the use of cookies helps to give you a more personalized experience at the Site. The information is used to keep our Site fresh and content relevant to the users. Cookies also identify you to our server, allowing you to avoid retyping your e-mail address and password each time you access certain parts of the Site.
MAY I DECLINE TO ACCEPT A COOKIE?
Yes, you may decline to accept cookies sent by the Site by selecting an option on your browser to reject cookies. However, portions of the Site which require registration will not be accessible to you. In such cases, we ask that you register and provide us with basic information on our registration page, or sign in if previously registered. Cookies enable our server to know that you are a registered user, and will eliminate the need for you to log in each time you access our Site.

WHAT ARE WEB BEACONS?
Web beacons (sometimes called single-pixel or clear-pixel gifs) are invisible images that are used to write cookies or access the cookies we have written previously or to access information about the page you are viewing for the purposes of recognizing our returning users and analyzing traffic patterns. A Web beacon cannot access cookies sent by other Web sites or the information contained therein.

We use an advertising server developed by a third party to display ads on the Site. Some of these ads may contain cookies or Web beacons that are sent by third parties (e.g. advertising partners or advertising clients). We do not have access to these cookies or Web beacons or any information that the cookies contain. We encourage you to contact the advertiser or its privacy policy for more information on their data collection practices. Links from other Web sites that appear on the Site may also use cookies or Web beacons, but we do not control such activities. We also use Web beacons to write cookies, to access the cookies that we have written previously, and to access information about the page you are viewing, in order to recognize our returning users and analyze traffic patterns.
