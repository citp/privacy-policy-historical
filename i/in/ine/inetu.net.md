# Flexential Privacy Statement | People-Powered IT Solutions

**Last Updated: January 2nd, 2019**

Flexential Corp., formerly known as Peak 10, Inc., Flexential Colorado Corp., formerly known as ViaWest, Inc., and their respective subsidiaries (collectively, “Flexential”) are committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of Personal Information we may collect. This Privacy Policy describes what information we collect, how we collect it, what we do with information, how we treat the information and your choices with respect to the information we collect. By visiting our websites or otherwise providing Flexential with Personal Information by any means, you accept and consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. 

This website is not designed for, or directed to, children under the age of 13 and Flexential does not intentionally collect information about anyone under the age of 13 on this website. 

## EU-U.S. and Swiss-U.S. Privacy Shield

Flexential complies with the EU-U.S. and Swiss-U.S. Privacy Shield Frameworks (the “Privacy Shield Frameworks”) as set forth by the U.S. Department of Commerce regarding the collection, use and retention of personal information transferred from the European Union and Switzerland to the United States. This Section (EU-U.S. and Swiss-U.S. Privacy Shield) only relates to such information. Flexential has certified to the Department of Commerce that it adheres to the Privacy Shield Principles for both of the Privacy Shield Frameworks, and is subject to the investigatory and enforcement powers of the U.S. Federal Trade Commission. The specific Flexential legal entities that have made this certification include: Flexential Corp., Flexential Kentucky LLC, Flexential Tampa 3.0 LLC, Flexential Tennessee LLC, Flexential Virginia Corp., Flexential South Florida Corp., Flexential Colorado Corp., Flexential Dataside LLC, Flexential Utah Corp., Flexential SingleEdge Corp., Flexential Technology Services Corp., Applied Trust Engineering, Inc. d/b/a Flexential Professional Services and Flexential INetU Corp. If there is any conflict between the terms in this Privacy Policy and the Privacy Shield Principles for either of the Privacy Shield Frameworks, the applicable Privacy Shield Principles shall govern. To learn more about the Privacy Shield Program, and to view our certification, please visit <https://www.privacyshield.gov/>. 

Flexential’s accountability for personal data we receive under each of the Privacy Shield Frameworks and subsequently transfer to a third party is described in the applicable Privacy Shield Principles. In particular, Flexential remains responsible and liable under the applicable Privacy Shield Principles if third-party agents that we engage to process personal data on our behalf to do so in a manner inconsistent with those Principles, unless Flexential proves that we are not responsible for the event giving rise to the damage.

Flexential commits to resolve complaints about collection or use of your Personal Information. Individuals in the European Union or Switzerland with inquiries or complaints regarding our Privacy Shield policy should first contact Flexential via email at [privacy@flexential.com](mailto:privacy@flexential.com) or via mail at 8809 Lenox Pointe Drive, Suite G, Charlotte, NC 28273 Attn: Privacy & Security Officer. For any Privacy Shield complaints that cannot be resolved with Flexential directly, Flexential has committed to refer unresolved Privacy Shield complaints to the International Centre for Dispute Resolution/American Arbitration Association, an alternative dispute resolution provider located in the United States. For any Privacy Shield complaints that cannot be resolved with Flexential directly, please contact or visit the ICDR/AAA at http://go.adr.org/privacyshield.html for more information or to file a complaint. The services of the ICDR/AAA are provided at no cost to you. As further explained in the Privacy Shield Principles for each of the Privacy Shield Frameworks, a binding arbitration option will also be made available to you in order to address residual complaints not resolved by any other means.

## Definitions

_“Anonymous Information”_ means information that does not personally identify an individual, including aggregate demographic information.

_“Cookies”_ means small files that are stored by your web browser to help a particular system recognize you and the pages you visited in a website. Flexential’s Website uses cookies to store your account information between sessions and to maintain information regarding how you navigate our website. Flexential does not collect any Personal Information from cookies. Most browsers automatically accept cookies but you can set your browser to refuse cookies, though you may not be able to use all the features of this website. Flexential may use third party cookies (i.e. a cookie planted by a third party such as Google).

_“Customer”_ means an entity which receives services from Flexential pursuant to a written agreement.

_“Information”_ means, collectively, Personal Information, Anonymous Information and Sensitive Information.

_“Partner”_ means an entity which participates in the Flexential Partner Network Program pursuant to a written agreement(s).

_“Partner Network Program”_ means one or more partner programs operated by Flexential allowing Partners to, among other things: refer prospective customers to Flexential and receive a referral fee or an on-going commission fee; maintain end-user relationships on Participating Partner contracts; or manage billing functionality for Customers.

_“Personal Information”_ means information that can identify a person as a specific individual, such as name, phone number, email address, Social Security number or credit card number. Personal Information includes the information provided by Customers or Partners which may be necessary for Flexential to provide the Flexential services. Personal Information includes Personal Information provided by Partners.

_“Sensitive Information”_ means personal information that reveals a natural person’s race; ethnic origin; political, philosophical or religious beliefs; trade union membership; information specifying a person’s sex life; or that concerns a natural person’s health or medical condition.

## Google Analytics

Flexential uses a tool called “Google Analytics” to collect information about the use of this site. Here’s how it works: Google Analytics places a cookie on your computer so that it can recognize your computer if you visit our website in the future. This cookie does not collect Personal Information. Google Analytics collects information such as how often users visit this website, what pages they visit when they do so and what other sites they used prior to coming to this website. Flexential may use Google Analytics to advertise online and third party vendors, including Google, may show Flexential advertisements on websites across the Internet. These third party vendors, including Google, use first party cookies and third party cookies together to inform, optimize and serve ads based on your past visits to this website and to report how your ad impressions, other uses of ad services and interactions with these ad impressions and ad services are related to visits to this website. Flexential and Google’s ability to use and share information collected by Google Analytics about your visits to this website is restricted by the [Google Analytics Terms of Use](https://www.google.com/analytics/terms/us.html) and the [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy?hl=en&gl=us). You can prevent Google Analytics from recognizing you on return visits to this website by disabling cookies on your browser.

## Sharing Information

_1\. Personal Information._ Flexential does not disclose Personal Information to third parties except as set forth below. Flexential may share Personal Information with third parties: 

  * a. When we work with businesses, partners or agents to provide services for you on our behalf or to help process or service your request or correspond with you. For example, Personal Information may be provided to a third party to verify the validity or credit limit of your credit card.
  * b. When we work with businesses, partners or agents to develop a direct relationship with you.
  * c. In connection with the Referral Program.
  * d. As may be required by lawful requests by public authorities (i.e. through a subpoena, search warrant, other legal process), including to meet national security or law enforcement requirements.
  * e. When, in Flexential’s reasonable opinion, disclosure is required to enforce our legal rights or to protect the rights and safety of others.
  * f. In the event of a disclosure described in (a) or (b) above, Flexential ensures that the third party receiving Personal Information is contractually required to provide the same level of privacy protection as required by this Privacy Policy.



_2\. Anonymous Information._ Flexential may share Anonymous Information with third parties.

Flexential will only use Personal Information in a way that is compatible with and relevant for the purpose for which it was collected or authorized. To the extent necessary for those purposes, Flexential takes reasonable steps to ensure information is accurate, complete, current and reliable for its intended use.
