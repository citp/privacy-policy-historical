Privacy Policy

 

ICF International, Inc., ("ICF International") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information and does not sell, rent, or trade personal information provided to us through this website. "Personal Information" is information that would allow someone to identify or contact you, including, for example, your full name, address, telephone number, or email address.

By accessing this website, you consent to the collection, usage, and storage of your personal information by us in the manner described below. If you have questions regarding this Privacy Policy, please contact us. Because the web is an evolving medium, we reserve the right to amend this policy at any time by posting the amended version on our website and updating the Effective Date of the policy to reflect the date of the changes. By continuing to use this website after we post any such changes, you accept the Privacy Policy as modified.

We encourage you to learn as much as you can about your privacy on the Internet. This Privacy Policy covers the following issues and information:

  • How Personal Information is Collected and Used
  • Your California Privacy Rights
  • The Privacy and Security of Your Personal Information
  • Website Linking Policy and Disclaimer

How Personal Information is Collected and Used

Information is collected in two ways:

  1. Information You Voluntarily Provide to Us
  2. Information That is Collected Automatically

1. Information You Voluntarily Provide to Us

We directly collect Personal Information from you when you submit an online form through our website or send an email to us requesting information or assistance, such as through the submission of a contact form, signing up for a mailing list, downloading a white paper, or in the recruitment process. ICF International may also collect and store identifiable information about personal website usage via email when you voluntarily follow a link from an email to a feature on our website. We also directly collect Personal Information when you participate in a forum, such as submitting a comment on a blog. Individual data can be used to customize your view of the website in a registration-based area and to tailor information you receive from ICF.

If we collect Personal Information from you at any other time on our site, we state that Personal Information is being collected at that time and it is always a voluntary submission. Due to the open nature of the Internet, ICF International requests that no unsolicited proprietary or other confidential information be sent to it through the website. ICF International does not accept unauthorized idea submissions outside of established business relationships. Any such information supplied through the website will be deemed to be nonconfidential.

Persons who supply their email addresses or any other personal contact information online may receive direct mail, email, or telephone contact from us for assistance. If you decline to supply certain information while using the website, you may not be able to access select content or participate in some features. To ensure that you receive emails from ICF International, please add feedback@icfi.com to your address book or to your safe sender list.

ICF International will not sell, rent, or trade personal information you submit to any third party. For example, we do not sell your email address or your name and personal demographic information to mass marketers or advertisers. Personal Information is used only by ICF International, its subsidiaries, and, on some occasions, subcontractors of our firm. Our subcontractors are subject to strict contractual prohibitions on selling or sharing any Personal Information as well. Please note that as our business evolves, we may buy or sell various assets, and information about our website visitors may be among the transferred assets.

Please note that when you voluntarily make your personal information available for viewing by third parties online, such as on a blog, your information can be viewed, collected, and used by individuals and companies outside of ICF International. ICF International is not responsible for any unauthorized third party use of such information that is provided in a public forum.

2. Information That is Collected Automatically

Information is collected indirectly through the technology common to all websites. When you enter a URL or a search query, or as you navigate through the site, your browser requests a page from our server. At that time, the server automatically logs certain non-identifiable information, such as time, date, browser type, and IP address.

ICF International also uses standard “cookies” to enhance your experience on the website. Cookies are small files that your web browser places on your hard drive for record-keeping purposes and to speed up performance, for instance, by remembering you and your preferences. You may choose to turn off cookies in your web browser’s options, but it may reduce your ability to navigate the site and to access select content or participate in some features.

ICF International studies this data in our Internet access logs at both an aggregate and individual level to help us tailor content toward your interests and needs. For instance, to determine the usefulness of a particular website feature, we may analyze collective traffic to a portion of our site. This collective data contains no personally identifiable information and is used to help us operate and enhance our site and services.

If applicable, this information would include a list of the categories of personal information that was shared and the names and addresses of all third parties with which we shared information in the immediately preceding calendar year.
