A Primer on Data Regulation

The General Data Protection Regulation is an EU law, taking effect from 25th May 2018. It broadens the definition of personal data to include anything unique to an individual. It also restates the requirements to have a solid legal basis for processing personal data and being transparent to data subjects (i.e. people) about what processing is taking place.
Personal Data is therefore an EU term, including things like name, email address, but also unique cookie IDs and IP addresses

Disable Cookies

It is possible to disable cookies, but this will limit your browsing experience.
Without cookies you can't use the "My Favorite icons" function or the "Latest icons" function on IconArchive.com.
First (and easy) method: In your browser open a new "Incognito" or "Private mode" window for better privacy.
No tracking is done on your computer and only nondurable session cookies are used.
Second method: Disable Cookies completely in the Preferences of your browser.
Disabling cookies depends on your browser and opererating system, so please do a google search like this one:
"Disable cookies in Chrome on Windows" or
"Disable cookies in Safari on iPhone"
