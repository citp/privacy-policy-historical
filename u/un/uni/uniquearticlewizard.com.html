Privacy and Security

I believe very strongly in the right of all individuals to keep information about themselves to themselves.  Increasingly we seem to be giving away more and more of our privacy of our own accord!  Big brother is already watching us – but it is because we invited him to!

For example, every time you use your credit card you give away information about where you were, at what time, what shop you were in, and what you bought.  From this the credit card company, or anyone they sell or give that information to, can build up a whole profile of your lifestyle.  It may seem insignificant, but add that to all the other information that people are collecting about you and you quickly realise your life is no longer only your affair!

This may or may not be of concern to you.  In either case, I can assure you that I will not be adding to the problem.  My formal Privacy Policy (which my lawyer requires me to have) is written out below. Here are some additional points that will probably be of interest to you:

Shopping Security

All orders are processed by PayPal, one of the world’s leading e-commerce companies, using state of the art encryption, secure servers, and fraud detection.  We use them as they are able to offer much higher levels of security than we, as a small company, could possibly provide.  You can have full confidence that all information you enter, such as your credit card details are entirely safe.

Email Security

Emails that you send are like postcards – anything you write in them can be viewed by anyone who cares to look.  Obviously it takes a bit of programming know how to intercept people’s emails, but it can, and is, being done.   Of course, with billions of emails whizzing around it is unlikely that someone will pick on yours – unless you are including obvious info like credit card numbers.  But be especially careful if you are emailing from work – the chances are that you are going through their server and all your emails will be saved somewhere on the system.  They may well be read to make sure that you are working at work, instead of doing private correspondence!

Javascript

The website does use Javascript in order to make it easier to use.  These scripts do not take over your computer – all they do is to control the way the web pages are displayed to you.  Javascript can do things like reading your computer’s clock to display today’s date, and determining the type of browser you are using, and the screen size.  But it cannot read your hard drive and send me, or anyone else, information from your files.

If you are concerned about people getting access to your hard drive, the one to watch out for is “Active-X”.  In the wrong hands that can be truly malicious.  My site does not use Active-X.

Cookies

There is even more misinformation about cookies.   Cookies are simply small text files that are sent from the web server to your computer.  They carry the identity of the web server from whence they came (e.g. us) and can be read by Javascript.   This means that the Javascript can tell if you already have a cookie from us, which would indicate that you have been to this site before.  They don’t carry viruses, and they can’t run any programmes – they are just simple text files.  To see which cookies you have at present, simply do a search (Find Files) on your computer, looking for “cookies”.  Any that you find can be read with Notepad or any other text editor.

My site does use cookies to make it more user friendly, and to process shopping orders.   For more details see the Privacy Policy below.
