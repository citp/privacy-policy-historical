Privacy Policy

Last Modified: 07.01.2013

Welcome to the webdoc, Inc. (“Urturn”) web site and online services (the “Urturn Site”). Urturn is commited to protecting the privacy of our members and others who visit and use the Urturn Site (individually, “You” or collectively, “Users”). We make it a priority to protect personally identifiable information that we obtain from and about individuals both online and off-line.

Our Privacy Policy reflects our fundamental philosophy that any personal information that we collect is only for our use to improve the products and services offered to you on the Urturn Site. We won’t ask you for, collect or store personal information that is not necessary for the provision of the Urturn Services. Your personal information is stored on servers that are protected from unauthorized access by appropriate physical access restrictions and data encryption techniques. Additionally, we will not share the personally identifying information you provide with any third party, except as expressly set forth herein or unless we have your explicit consent or if we are required to do so by law or to protect our own rights.

This Privacy Policy describes in detail what information we gather from you, how we use that information, and what we do to protect it. By using the Urturn Site (whether or not you are a registered member), you expressly consent to the information handling practices described in this policy. If you do not want information about you to be used in the manner set forth in this Privacy Policy, please do not use the Urturn Site.

This Privacy Policy is incorporated into and is subject to the Urturn Terms of Service. Your use of the Urturn Site and any personal information you provide on the Urturn Site are subject to the terms of this Privacy Policy and Urturn’s Terms of Service .

The Information Urturn Collects:

  • User-provided Information: When you register as a member of the Urturn Site, you may provide to Urturn what is generally called “personally identifiable” information (such as your name, password, gender, email address, and so on). Urturn collects personally identifiable information when you register as a member in order to establish and enhance our relationship with you. Urturn uses this information to operate, maintain, enhance, and provide all of the features and services found on the Urturn Site. From time to time, Urturn may also ask you optionally to provide other information, for example to provide specific services or for security purposes. If you choose to provide such information, during registration or otherwise, you are giving Urturn the permission to use and store it consistent with this policy. Therefore, please understand that when you sign in with Urturn, you are not anonymous to us.
  • User Postings: As a user of the Urturn Site, you may submit content in the form of notes, written works, images, videos, applications or other communications (collectively, “User Postings”) for hosting, sharing, publishing and/or distribution on the Site. One of the main purposes of Urturn is to allow you to share these User Postings with others through the Urturn Site. All the content of the User Postings you submit, including any metadata associated with multi-media content such as videos or photos, will be stored on our servers in the course of providing the service and is subject to this Privacy Policy.
    “Cookies” Information: When you visit the Urturn Site, whether or not you are a registered member, we may send one or more cookies – small text files containing a string of alphanumeric characters – to your computer. Cookies remember information about your activities on a website. Urturn may use “cookies” information to: (a) remember your information so that you will not have to re-enter it during your visit or the next time you visit the Urturn Site; (b) monitor aggregate site usage metrics such as total number of visitors and pages viewed; (c) provide customized third-party advertisements, content, and information; (d) monitor the effectiveness of third-party marketing campaigns; and (e) track your entries, submissions, and status in any promotions or other activities. Urturn may use both session cookies and persistent cookies. A session cookie disappears after you close your browser. A persistent cookie remains after you close your browser and may be used by your browser on subsequent visits to the Urturn Site. Persistent cookies can be removed. Please review your web browser “Help” file to learn the proper way to modify your cookie settings. However, without cookies you may not have access to certain services on the Urturn Site. Third party advertisements displayed in connection with the Urturn Site may also contain cookies set by internet advertisers. We do not control these cookies and users of the Urturn Site should check the privacy policy of the advertiser to see whether and how it uses cookies.
  • Information from Other Sources & Friends’ Information: We may also obtain information, including personally identifiable information, from third parties and sources other than the Urturn Site. If we combine or associate information from other sources with personally identifiable information that we collect through the Urturn Site, we will treat the combined information as personally identifiable information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. In some cases, you may be able to opt out of combining this information. Urturn also provides tools for importing your friends’ addresses so that you can invite them to or share content with them on Urturn. Urturn uses your friends’ addresses only for the purpose of sending the invitations and does not otherwise store or process them in any way. If you are asked to provide a password from another service to access your friends’ addresses, we will not store the password once your friends’ information has been retrieved.

Sharing Information on Urturn:

Urturn allows you to control who can see each of your User Postings.

  • Personal information: One of the primary purposes of Urturn is to share content with your friends. So that your friends can connect with you and identify you as the source of your content, a user profile is associated with your account. Information that is part of this profile such as your username, profile picture, website, twitter handle, followers, and people following you are always visible. Other personal information associated with your profile or account such as your email address or accounts on other services will not be visible except as authorized by you in your profile privacy settings.
  • Your User Postings: When you submit content to Urturn in the form of User Postings, you control access to it through your privacy and sharing settings. User Postings may be private (visible only to you), shared with a subset of other Urturn users or published to everyone. When you share a User Posting with everyone, this means that anyone, including people outside the Urturn Site, may access your User Posting and associate it with your username and any information about your Urturn profile that you have made public.
  • Your comments and contributions: When you submit a User Posting, you may control how other Users are allowed to interact with it. You may allow them to contribute to your User Posting by adding their own rich content (notes, images, videos, audio, applications, and so on) or you may limit their interaction to text-based comments and votes, ratings or other interactions via the embedded applications provided by Urturn. In any case, any contributions they make to your User Postings will be subject to the sharing settings you select for those Postings; other Users may not set the sharing settings for their individual contributions. Likewise, when you contribute to or comment on the User Postings of others, your contributions are subject to the sharing settings they have selected or may select for those User Postings.
  • Information Shared with Everyone: Information, data and content that is shared with the “Everyone” setting, such as your profile information and User Postings and the User Postings of others to which you have contributed, is considered public information. This means that it may be visible to anyone including people who are not logged into or using the Urturn Site. Such information may be indexed by third-party search engines and imported, exported, distributed or redistributed by Urturn or by others without restrictions concerning privacy. Urturn has no control over information shared under the “Everyone” setting once it leaves the Urturn Site. If you do not want your content to be available outside Urturn, please adjust your privacy settings appropriately.
