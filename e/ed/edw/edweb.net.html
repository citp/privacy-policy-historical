Information We Collect
When you visit the edWeb we collect two types of information: personal information you choose to disclose when you register or update your user profile, and information collected by us as you interact with the Site.
User-Provided Information
We collect personal information from you when you register with our Site, update your profile, and use various features of our Site. When you register with the Site, you provide your full name, your email address, your job function, title and status, the institution where you work, and the city, state/province, zip/postal code, and country of your institution. We do not ask for any personal information such as your home address, phone number or any other information about you. When you complete our registration form you will be able to access the Site. Once you are registered, you have the option to add to your profile a personal photo and additional professional information including a brief biography, your areas of interest, and associations with whom you maintain affiliations. If you are interested in connecting with other members of the site for networking purposes, you also have the opportunity to select those areas from a checklist. We may also collect information regarding your interaction with other Users and your use of the Site. From time to time we may also collect information about you through other methods, including information we obtain from third parties, including verification services, data compilers, as well as public sources. We use this information to continually offer you the most value as an edWeb member.

Each member of the edWeb has a personal home page and associated sub-pages connected to the personal home page. The personal home page displays the profile information described above, as well as any Content that you have uploaded or added to your personal home page. Your personal home page does not display your email address or the networking interests you may have selected in your profile. Your email address is not displayed anywhere publicly on the site, unless you choose to disclose it in any Content you post. Your networking interests are not displayed anywhere on the Site, either. These interests are used in our search functionality to enable like-minded members interested in similar topics to meet.

By using the permission settings on your profile, you can control which members of the Site have access to your personal profile data. By default, these permission settings are set to show your profile only to members of the Site who are in your "My Contacts & Friends" list. You can change these settings at any time by going to the "My Settings" link in your personal navigation bar. You can set your profile to be seen by all members of the Site, only linked "Contacts & Friends," or even no one.

Please note: the Site is a professional site and does not use screen names or nicknames to hide a member's identity. While you have control over who can access your profile and other Content on your home page, your full name and profile picture, if you have uploaded a photo, cannot be hidden and will always be viewable by all members of the Site when they visit your personal home page, when they access the Site's list of members, and when you add Content, such as a discussion or blog post, to a public community or the edWeb Forums.

Your name and profile picture also will be available to all members of the Site when they use the Site's search functionality. This is primarily so your existing contacts and friends can find you and send a request to become a linked connection of yours on the Site. Members of the Site can also use the search function to find other members who share similar networking interests. However, members who see your name in search results will not be able to read your profile information unless they have a relationship with you (i.e. they are a linked Contact & Friend) or your privacy settings allow all members of the edWeb to access your profile.

As an edWeb Member, you can share personal Content, such as a blog, files you have uploaded and multimedia such as photos. Please be aware that by using any of these features, your personally identifiable information may be accessible to other members of the edWeb, and we have no control over how other members may use your personally identifiable information.

Profile information you submit to the Site and Content you post to your personal home page and sub-pages will be available to other members of the Site you allow to access the information through your privacy settings. You may set your privacy settings to allow all members of the edWeb to access your profile, only linked Contacts & Friends, or no one. These permissions are set within each of the tools provided on the personal home page. To protect your privacy, we recommend that you choose your profile and Content viewing preferences with care. We also recommend that you avoid posting personal or private information in discussions, on forums or blogs, or in any files you choose to upload and share with other members, as any information you provide may be viewable by other members of the Site and authorized edWeb staff.

Cookies and Other Technologies We Use
When we track activity on the edWeb, we collect information such as your IP address, browser type and version, and pages you view. We receive an IP address each time you view the Site, because our server automatically reports it to us. We may collect your connection information including details of IP addresses, operating systems, browsers, versions, and the website that you visited prior to the Site (but not e-mail addresses) as part of our analysis of the Site. We would use this information in our business for various purposes, including diagnosing service or technology problems, assessing and maintaining system security, and/or displaying the most appropriate advertising or content. We do not track any URLs that you type into your browser that are not URLs associated with the Site and we do not track you across the Internet once you leave our Site. We gather this information for all edWeb members. By accessing the Site, you unconditionally consent to allow us to collect your connection information and use that information in accordance with this Privacy Policy for any purpose not prohibited by applicable law.

We use "session cookies" to manage your experience on our Site. A session cookie is a small piece of information we ask your browser to send to us along with each request you make to view a page on our Site. We use this information so that you can best take advantage of the features of the Site during your visit. Your browser will no longer send session cookies to us either when you close your browser or when you log out. The next time you visit us, you will be given new session cookies. This is what differentiates session cookies from permanent cookies; permanent cookies are stored on your computer, and your browser will be able to send the same information during your visit as was sent during earlier visits. We do not use permanent cookies.

Most browsers are set up to accept cookies, but you can configure yours to refuse cookies or notify you when you've received one. Browsers differ, so check your "Help" menu to learn how to change your cookie preferences. However, if you reject cookies on the Site, you may not be able to access all areas of the Site or use some of its features.

When we send you emails, we may use a variety of mechanisms including the use of embedded images to determine the number of members who open our emails. These images would also allow us to count users who have visited the Site and to deliver co-branded services. When you click on a link in an email, we may record this individual response to allow us to customize our offerings to you. Embedded images may also be used to conduct research on behalf of some partners on their websites for auditing purposes and/or to determine aggregate information about users, including demographic and usage information. Embedded images are not used to access your personally identifiable information, but they are a technique we may use to compile aggregated statistics about usage of the Site. Embedded images collect only a limited set of information including a cookie number, time and date of a page view, originating IP address, and a description of the page on which the embedded images resides. If you find our Site using a search engine, the search engine and the search term used may be transmitted back to our ad-serving service providers via a cookie. This information is anonymous and does not contain your name, address, telephone number, or e-mail address. This information is used only in the aggregate to evaluate how users reach our site.

In addition, some of our advertisers use third-party networks to serve their advertisements on the Site. Often, these third-party advertising companies use cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to measure advertisement effectiveness. We do not control, nor do we have access to, their cookies or the information they obtain. Use of their tracking technology is subject to their own privacy policies.

Members of the edWeb should be aware that it is possible for a community that is marked as private or hidden to become public if the administrator of the community changes the community's access policy.

The edWeb Forums are public forums available to all members of the edWeb. Unlike your personal home page or a private or hidden community you may belong to, the Forums are a public space on the Site. Your name, photo, if you've uploaded one, and any posts you make to these Forums are accessible to all members of the edWeb.
