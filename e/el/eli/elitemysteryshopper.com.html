How We Collect and Use Information:  Cookies, Clear gifs, and Other Means
Cookies are small pieces of information stored on your hard drive which help us recognize you as a user or Member and assist you in navigating the Site as easily as possible.  We use cookies to recognize users and collect information about how our Site is used. Information gathered from cookies may include the date and time of visits, the Site pages viewed, time spent at our Site and the websites that you visited just before and just after your visit to our Site. If you do not wish to transmit "cookie" information about yourself, you may turn off the cookie function in your web browser. Please consult the "Help" section of your web browser in order to correctly turn off or disable the cookie functions. However, turning off or disabling the cookie functions on your web browser may prevent our system from recognizing you when you visit our Site, and consequently may make utilization of the Site more difficult, or even impossible.

We may also use "clear gifs" (which also have other names, such as "pixel tags") and/or JavaScript plug-ins, on our website and in our emails to you. These are small graphic images or code which are used for such things as recording web pages and links clicked-on by a user, or for tracking the performance of email marketing campaigns. These devices help us measure the effectiveness of our Site and our marketing. We may work with third-party service providers that help us track, collect, and analyze this information.  Third-party entities with whom we have agreements may also place these devices on the Site and/or in emails to use information obtained from them such as pages viewed, items purchased, emails opened and items upon which you may click in emails.

A "widget" is generally an application that can be embedded in a web page.  Widgets can provide real-time information to the page.  Widgets are often provided by third-parties and we may provide widgets on our Site.  Widgets may enable the third-parties that provide them to collect data about users visiting this Site.  Also by clicking on certain widgets, some of your information may be shared with the third-party placing the widget.  We may also obtain information shared with us through the providers of these widgets. 

Our Site's servers also automatically identify your computer by its IP address, which is a unique string of numbers that is assigned to your computer by your Internet service provider. IP addresses are automatically logged by web servers, collecting information about a user's traffic patterns. While the IP address does not identify an individual by name, it may, with the cooperation of the ISP, be used to locate and identify an individual using the Internet. Your IP address can also reveal what geographic area you are connecting from, or which ISP you are using. Your IP address may be used to address problems with our server, administer our Site and/or to gather broad demographic information about our users and Members. Finally, the other websites that you visit have IP addresses, and we may collect the IP addresses of those websites and their pages.
