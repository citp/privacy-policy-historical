Privacy

USA (EN) (ES) | Canada (EN) (FR) (ES) | Europe (EN) (DE) (ES)

Experient Inc. ("Experient") is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Maritz Holdings Inc., its subsidiaries and divisions (including Maritz Global Events), is committed to building user trust and confidence by promoting, and complying with, business practices that help protect the privacy of our customer and user data. This policy provides our commitment to your privacy by describing our information, practices and policies to ensure compliance with the Privacy Shield privacy framework, as defined by the U.S. Department of Commerce in cooperation with Switzerland and the European Commission.  The policy applies to your usage of our online websites, mobile applications and our transfer of personally identifiable information.

The Privacy Shield program helps assure Swiss and other European members that adequate privacy protections are followed by participants to allow the transfer of personal data to non-European Union (EU) countries otherwise not meeting the Swiss and EU “adequacy” standards for privacy protection.

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has jurisdiction over Maritz' compliance with the Privacy Shield.

Maritz has met the requisite seven privacy principles to participate in the Privacy Shield program. For more information about the Privacy Shield program and its benefits visit https://www.privacyshield.gov/welcome.

A list of the seven principles and how Experient meets these requirements follow:

 

NOTICE AND CHOICES

Children

Experient is a family-friendly company and does not publish information that is not suitable for children or minors. Examples of pornography, offensive language, graphic pictures, or violence are not published on any Experient site. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware of our receipt of personal information of a child under 13 we will take steps to promptly remove such information.

Information Collection & Use

Experient will only collect your personal information (such as your name, email, address or phone number) if you provide it voluntarily. The information collected through this website is used by Experient for marketing and/or to contact the visitor to resolve questions and/or concerns.

 

ONWARD TRANSFER

Information Sharing and Disclosure

Experient reserves the right to share your information when required by subpoena, court order, applicable law, government statute, regulation or other legal process. Additionally, we may share your information when we have a good faith belief that the disclosure is necessary to prevent or respond to fraud, defend our websites, mobile applications or other services against attacks, or protect our property and security, or our customers and users. At times, we may append your contact information by purchasing updated address, phone and other corporate information from national list companies. We do this to help ensure relevancy and accuracy in our marketing, promotion and sales efforts. Customers that use Experient services to collect personal information must agree to abide by the same principles followed by us. Additional information describing the procedures and policies that we follow to protect your data can be requested through email at privacy@maritz.com

Legal Disclaimer

We reserve the right to disclose personal information as required by law and when we believe that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights and/or comply with a judicial proceeding, court order, or legal process served on our site.

Maritz is liable for appropriate onward transfers of personal information to third parties.
 

ACCESS AND DATA INTEGRITY

Your Account

Access Usernames and passwords are stored in our databases. All passwords are encrypted. Account information is kept secure and used solely by you to access your client account, and to facilitate communication. Company administrators have permission to make corrections or changes to their account information as needed. User information is stored only when a user voluntarily gives this information for use by a client company. No information is released outside of the Experient unless with the authority of the user or as required under applicable law.

SECURITY

Features

Experient has implemented a number of security features to help prevent the unauthorized release of or access to personal information. Although the company strives to create and maintain secure and reliable site, mobile applications and other services for you, the confidentiality of any communication or material transmitted to or from Experient via the website, mobile applications or email system cannot be guaranteed.

Cookies

A cookie is a small string of text that a website can send to your browser to assist in distinguishing your browser from a previous visitor by saving and remembering any preferences that may have been set while browsing the particular site. Currently, Experient uses cookies only for session-state management and has no current plan to use cookies for any other purpose. We will revise this policy accordingly if we elect to begin using cookie technology for any other purpose, so you may wish to check this policy periodically for updates.

 

ENFORCEMENT

Opt Out

You may unsubscribe or opt out of receiving electronic communications from us at any time. We contact customers, prospects, and survey respondents with different types of communication: operational, marketing, and research. To opt out of our marketing communication, send your request to privacy@maritz.com. Clicking on an unsubscribe link in the email invitation will take you to a web page where you can complete the opt-out process. Certain programs will provide a contact for support or other means to remove your name from our contact list. Please allow up to 10 business days of our receipt of your request for the unsubscribe or opt out process to take effect.

Dispute Resolution

In compliance with the US-EU Privacy Shield Principles, Maritz commits to resolve complaints about your privacy and our collection or use of your personal information.  EU individuals with questions or concerns about our use of personal information or our privacy policy should contact us at privacy@maritz.com.

If your question or concern is not satisfied through this process Maritz has further committed to refer unresolved Privacy Shield complaints to JAMS, an independent dispute resolution provider located in the United States.  Information about how to file a complaint with JAMS related to our Privacy Shield program can be found at https://www.jamsadr.com/file-an-eu-us-privacy-shield-or-safe-harbor-claim.  The services of JAMS are provided at no cost to you.  Under certain circumstances, EU individuals may seek redress from the Privacy Shield Panel, a binding arbitration mechanism.

Maritz commits to cooperate with EU data protection authorities (DPAs) and comply with the advice given by such authorities with regard to human resources data transferred from the EU in the context of the employment relationship.
