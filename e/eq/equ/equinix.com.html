EQUINIX INTERNET EXCHANGE

Networks and content providers can connect directly with each other to peer their traffic in order to improve performance and reduce cost. As the leading global network-neutral data center provider, Equinix offers network service providers, content & digital media properties, enterprises, financial firms and cloud/SaaS platforms the ability to connect with over 650 networks worldwide. The Equinix Internet Exchange peering platform combines secure colocation services with a network-rich interconnection ecosystem and the largest aggregation of networks in the world.

Across North America, Asia-Pacific and a number of European locations, the Equinix Internet Exchange provides a reliable switching platform for peering and interconnection. Internet Exchange is a cost-effective service designed to reduce operating costs and enable efficient IP traffic interchange by aggregating thousands of peering sessions onto a single hardware platform at an IBX data center.

WHY PEER?

  • Improved performance: Direct interconnection to more networks allow for more direct paths and fewer hops which means bypassing potential bottlenecks
  • Reduced cost: Instead of paying transit costs for every packet of data to an Internet destination, one can bypass that cost by peering with networks that reach that destination
  • Increased capacity: Large amounts of traffic can be passed by distributing traffic across many networks instead of going through one network
  • Increased control: You can reduce dependence on one or more transit providers for delivering your traffic

A cookie file can contain information such as a user ID that the site uses to track the pages you've visited, but the only personal information a cookie can contain is information you supply yourself. A cookie can't read data off your hard disk or read cookie files created by other sites. Some parts of Equinix’s Web site use cookies to track user traffic patterns. We do this in order to determine the usefulness of Equinix’s information to our users and to see how effective our navigational structure is in helping users reach that information. Equinix does not correlate this information with data about individual users, nor does it share this information or sell it to any third party

You may set most browsers to notify you if you receive a cookie or you may choose to block cookies with your browser, but please note that if you choose to erase or block your cookies, you will need to re-enter your original user ID and password to gain access to certain parts of the Web site.

Tracking technologies may record information such as Internet domain and host names; Internet protocol (IP) addresses; browser software and operating system types; clickstream patterns; and dates and times that our site is accessed. Our use of cookies and other tracking technologies allows us to improve our Web site and your Web experience.
