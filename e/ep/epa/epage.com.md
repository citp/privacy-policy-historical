Your Email Address and Communication With You    
We do not rent, sell, or otherwise disclose your email address to third parties. We do not Spam (send advertising emails or solo ads) our users. Your email address is required for many parts of our service. It is not required to browse the site and view ads. An email address is required to place a classified ad or auction, reply to an ad or auction, use the feedback system, sign up for a SearchAlert, or participate in our Affiliate program. 

We send a monthly email to all users. This monthly email will contain updates, news, and special offers, as well as information about your account. This email may contain advertising or offers from third parties and this will be indicated as such. If you do not want to receive this monthly email, you can opt-out from a link in the email as well as from your account page. 

We send a weekly email with the status and performance of your ads to all users who have active ads listed on the Web site. If you do not want to receive this weekly email, you can opt-out from the summary area of your Members page.

We send SearchAlert emails to users up to once per day to those who have signed up for a SearchAlert when there is a match to the search criteria that they create. If you do not want to receive this email, you can delete your SearchAlert from the SearchAlert area of your Members page.

We send you email on a user's behalf when they reply to your ad. We strive to intercept unwanted replies to ads, but we take no responsibility for the content of any reply you get. Your contact information, including email address is not disclosed to browsers unless you expressly enter it into the ad for the public to see. We have a procedure for you to report unwanted replies to us as part of the email. Do not report any email we send you to authorities as Spam or your account priviledges may be terminated. 

We send regular account reminders to all users who have outstanding balances. If you do not want to recieve these notices, you can pay the balance due via PayPal, Credit Card, or a check or money order in US funds sent to our place of business. This information is available in the account area of your Members page. 

We send a weekly email to all affiliates with a short newsletter and statistics about their affiliate account. Affiliates can opt out of this email from the Affiliate Manager area.

We will send email to you for other legitimate purposes as part of the site operation. You will get email confirmation when you register, place an ad, reply to an ad, make a payment, use the feedback system, and for other operational reasons.

Our Use of Cookies  
Cookies are very tiny text files that are stored on your computer when you visit certain web pages that record your preferences. They cannot harm your computer and they do not contain any personal or private information. 

If you take out an ad on EPage Network Classifeds or become an affiliate we use temporary cookies to help format the page, and indicate what ads you can view. We also place permanent cookies to enable some of the advanced benefits, such as viewing all ads, for Gold members. In the course of serving advertisements to this site, our third-party advertiser may place or recognize a unique "cookie" on your browser. 

Most cookies expire after a certain time period. The most popular browsers, such as Netscape Navigator and Internet Explorer, can be set to give you the option of accepting or rejecting all cookies. 

To use many of our services, your browser will need to be configured to accept cookies. Here's how to do it: In Netscape Navigator, click the Edit menu and select Preferences from the browser's drop-down menu. In the tree structure, click Advanced (Netscape 4) or Privacy & Security (Netscape 7). Under Cookies, you can slect the option you feel most comfortable with that enables cookies. In Internet Explorer, click the View (older versions of Explorer) or Tools (current version) menu and select Internet Options from the drop-down menu. Click the Advanced tab, scroll to the Security heading, and select the option you feel most comfortable with that enables cookies. 

If you wish, you may set your browser to reject all cookies and still view the site. Because our system requires cookies for many processes, you may find that you are unable to place an ad or perform other tasks.

Credit Card Security  
The Internet is a vast network of computers. Because the data passes from computer to computer, some Internet shoppers worry that unauthorized persons may intercept the data and gain access to credit card numbers, but shopping on a secure site is actually safer than handing a credit card to a waiter in a restaurant. Taking the steps below will lower the chances of interception of your credit card number: 

  * Limit your online shopping to Internet merchants, such as us, with sites equipped with Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) technology. SSL prevents unauthorized people from intercepting your data. 
  * Use a browser that supports SSL. These browsers show a symbol in the status bar, such as a key or lock, when you are on a site that uses SSL. Most banks will cover the cost of fraud resulting from unauthorized use of your credit card or may limit your liability. 



Credit card information collected is encrypted through Secure Sockets Layer before it is transmitted over the Internet.
