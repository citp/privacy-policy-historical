Singular Summary and Aggregate Statistical Information

e-Miles' Use of Cookies and Web Beacons
A cookie is simply a small text file containing information about the user that is placed by a website on the user's hard drive. e-Miles places a cookie on your computer when you complete the registration form or log into the e-Miles website. e-Miles uses the cookie to recognize you when you visit our website so that we may provide you with customized pages based on information in your Member account or profile. The cookie is also required to log in and view Member-specific information on the website. We may also use cookies to track certain information about you based upon your activity on our site or on a third party's sites. This information may include the URL that you just came from (whether this URL is on our site or not), which URL you next go to (whether this URL is on our site or not), what browser you are using and your IP address. We may use this information to conduct internal research on our Members' demographics, interests and behavior to better understand and serve our Members. We reserve the right to share this information in summary and aggregate form with third parties.

The use of cookies by our partners and service providers is not covered by our privacy policy. We do not have access or control over these cookies. Our partners and service providers use session ID and persistent cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our site and in order to serve you with offers based upon your interests and browsing history. Web Beacons/Web Bugs

We employ and our third party advertising partner employs a software technology called Web Beacons (a.k.a. clear gifs, pixel tags, Web bugs) that help us better manage content on our site by informing us what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of Web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user's computer hard drive, Web beacons are embedded invisibly on Web pages and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence. We normally do not tie the information gathered by Web beacons to our customers' personally identifiable information. Sometimes, when a member takes an action on our site or an advertising partner's site, a web beacon will inform e-Miles of such action so appropriate miles can be awarded.

We use Web beacons in our HTML-based emails to let us know which emails have been opened by recipients. This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. If you would like to opt-out of these emails, please see "Choice/Opt Out".

We partner with a third party ad network to either display advertising on our Web site or to manage our advertising on other sites. Our ad network partner uses cookies and Web beacons to collect non-personally identifiable information about your activities on this and other Web sites to provide you targeted advertising based upon your interests. If you wish to not have this information used for the purpose of serving you targeted ads, you may opt-out by clicking here: Ad Choices. Please note this does not opt you out of being served advertising. You will continue to receive generic ads.
